  2. Go to Section 2 and fill out your 90-Day Calendar start date -- pick a Monday
  3. Go to Section 4 (Skincare Routine Builder) and build your AM and PM routines
  4. Tomorrow morning, use the Daily Tracker on page Section 3 for your first day
  5. Do your Weekly Check-In every Sunday -- it takes 10 minutes and keeps you honest

That is it. Do not try to fill out the entire planner right now. Start with Day 1 and build from there. This is a marathon, not a sprint.

How This Planner Works

This is not a "drink more water" checklist you will abandon by Week 2. This is a structured 90-day transformation system that covers the five pillars of a real glow-up: skin, body, mind, style, and self-care. Each week builds on the last. Each section works together.

The rules:

  • Progress over perfection. A 70% day is still a glow-up day. You do not need to hit every single checkbox.
  • Consistency beats intensity. Doing your skincare routine daily matters more than a single 2-hour spa night.
  • You are allowed to adjust. If something is not working by Week 3, change it. This planner is a tool, not a prison.
  • Rest is productive. Recovery days are built into this system on purpose. Use them.

Before you start: Take your Day 1 photos (front, side, no makeup, natural lighting). You will not want to later, and you will be so glad you did at Day 90. Store them somewhere private -- they are just for you.

Section 4: Skincare Routine Builder

Use this section to design your personal AM and PM routines. Fill in specific products as you find what works for your skin.

Know Your Skin Type First

Check the one that fits best:

  • Oily -- Shiny by midday, visible pores, prone to blackheads. Use: gel cleansers, lightweight moisturizers, niacinamide, salicylic acid.
  • Dry -- Tight, flaky, sometimes rough. Use: cream cleansers, rich moisturizers, hyaluronic acid, ceramides.
  • Combination -- Oily T-zone, dry cheeks. Use: gentle cleansers, balanced moisturizers, zone-treat as needed.
  • Sensitive -- Reacts easily, redness, stinging. Use: fragrance-free everything, minimal ingredients, centella/aloe-based.
  • Normal -- Balanced, few concerns. Use: whatever feels good, focus on prevention and SPF.

The Golden Rule of Layering

Thinnest to thickest. Always apply products from the lightest/most watery consistency to the heaviest/most occlusive. This ensures each layer absorbs properly and nothing gets blocked.

AM Routine Builder

Morning is about protection and glow. The goal: clean, hydrated, protected skin before you face the day.

Step Product Category Why My Product
1 Gentle Cleanser (or water rinse) Removes overnight oils without stripping. Dry/sensitive skin can skip and just rinse.  
2 Toner / Essence Balances pH, adds first layer of hydration. Look for hydrating, not astringent.  
3 Vitamin C Serum Antioxidant protection, brightens, fights dark spots and sun damage. Use AM only.  
4 Eye Cream Delicate eye area needs its own lightweight formula. Pat gently with ring finger.  
5 Moisturizer Seals in all previous layers, strengthens moisture barrier.  
6 SPF 30+ (Broad Spectrum) THE most important anti-aging step. Apply generously. Reapply every 2 hours in sun.  
7 Lip Balm with SPF Lips burn and age too. Protect them.  

PM Routine Builder

Evening is about repair and treatment. This is when active ingredients do their best work while your skin regenerates overnight.

Step Product Category Why My Product
1 Oil Cleanser / Micellar Water First cleanse dissolves makeup, SPF, and oil-based grime. Essential even without makeup.  
2 Water-Based Cleanser Second cleanse removes remaining residue. Ensures truly clean skin for treatments.  
3 Exfoliant (2-3x per week only) AHA (glycolic, lactic) for dullness/texture. BHA (salicylic) for pores/acne. NOT daily.  
4 Toner / Essence Preps skin to absorb treatments. Adds hydration layer.  
5 Treatment Serum Retinol (anti-aging), niacinamide (pores/redness), or targeted concern serum.  
6 Eye Cream Richer formula okay at night. Target dark circles, fine lines, or puffiness.  
7 Moisturizer / Night Cream Richer than AM moisturizer. Locks in all treatments. Supports overnight repair.  
8 Facial Oil (optional) Goes LAST (over moisturizer). Seals everything in. Great for dry/dehydrated skin.  
9 Lip Treatment Overnight lip mask or heavy balm. Heals and plumps while you sleep.  

Ingredient Combinations to AVOID

  • Retinol + AHA/BHA -- Do not use on the same night. Alternate nights to avoid irritation.
  • Retinol + Vitamin C -- Can irritate together. Use vitamin C in AM, retinol in PM.
  • AHA/BHA + Vitamin C -- Too much acid at once. Separate by AM/PM or alternate days.
  • Niacinamide + Vitamin C -- Older advice said to avoid; newer research says it is fine together, but if you get flushing, separate them.
  • Benzoyl Peroxide + Retinol -- They can deactivate each other. Use at different times.

New to retinol? Start with 0.25% or 0.3% concentration, 2 nights per week. Increase to every other night after 4 weeks if no irritation. Always use SPF the next morning. Your skin will purge for 2-6 weeks -- this is normal. Push through unless you have actual allergic reaction symptoms (hives, swelling, pain).

Section 6: Mindset & Self-Care

A glow-up that is only external will not last. This section builds the internal foundation that makes everything else sustainable.

Daily Affirmations

Pick one each morning. Say it out loud or write it down. Yes, it feels strange at first. Do it anyway.

Week 1-4 Affirmations (Foundation)

  • I am worthy of the time and effort I invest in myself.
  • I do not need to be perfect to be making progress.
  • My body is capable of more than I give it credit for.
  • I am building habits that my future self will thank me for.
  • Taking care of myself is not selfish -- it is necessary.
  • I am allowed to start over as many times as I need.
  • Small steps still move me forward.

Week 5-8 Affirmations (Elevation)

  • I am becoming the person I have always wanted to be.
  • I release the version of myself that played small.
  • I am strong, I am disciplined, and I am consistent.
  • My confidence comes from showing up, not from being flawless.
  • I trust the process even when I cannot see the results yet.
  • I do not compare my Day 30 to someone else's Day 300.
  • I am proud of the work I have put in so far.

Week 9-13 Affirmations (Transformation)

  • I have proven to myself that I can commit and follow through.
  • I am not the same person who started this 90 days ago.
  • I glow because I chose myself, consistently, even on hard days.
  • I set the standard for how I am treated by how I treat myself.
  • My transformation is not just physical -- it is who I am becoming.
  • I am magnetic because I am aligned with my values.
  • I do not need external validation. I validate myself.

Journaling Prompts

Use one prompt per day or per week. Write for at least 5 minutes without stopping. No editing, no judgment.

Self-Discovery Prompts

  1. What does my ideal day look like in 6 months? Describe it in detail.
  2. What am I tolerating in my life right now that I should not be?
  3. If I had zero fear of judgment, what would I change about my appearance or style?
  4. What is one compliment I struggle to accept? Why?
  5. Who do I admire and what specific qualities do they have that I want to develop?

Growth Prompts

  1. What is a belief about myself that I have outgrown?
  2. What would I attempt if I knew I could not fail?
  3. Where am I being too hard on myself? Where am I not being hard enough?
  4. What is the hardest boundary I need to set right now, and with whom?
  5. Write a letter to the version of yourself 90 days from now. What do you want her to know?

Reflection Prompts

  1. What has surprised me most about this process so far?
  2. What have I learned about my own discipline and consistency?
  3. When did I feel most confident this week? What was I doing?
  4. What is one thing I would tell my younger self about self-care?
  5. How has my relationship with my body or appearance changed since I started?

Exercise 2: Boundary Scripts

Practice saying these out loud until they feel natural:

  • "I appreciate you thinking of me, but I cannot commit to that right now."
  • "I need some time to myself this evening. Let's connect tomorrow."
  • "That does not work for me, but thank you for understanding."
  • "I am focusing on my health right now, so I am going to pass."
  • "I love you, and I also need you to respect this boundary."

Exercise 3: The Social Media Reset

  • Unfollow 10 accounts that make you feel bad about yourself
  • Follow 10 accounts that inspire, educate, or genuinely make you smile
  • Set a daily screen time limit for social media (try 60 minutes)
  • No phone for the first 30 minutes after waking and last 30 minutes before bed

If you answered "no" to 2+ questions, it goes in the donate/sell pile.
