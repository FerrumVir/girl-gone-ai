Section 1: Quick Start Guide

START HERE -- 5 Minutes to Set Up

  1. Duplicate this template into your Notion workspace (or recreate in your preferred tool)
  2. Customize the Client Intake Form with your specific services and branding
  3. Set up the Client Profile Database -- add your existing clients first
  4. Review the Treatment Protocol Library and adjust pricing to your market

That's your foundation. Add the revenue dashboard and inventory tracker as you get comfortable. Don't try to build everything at once.

How This System Works

This is a complete business-in-a-box for estheticians -- whether you're solo, renting a suite, or building a team. Every section is designed to work independently or together. Use what you need now, grow into the rest.

The core loop:

  • Intake -- New client fills out the intake form (digitally or on paper)
  • Profile -- You create their client profile in the database
  • Treat -- You pull the treatment protocol, customize for their skin, and document everything
  • Follow up -- Automated reminders for aftercare, rebooking, and retention
  • Track -- Revenue, inventory, and growth metrics update as you work

Pro tip: Print the Client Intake Form and Treatment Protocols for your treatment room. Keep the digital versions for your database and tracking. The paper-digital combo is how most successful solo estheticians actually operate.

Section 4: Treatment Protocol Library

Reference these protocols during consultations and treatments. Adjust pricing to your local market and experience level. These are starting frameworks -- customize based on your training and product lines.

1. Classic European Facial

Overview

The foundational facial service. Includes cleansing, skin analysis, exfoliation, extractions (if needed), massage, mask, and moisturizer with SPF. Suitable for most skin types and the ideal first-visit service for new clients.

DetailSpecification
Duration60 minutes
Suggested Price Range$75 -- $125
FrequencyEvery 4--6 weeks
Skin TypesAll skin types

Contraindications

  • Active sunburn or open wounds on treatment area
  • Active cold sores (reschedule)
  • Severe cystic acne (modify -- avoid aggressive extractions)

Aftercare Instructions

  • Avoid direct sun exposure for 24--48 hours; apply SPF 30+ daily
  • Do not touch your face or apply makeup for at least 6 hours post-treatment
  • Skip active ingredients (retinol, AHAs, BHAs) for 48 hours
  • Stay hydrated -- drink extra water for 24 hours
  • Mild redness is normal and should resolve within a few hours

2. Chemical Peel (Light / Medium)

Overview

Chemical exfoliation using AHAs (glycolic, lactic, mandelic), BHAs (salicylic), or combination peels. Light peels (20--30% concentration) have minimal downtime. Medium peels (30--50%) produce visible peeling for 3--7 days. Treats hyperpigmentation, fine lines, acne, and texture.

DetailSpecification
Duration30--45 minutes
Suggested Price Range$85 -- $175 (varies by depth)
FrequencyEvery 2--4 weeks (light) / Every 4--6 weeks (medium)
Series Recommendation4--6 sessions for optimal results

Contraindications

  • Accutane use within the past 6--12 months
  • Active retinoid use (discontinue 5--7 days prior)
  • Pregnancy or nursing
  • Recent waxing, laser, or sunburn on treatment area (within 7--14 days)
  • Active herpes simplex (prescribe antiviral prophylaxis for history of cold sores)
  • Fitzpatrick V--VI: use mandelic or lactic acid only; avoid glycolic to prevent PIH

Aftercare Instructions

  • NO sun exposure for 7--14 days. SPF 50 is mandatory, reapply every 2 hours outdoors
  • Do not pick, peel, or scratch flaking skin -- let it shed naturally
  • Use only gentle cleanser and hydrating moisturizer for 7 days
  • No retinol, AHAs, BHAs, vitamin C, or active exfoliants for 7--10 days
  • No hot showers on face, saunas, steam rooms, or heavy exercise for 48 hours
  • Expect redness, tightness, and peeling -- this is normal and means the peel is working

3. Microdermabrasion

Overview

Mechanical exfoliation using diamond-tip or crystal handpiece to remove the outer layer of dead skin cells. Improves texture, reduces mild scarring, evens skin tone, and enhances product penetration. Often combined with a hydrating mask or LED therapy for enhanced results.

DetailSpecification
Duration30--45 minutes (60 with add-ons)
Suggested Price Range$100 -- $175
FrequencyEvery 2--4 weeks
Series Recommendation6--10 sessions for best results

Contraindications

  • Active acne, rosacea flare-ups, or inflamed skin
  • Eczema or psoriasis on treatment area
  • Recent chemical peel (within 14 days)
  • Use of blood thinners (increased bruising risk)
  • Active cold sores
  • Sunburn or recent excessive sun exposure

Aftercare Instructions

  • SPF 30+ daily for at least 2 weeks -- skin is more vulnerable to UV damage
  • Apply hydrating serum and moisturizer morning and night
  • Avoid makeup for 24 hours if possible
  • No retinoids or exfoliating acids for 5--7 days
  • Skin may feel slightly tight or sensitive -- this resolves within 24 hours
  • Stay hydrated and avoid touching the treated area

4. LED Light Therapy

Overview

Non-invasive treatment using specific wavelengths of light to target skin concerns. Red light (630--700nm) stimulates collagen and reduces inflammation. Blue light (400--490nm) kills acne-causing bacteria. Near-infrared (800--900nm) promotes deep tissue healing. Excellent as a standalone or add-on service.

DetailSpecification
Duration15--20 minutes (standalone) / 10--15 minutes (add-on)
Suggested Price Range$35 -- $65 (standalone) / $15 -- $30 (add-on)
Frequency2--3 times per week for best results
Series Recommendation8--12 sessions, then maintenance 1x/week

Contraindications

  • Epilepsy or seizure disorders (light sensitivity)
  • Photosensitizing medications (tetracycline, doxycycline, certain diuretics)
  • Active use of Accutane
  • Pregnancy (as a precaution -- limited research)
  • Eye conditions -- protective goggles must be worn during treatment

Aftercare Instructions

  • No specific downtime -- client can apply makeup and resume normal activities immediately
  • Apply SPF as part of regular routine
  • Drink water to support cellular recovery
  • Results are cumulative -- encourage consistent sessions for visible improvement

Business tip: LED therapy is the highest-margin add-on in esthetics. It takes almost no extra time, has zero consumable cost (just the device), and clients feel immediate results. Offer it as a $25 add-on to every facial and watch your average ticket jump.

5. Microneedling

Overview

Collagen induction therapy using a derma pen or roller with fine needles (0.25mm--2.0mm depth, depending on license and training). Creates controlled micro-injuries that trigger the body's wound-healing response, producing new collagen and elastin. Treats scarring, fine lines, enlarged pores, stretch marks, and hyperpigmentation.

DetailSpecification
Duration45--75 minutes (including numbing time)
Suggested Price Range$200 -- $400 per session
FrequencyEvery 4--6 weeks
Series Recommendation3--6 sessions for significant improvement
Numbing RequiredYes -- 20--30 minutes with topical lidocaine

Contraindications

  • Active acne, pustules, or open wounds in treatment area
  • Accutane use within past 6 months
  • Active eczema, psoriasis, or rosacea
  • Pregnancy or nursing
  • Blood clotting disorders or anticoagulant use
  • Keloid or hypertrophic scarring tendency
  • Active skin infections (bacterial, viral, fungal)
  • Recent Botox or filler (within 2 weeks)

Aftercare Instructions

  • Skin will be red and feel sunburned for 24--72 hours -- this is expected
  • Apply only approved post-procedure serums (hyaluronic acid, growth factors) for 24 hours
  • NO makeup for 24 hours minimum (48 hours ideal)
  • NO sun exposure for 7--14 days. SPF 50 is non-negotiable
  • No retinoids, AHAs, BHAs, or vitamin C for 7 days
  • No swimming, sauna, hot tub, or heavy sweating for 72 hours
  • Sleep on a clean pillowcase (silk preferred)
  • Do not touch or scratch the treated area
  • Full results visible at 4--6 weeks as collagen remodels

6. Waxing Services

Overview

Hair removal using hard wax (no strip, ideal for sensitive areas) or soft/strip wax. Services include face (brow, lip, chin, sideburns), body (underarms, arms, legs, back), and bikini (bikini line, Brazilian). Proper technique minimizes discomfort and ingrown hairs.

Contraindications

  • Accutane use within past 6--12 months (skin tears easily)
  • Active retinoid or prescription exfoliant use on wax area (discontinue 5--7 days prior)
  • Sunburned or irritated skin
  • Varicose veins (avoid waxing directly over them)
  • Recent chemical peel or microdermabrasion (within 7--14 days)
  • Diabetes with poor circulation (higher infection risk)
  • Moles or skin tags -- wax around, never over

Aftercare Instructions

  • Avoid sun exposure, tanning beds, hot baths, and saunas for 24--48 hours
  • No tight clothing over waxed areas for 24 hours
  • Apply a soothing, fragrance-free aloe or post-wax lotion
  • No exfoliating for 48 hours, then gently exfoliate 2--3 times per week to prevent ingrowns
  • Avoid deodorant on underarms for 12 hours post-wax
  • Do not scratch or pick at bumps -- mild redness and bumps resolve within 24 hours
  • For bikini/Brazilian: wear cotton underwear and avoid sexual activity for 24 hours

7. Lash Services

Overview

Includes lash lifts (also called lash perms), lash tinting, and lash lift + tint combos. Lash lifts use a silicone rod and perming solution to curl natural lashes upward, creating the appearance of longer, fuller lashes without extensions. Results last 6--8 weeks through the natural lash cycle.

ServiceDurationSuggested Price
Lash Lift45--60 min$65 -- $95
Lash Tint15--20 min$25 -- $40
Lash Lift + Tint60--75 min$85 -- $120

Contraindications

  • Eye infections, styes, or conjunctivitis
  • Allergic reaction to prior lash treatments (perform patch test 24--48 hours prior)
  • Recent eye surgery (within 3 months)
  • Very short or damaged lashes (lift may not hold or could cause breakage)
  • Pregnancy (some clients experience poor results due to hormone changes)

Aftercare Instructions

  • Keep lashes completely dry for 24--48 hours after treatment
  • Do not rub or touch eyes for 24 hours
  • Avoid waterproof mascara (regular mascara is fine after 48 hours)
  • No oil-based products around the eye area for 48 hours
  • Use a lash serum to nourish and prolong results
  • Avoid saunas, steam rooms, and swimming for 48 hours
  • Results last 6--8 weeks; schedule next appointment at the 6-week mark

8. Brow Services

Overview

Includes brow shaping (wax, tweeze, or thread), brow tinting, brow lamination, and combination services. Brow lamination uses a chemical straightening solution to redirect brow hairs upward and set them in place, creating a fuller, brushed-up look for 4--6 weeks.

ServiceDurationSuggested Price
Brow Shape (wax + tweeze)15--20 min$20 -- $35
Brow Tint10--15 min$20 -- $30
Brow Shape + Tint25--30 min$35 -- $55
Brow Lamination45--60 min$65 -- $95
Brow Lamination + Tint60--75 min$85 -- $120

Contraindications

  • Allergies to dye ingredients (always patch test 24--48 hours before first tint)
  • Open cuts, abrasions, or sunburn on brow area
  • Recent Botox in forehead area (within 72 hours)
  • Active eczema or psoriasis on brow area
  • Over-processed or very thin brow hairs (lamination may cause breakage)

Aftercare Instructions

  • For tinting: avoid oil-based cleansers around brows for 48 hours to extend color
  • For lamination: keep brows dry for 24 hours; do not get them wet, apply makeup, or brush them
  • After 24 hours, brush brows into place daily with a spoolie and apply nourishing brow serum
  • Avoid exfoliating the brow area for 72 hours
  • Results last 4--6 weeks; schedule maintenance at the 4-week mark

9. Body Treatments

Overview

Includes body wraps, scrubs, back facials, and dry brushing. Body treatments address concerns like cellulite appearance, dry skin, back acne (bacne), KP (keratosis pilaris), and overall skin texture. These are high-value upsell services that differentiate your menu.

ServiceDurationSuggested Price

Contraindications

  • Open wounds, sunburn, or rashes on treatment area
  • Pregnancy (certain wraps with detox ingredients -- check ingredient safety)
  • High blood pressure or heart conditions (body wraps increase circulation)
  • Claustrophobia (for full body wraps -- offer alternative draping)
  • Varicose veins (avoid dry brushing or heavy pressure directly on veins)
  • Recent shaving (within 12 hours -- causes stinging with scrubs/peels)

Aftercare Instructions

  • Drink at least 8 glasses of water in the 24 hours following treatment
  • Avoid sun exposure on treated areas for 48 hours
  • For wraps: avoid showering for 4--6 hours to let products continue absorbing
  • For scrubs/peels: moisturize daily and avoid harsh soaps for 48 hours
  • Mild redness or tingling is normal and should resolve within hours

10. Dermaplaning

Overview

Manual exfoliation using a sterile surgical scalpel (10R blade) held at a 45-degree angle to gently scrape away dead skin cells and vellus hair (peach fuzz) from the face. Creates an immediately smoother, brighter complexion and dramatically improves product penetration and makeup application.

DetailSpecification
Duration30--45 minutes (standalone) / 15--20 minutes (add-on)
Suggested Price Range$75 -- $125 (standalone) / $35 -- $50 (add-on)
FrequencyEvery 3--4 weeks (matches skin cell turnover cycle)
Pairs Well WithChemical peels, LED therapy, hydrating facials

Contraindications

  • Active acne, pustules, or inflamed breakouts
  • Rosacea or highly reactive/vascular skin
  • Active cold sores or skin infections
  • Accutane use within past 6 months
  • Use of blood thinners (increased nick risk)
  • Raised moles, skin tags, or warts in treatment area (blade around, not over)
  • Sunburned or recently peeled skin

Aftercare Instructions

  • SPF 30+ daily -- freshly exposed skin is more UV-sensitive
  • No retinoids, AHAs, or BHAs for 3--5 days
  • No heavy makeup for 24 hours (mineral makeup OK after 12 hours)
  • Avoid sun, sauna, steam, and heavy exercise for 24--48 hours
  • Use gentle cleanser and hydrating moisturizer only for 48 hours
  • Hair will NOT grow back thicker -- this is a myth. Vellus hair regrows the same

11. Hydrafacial / Hydrodermabrasion

Overview

Multi-step treatment that combines cleansing, exfoliation, extraction, hydration, and antioxidant protection simultaneously using vortex suction technology. Delivers serums deep into the skin while removing impurities. No downtime. Suitable for almost all skin types, including sensitive skin.

DetailSpecification
Duration30--45 minutes
Suggested Price Range$150 -- $275
FrequencyEvery 2--4 weeks
Series RecommendationMonthly maintenance; series of 3--6 for targeted concerns

Contraindications

  • Active rashes, sunburn, or rosacea flare-up
  • Pregnancy (some boosters contain contraindicated ingredients -- check each one)
  • Active cold sores
  • Recent Botox or filler (within 72 hours)

Aftercare Instructions

  • Minimal to no downtime -- client can apply makeup and resume normal activities
  • Apply SPF as usual
  • Avoid exfoliating products for 48 hours (skin is already freshly exfoliated)
  • Skin may appear slightly pink for 30--60 minutes post-treatment
  • Best results with consistent monthly treatments

Booking & Follow-Up System

Before the Appointment

  • Send booking confirmation immediately after scheduling
  • Send reminder 48 hours before appointment
  • Send reminder 2 hours before appointment (text or automated)
  • Review client profile: allergies, contraindications, last treatment notes
  • Prep room: clean linens, products staged, equipment sanitized

After the Appointment

  • Update treatment notes in client profile within 1 hour
  • Send aftercare instructions via text or email (same day)
  • Send follow-up check-in at 48 hours ("How is your skin feeling?")
  • Send rebooking prompt at 2 weeks if no appointment scheduled
  • Log revenue and services in the daily tracker

Buffer time matters. Schedule 15-minute buffers between appointments for room turnover, notes, and bathroom breaks. Running behind stresses you and the client. A packed schedule with no buffers is a recipe for burnout.

Margin rule of thumb: Retail skincare should have a 50--65% margin (you pay $15, you sell for $38--$42). If your margin is under 50%, the product is not worth shelf space unless it's a loss leader that drives rebookings. Track your cost-per-use on back bar products too -- you need to know the true cost of each treatment you deliver.

Inventory Review Checklist (Monthly)

  • Count all back bar products and update Qty on Hand
  • Count all retail products and update Qty on Hand
  • Check expiration dates -- pull anything expiring within 60 days
  • Reorder products at or below reorder level
  • Review which retail products sold well vs. sat on the shelf
  • Calculate total inventory value (wholesale cost x quantity)
  • Evaluate if any products should be discontinued or replaced

Revenue Health Check

  • Healthy product-to-service ratio: 15--25% (if $1,000 in services, you should sell $150--$250 in retail)

Growth Decision Tree

If retention is below 60%

Your clients are not coming back. Investigate: Are follow-ups happening? Is the experience memorable? Are results meeting expectations? Start with the aftercare follow-up and rebooking templates in Section 8.

If average ticket is stagnant

Introduce add-on services (LED $25, dermaplaning $35, scalp massage $15). Train yourself to recommend one add-on per client visit. "Based on your skin today, I'd love to add 10 minutes of LED therapy -- it'll help with that redness we talked about."

If product-to-service ratio is below 15%

You're leaving money on the table. Use the products on the client during treatment, then recommend the take-home version. "I used this vitamin C serum on you today -- you can feel how it absorbed. Using it at home between visits will maintain everything we did today."

If capacity is above 85%

You're at peak capacity. Time to raise prices (start with 10--15%), extend your rebooking intervals slightly, or bring on a junior esthetician. Do not burn out trying to squeeze more into a full schedule.

If no-show rate is above 5%

Implement or enforce: (1) 48-hour text reminders, (2) credit card on file policy, (3) clear cancellation fee ($25--$50), (4) 24-hour cancellation policy. No-shows cost you the full price of the service in lost revenue.

Marketing Rules for Estheticians

  • Show your face. People book with people, not logos. Your face on your content builds trust faster than anything.
  • Educate more than you sell. 80% education, 20% promotion. Clients who understand their skin invest more in treatment.
  • Post consistently, not constantly. 3--5 posts per week beats 14 mediocre ones. Quality always wins.
  • Before/afters are your best marketing. Get photo consent at intake, take consistent photos (same lighting, same angle, no makeup), and post them regularly.
  • Google reviews matter more than followers. Ask every happy client for a Google review. 50 five-star reviews will fill your books faster than 5,000 Instagram followers.
  • Email your client list monthly. A simple newsletter with one tip, one promo, and one personal touch keeps you top of mind.

Final Notes: Building a Thriving Esthetics Practice

How long until I see results from tracking all of this?

Most estheticians who implement consistent tracking see measurable improvement within 60--90 days. The first month is setup and baseline. The second month shows patterns. By month three, you're making decisions based on data instead of guessing -- and that's when revenue starts climbing.

Do I need to use every section right away?

No. Start with the Client Intake Form and Appointment Tracker -- those are your foundation. Add the Revenue Dashboard in week two. Layer in the Treatment Protocols, Inventory Tracker, and Marketing Calendar as you get comfortable. Building systems is a process, not an event.

What's the single most impactful thing I can do for my business?

Rebook at checkout. If you do nothing else from this entire system, ask every single client to book their next appointment before they leave. A 60%+ rebooking rate transforms an unpredictable schedule into a reliable income stream. Everything else builds on that foundation.

How do I raise my prices without losing clients?

Give 30 days notice. Communicate the value ("I've invested in advanced training and upgraded my product line to give you better results"). Raise by 10--15% at a time, not 50%. Loyal clients understand. The ones who leave over $10 were never your ideal client. And always grandfather existing series packages at the old price.
