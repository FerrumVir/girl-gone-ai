Section 3: Legal & Licensing Checklist

CRITICAL DISCLAIMER: Med spa regulations vary significantly by state. Some states allow NPs and PAs to own and operate independently; others require physician ownership or a management services organization (MSO) structure. The information below is a general framework. You MUST consult a healthcare attorney in your state before making legal or structural decisions.

Entity Formation

  • Choose your entity type: LLC, PLLC, PC, or S-Corp (depends on state and who owns it)
  • Research corporate practice of medicine (CPOM) laws in your state -- this determines if a non-physician can own the med spa
  • If CPOM applies: Set up a Management Services Organization (MSO) model -- business entity manages operations, physician entity provides medical services
  • File Articles of Organization / Incorporation with your Secretary of State
  • Obtain EIN from the IRS (free, takes 5 minutes online)
  • Register for state tax accounts (sales tax if your state taxes services)
  • Open a business bank account -- keep personal and business finances completely separate
  • Draft operating agreement / bylaws with your attorney

Medical Director Agreement

What a Medical Director Does

  • Provides medical oversight for all clinical services
  • Approves treatment protocols and standing orders
  • Reviews patient charts (frequency varies by state -- monthly or quarterly)
  • Available for consultations and emergencies
  • Signs off on prescriptions and medication orders if required

Typical Medical Director Compensation

  • Monthly stipend: $2,000 - $10,000/month depending on involvement and market
  • Per-chart-review: $25 - $75 per chart
  • Equity or profit share: Some states allow, some prohibit (fee-splitting rules)

Contract Must Include

  • Scope of oversight responsibilities
  • Chart review frequency and documentation
  • Compensation structure (must be fair market value -- not based on referral volume)
  • Term, termination, and non-compete clauses
  • Liability and indemnification provisions

Licenses & Permits Checklist

  • State medical facility license (required in most states for med spas)
  • Business license (city/county)
  • Zoning approval -- confirm your location is zoned for medical/commercial use
  • State Board of Pharmacy registration (if dispensing medications or storing controlled substances)
  • DEA registration (if using controlled substances -- some IV therapy protocols)
  • Radiation safety / laser registration (many states require registration of laser devices)
  • OSHA compliance -- bloodborne pathogen plan, sharps disposal, safety protocols
  • HIPAA compliance program -- privacy policies, BAAs with vendors, staff training
  • Certificate of Occupancy from your local building department
  • Fire department inspection / permit
  • Signage permit (if applicable in your municipality)

Pricing Strategy Framework

Three-Tier Pricing Model

  • Premium (Full Price): Your published menu price. This is for walk-ins and first-time clients who are not members.
  • Member Price: 10-20% off menu price for monthly membership holders. This drives recurring revenue and loyalty.
  • Package Price: 15-25% off per-session price when purchasing a series of 3-6 treatments. Increases prepaid revenue and rebooking.

Pricing Rules

  • Never compete on price alone -- compete on experience, results, and expertise
  • Price injectables per unit (Botox) or per syringe (filler) for transparency
  • Avoid Groupon-style deep discounting -- it attracts deal-seekers, not loyal clients
  • Review and adjust pricing annually based on product cost changes and market data
  • Use introductory offers for new clients (e.g., 20% off first treatment) rather than ongoing discounts

Compensation Models for Providers

Common Injector Pay Structures

  • Base + Production Bonus: $80,000-$120,000 base salary + 10-20% commission on collections above a monthly threshold (e.g., 15% of everything collected above $30,000)
  • Straight Commission: 30-45% of collections (higher risk, higher reward for both parties)
  • Hourly + Commission: $40-$60/hour + 5-10% of product revenue (common for part-time or new providers)

Esthetician Pay

  • Hourly + Commission: $18-$28/hour + 10-15% commission on retail sales + 5-10% on service upsells
  • Base + Performance: $40,000-$55,000 base + quarterly bonuses tied to rebooking rate and retail targets

Front Desk Pay

  • Hourly: $16-$24/hour depending on market
  • Bonus opportunities: $50-$150/month for hitting membership enrollment targets, review generation, or rebooking metrics

Hiring Tip: Your front desk person is your most important hire after your injector. They control the patient experience from first phone call to checkout. A great front desk coordinator can increase rebooking rates by 30-50% and drive significant retail revenue. Hire for personality and train for skills.

Referral Program

Structure: Give $50, Get $50

  • Existing client refers a friend who books and completes a treatment
  • Referring client receives $50 credit toward any service
  • New client receives $50 off their first treatment
  • Track with referral cards (physical) and a simple spreadsheet or CRM tagging
  • Promote the program at checkout, in follow-up emails, and on social media

Expected ROI: Referred clients have a 25-35% higher lifetime value than non-referred clients. A $100 referral cost ($50 to each side) typically generates $2,000-$5,000 in lifetime revenue.

Before & After Content Strategy

Photo Protocol

  • Consent: Always get signed photo/video consent BEFORE the treatment
  • Lighting: Use consistent ring light or overhead lighting -- same setup every time
  • Angles: Front, 45-degree left, 45-degree right, profile (for every patient, every time)
  • Background: Plain, clean background -- same color/surface for all photos
  • Timing: "Before" photos pre-treatment; "After" photos at optimal result time (2 weeks for Botox, 2-4 weeks for filler, etc.)
  • Storage: Organize by date, patient initials, and treatment type in a secure, HIPAA-compliant system

Content tip: Before/after photos are the single highest-converting content type in med spa marketing. Build a library from day one. Offer clients a small discount ($25 off next visit) for permission to use their photos on social media.

Section 9: Client Experience Flow

Map every touchpoint from first contact to loyal repeat client. The experience IS the brand.

Step 1: First Contact

Channels: Phone, DM, Website Form, Online Booking

  • Respond to all inquiries within 5 minutes during business hours (speed matters enormously)
  • Have templated but personalized responses for common questions
  • Always end with a booking action: "Let's get you scheduled for a complimentary consultation. Does Tuesday or Thursday work better?"
  • If they don't book, follow up in 24 hours, then 72 hours, then 7 days

Step 2: Pre-Appointment

48-Hour and Day-Of Touchpoints

  • Appointment confirmation via text/email at booking, 48 hours before, and morning of
  • Pre-treatment instructions sent with confirmation (avoid blood thinners, no alcohol 24 hrs, etc.)
  • Digital intake forms sent in advance so the client completes them before arriving (saves 15 min at check-in)
  • Parking/directions info included in the confirmation

Step 3: Arrival & Consultation

In-Office Experience

  • Greet by name within 30 seconds of entering
  • Offer water, tea, or coffee
  • Tour the facility if it's their first visit
  • Private consultation room -- never discuss treatment plans in public areas
  • Provider reviews goals, performs assessment, takes before photos, presents treatment plan with pricing
  • No pressure -- let the plan speak for itself. Offer to start today or schedule for later.

Step 4: Treatment

During the Procedure

  • Explain every step before doing it ("I'm going to apply the numbing cream now...")
  • Check in on comfort throughout
  • Keep conversation natural and professional
  • Maintain a calm, clean, private environment
  • Document treatment in EMR in real time or immediately after

Step 5: Aftercare & Checkout

Post-Treatment Protocol

  • Review aftercare instructions verbally AND provide printed/digital copy
  • Take post-treatment photos (immediate "after" for applicable services)
  • Process payment and apply any membership discounts
  • Book the next appointment BEFORE they leave (rebooking rate target: 70%+)
  • Recommend relevant skincare products (this is clinical advice, not a sales pitch)
  • Walk them to the door and thank them by name

Step 7: Loyalty & Retention

Loyalty Program Options

  • Points-based: $1 spent = 1 point; 500 points = $50 off. Simple and universally understood.
  • Tier-based: Silver / Gold / Platinum based on annual spend, with increasing perks at each level
  • Membership (recommended): Monthly membership with included services + discounts (see Section 6)
  • Birthday perks: Free treatment add-on or $50 credit during their birthday month
  • Allergan Brilliant Distinctions / Alle: Enroll clients in manufacturer loyalty programs (Botox/Juvederm points)

Retention Goal: It costs 5-7x more to acquire a new client than to retain an existing one. Your target client retention rate should be 60-70% at 12 months. Track rebooking rate, client lifetime value, and visit frequency as core KPIs.

Section 10: Operations Checklist

Daily Opening Procedures

  • Unlock facility and disarm security system
  • Turn on all lights, HVAC, music system
  • Power on all treatment devices and verify they are functioning
  • Review the day's schedule -- confirm all appointments, note new clients
  • Verify treatment rooms are stocked and sanitized
  • Confirm injectable product inventory matches the day's bookings
  • Check retail displays -- stocked, clean, well-lit
  • Open POS system and confirm payment processing is operational
  • Review any client notes, follow-ups due, or special instructions
  • Brief team huddle (5 minutes): today's schedule, VIPs, any promotions running

Daily Closing Procedures

  • All treatment rooms cleaned and sanitized per protocol
  • Sharps containers sealed if full; biohazard waste secured
  • All devices powered down properly (per manufacturer instructions)
  • Restock treatment rooms for tomorrow
  • End-of-day reconciliation: payments received vs. services rendered
  • Review tomorrow's schedule -- any prep needed? Special orders?
  • Check and respond to all phone messages, DMs, and emails received today
  • Lock all medications and controlled substances in secure storage
  • Turn off lights, set HVAC to after-hours mode, set alarm
  • Lock all doors and verify secure

Inventory Management

Product Inventory Tracking

  • Injectable products (Botox, fillers): Track by unit/syringe. Count weekly. Reconcile against treatments performed. These are your highest-cost items -- shrinkage or waste directly impacts profit.
  • Skincare retail: Count monthly. Set reorder points (reorder when stock hits 3-4 units). Track sell-through rates to avoid dead stock.
  • Consumables (tips, cartridges, serums): Count bi-weekly. Order on a regular schedule from your distributor.
  • Medical supplies: Monthly count. Maintain a par level for each item.

Systems

  • Use your EMR/POS inventory module if available (most med spa software includes this)
  • Alternatively, a simple spreadsheet with: item, current count, par level, reorder point, supplier, cost per unit
  • Designate one team member as inventory lead
  • Set up auto-reorder with major suppliers when possible

Compliance Documentation

  • HIPAA policies posted and accessible; staff trained annually; breach response plan documented
  • OSHA compliance binder: Exposure control plan, SDS sheets, bloodborne pathogen training logs
  • Medical director chart reviews: Documented per your agreement (monthly or quarterly)
  • Treatment protocols/standing orders: Signed by medical director, current, accessible to all providers
  • Informed consent forms: Signed before every procedure, stored in patient EMR
  • Photo/video consent forms: Separate from treatment consent; stored securely
  • Staff credentials file: Current licenses, certifications, CPR/BLS, malpractice coverage for each provider
  • Equipment maintenance logs: Calibration records, service dates, manufacturer-required maintenance
  • Adverse event/incident reports: Template on file; completed for any complications or incidents
  • Emergency protocol: Posted in treatment rooms -- anaphylaxis kit, emergency numbers, hospital route

Final Notes & Reminders

How long until my med spa is profitable?

Most med spas reach operational break-even within 2-4 months if well-marketed and properly staffed. Full return on initial investment typically takes 12-24 months. The speed depends on your startup costs, marketing effectiveness, and how quickly you build a client base. Injectables generate revenue from day one with minimal overhead -- they should be your cash engine.

Should I lease or buy equipment?

Lease your first major device unless you have strong cash reserves. Leasing preserves capital, lets you test demand before committing, and often includes maintenance. Once you know a device generates reliable revenue (6-12 months of data), purchase your next one. The sweet spot is owning your highest-use devices and leasing newer or lower-volume equipment.

What is the biggest mistake new med spa owners make?

Two things: (1) Over-investing in buildout and equipment before proving market demand, and (2) Under-investing in marketing. Your space does not need to be perfect on day one. Your marketing needs to be running before you open the doors. Patients do not show up just because you exist -- you have to reach them.

Can I do this without being a medical professional?

Yes, but it requires the right legal structure. Many successful med spas are owned by non-clinical entrepreneurs who partner with a medical director and employ clinical providers. You will likely need an MSO structure. Work with a healthcare attorney who specializes in aesthetics -- this is not optional.

You are building a real business. The aesthetics industry rewards those who combine clinical excellence with smart business strategy. Take it one section at a time. Execute consistently. The clients will come.
