Section 1: Morning Kickstart System

Start every day with clarity instead of chaos. These prompts replace the 30-minute scroll session with a 5-minute planning session that actually works.

Daily Planning Prompt

The 5-Minute Day Designer

I have the following tasks today: [paste your to-do list]

My energy level right now is [high / medium / low].
I have [X] hours of deep work time available.
My one must-win task today is: [describe it]

Please:
1. Prioritize these tasks by impact (not urgency)
2. Group them into 90-minute focus blocks
3. Put the hardest task in my peak energy window
4. Identify 2 tasks I should delegate or delete
5. Give me a realistic schedule with breaks built in

The Weekly Reset

It's [day]. Here's what happened this week:

Wins: [list 3-5 things that went well]
Misses: [list things that didn't happen]
Surprises: [anything unexpected]
Energy: I felt most productive on [days/times]

Please:
1. Identify patterns in my productivity
2. Suggest 3 adjustments for next week
3. Help me set my top 3 priorities for next week
4. Flag anything I should stop doing

The Decision Unsticker

I'm stuck deciding between:
Option A: [describe]
Option B: [describe]

Context: [relevant background]
Timeline: [when I need to decide]
What matters most to me: [your priorities]

Please:
1. List the top 3 pros and cons of each option
2. Identify which option is more reversible
3. Tell me what I'll regret more: choosing wrong or not choosing at all
4. Give me your recommendation and why

Section 2: Email & Communication Templates

Stop spending 20 minutes writing a 3-sentence email. These prompts generate professional, human-sounding messages in seconds.

Professional Email Prompts

The Follow-Up That Actually Gets Replies

Write a follow-up email for this situation:

Original context: [what the first email/meeting was about]
How long ago: [days since last contact]
What I need from them: [specific ask]
Relationship: [cold lead / warm contact / existing client / colleague]
Tone: [friendly but direct / casual / formal]

Make it:
- Under 100 words
- Include a specific question they can answer quickly
- Add a subtle deadline or reason to reply now
- Sound human, not templated

The "Saying No" Template

I need to decline this request: [describe what was asked]
From: [who asked, and our relationship]
Why I'm saying no: [honest reason]

Write a response that:
- Is kind but clear (no "maybe later" hedging)
- Acknowledges their request genuinely
- Offers an alternative if appropriate
- Keeps the relationship intact
- Is under 80 words

The Cold Outreach Message

Write a cold outreach message for:

Who I'm reaching out to: [their role/company]
What I'm offering: [product/service/collaboration]
Why them specifically: [what I noticed about them]
Platform: [email / LinkedIn / DM]
My credibility: [brief proof I'm legit]

Rules:
- First line must be about THEM, not me
- Under 100 words
- One clear CTA
- No corporate jargon
- Sound like a real person, not a sales bot

Section 3: Content Creation Accelerator

Whether you're writing social posts, blog articles, or video scripts, these prompts cut your creation time by 80%.

Social Media Content

The Viral Hook Generator

Generate 10 scroll-stopping hooks for a [platform] post about [topic].

My audience: [who they are]
Goal: [educate / entertain / sell / inspire]
My brand voice: [describe in 3 words]

Rules for each hook:
- Under 15 words
- Creates curiosity or tension
- Feels personal, not corporate
- Would make YOU stop scrolling

Format: Number each hook. Star the top 3.

The 7-Day Content Calendar

Create a 7-day content calendar for my [platform].

My niche: [topic/industry]
My audience: [demographics + pain points]
My products/services: [what I sell]
Content pillars: [3-4 themes I rotate through]
Posting frequency: [X times per day/week]

For each day, give me:
1. Content type (carousel, reel, story, text post)
2. Topic + angle
3. Hook (first line)
4. CTA
5. Best posting time
6. Hashtags (5-7, mix of niche and broad)

The Blog-to-Social Repurposer

Here's a blog post / article I wrote:

[paste your content]

Turn this into:
1. Three Twitter/X threads (each with a different angle)
2. One Instagram carousel script (8-10 slides)
3. One LinkedIn post (storytelling format)
4. Three Instagram story talking points
5. One TikTok/Reel script (30-45 seconds)

Keep my voice. Don't make it sound like a robot wrote it.

Section 4: Research & Learning Shortcuts

Learn faster, research smarter, and actually retain what you read.

The Speed-Learn Framework

I want to learn [topic/skill] in [timeframe].

My current level: [beginner / intermediate / advanced]
How I learn best: [reading / watching / doing / discussing]
Time I can commit daily: [X minutes/hours]
Why I'm learning this: [specific goal]

Create:
1. A learning roadmap broken into weekly milestones
2. The top 5 resources (free preferred) ranked by quality
3. A daily 30-minute practice routine
4. 3 mini-projects to test my knowledge
5. Common mistakes beginners make (so I can skip them)

The Article Summarizer + Action Extractor

Here's an article/report I need to process:

[paste the content]

Give me:
1. A 3-sentence summary (what, so what, now what)
2. The 5 key takeaways
3. What's actually new vs. what's common knowledge
4. 2-3 specific actions I could take based on this
5. One thing the author might be wrong about

The Meeting Prep Briefing

I have a meeting about [topic] with [who] in [timeframe].

Context: [what the meeting is about]
My role: [what I need to contribute]
My goal: [what I want to walk away with]
Concerns: [anything tricky or political]

Prepare me with:
1. Three smart questions to ask
2. Key talking points (with data if relevant)
3. Potential objections and my responses
4. A 30-second opening statement
5. The one thing I absolutely must communicate

Section 5: Business & Side Hustle Prompts

Whether you're running a business or building one on the side, these prompts handle the thinking work so you can focus on execution.

The Product Idea Validator

I have a product/service idea:

The idea: [describe it]
Target customer: [who would buy this]
Price point: [what I'd charge]
How it's different: [vs. existing options]

Be brutally honest:
1. Rate this idea 1-10 on market demand, competition, and execution difficulty
2. Who are the top 3 competitors and what do they charge?
3. What's the #1 reason this could fail?
4. What's the fastest way to validate demand before building?
5. If you were launching this tomorrow, what would you do first?

The Pricing Strategy Builder

Help me price my [product/service]:

What it is: [describe]
Who buys it: [target customer]
What they currently pay for alternatives: [competitor pricing]
My costs: [time, materials, tools]
My goal: [revenue target or positioning]

Give me:
1. Three pricing tiers (good / better / best) with what's included in each
2. The psychology behind each price point
3. Whether I should anchor high or start low
4. A launch pricing strategy (discount, early bird, etc.)
5. When and how to raise prices

The Customer Avatar Deep Dive

Help me build a detailed customer avatar for my [business/product]:

What I sell: [describe]
Current customers (if any): [who's buying now]
Industry: [niche]

Build a complete profile including:
1. Demographics (age, income, location, job)
2. Psychographics (values, fears, desires, frustrations)
3. Where they spend time online (specific platforms and communities)
4. What they Google at 2 AM (actual search queries)
5. The exact words they use to describe their problem
6. What they've already tried that didn't work
7. What would make them buy instantly vs. think about it

Section 6: Writing & Editing Toolkit

The Tone Matcher

Here's a sample of my writing style:

[paste 2-3 paragraphs of YOUR writing]

Now write [what you need] in this exact voice. Match:
- Sentence length and rhythm
- Level of formality
- Use of humor or directness
- Vocabulary level
- Any quirks or patterns you notice

Topic: [what to write about]
Length: [word count]
Purpose: [educate / sell / persuade / entertain]

The Ruthless Editor

Edit this text ruthlessly:

[paste your draft]

Rules:
1. Cut word count by 30% without losing meaning
2. Replace every passive voice sentence with active
3. Kill all corporate jargon and buzzwords
4. Make every sentence earn its place
5. Strengthen the opening and closing
6. Flag any claims that need evidence

Show me the before/after for each major change so I can learn.

The Headline Tester

I'm writing about: [topic]
For: [audience]
Goal: [get clicks / get shares / get sign-ups / get purchases]

Generate 15 headlines. For each one, rate it on:
- Clarity (1-5): Is it immediately clear what this is about?
- Curiosity (1-5): Does it create an itch to click?
- Specificity (1-5): Does it promise something concrete?

Star the top 3. Explain why they work best.

Section 8: Quick-Reference Prompt Formulas

Master these formulas and you can create your own prompts for anything.

Formula Structure Best For
R-T-F Role + Task + Format
"Act as a [role]. Your task is to [task]. Format the output as [format]."		 General-purpose prompts
C-R-E-A-T-E Context + Role + Examples + Adjustments + Type + Extras Complex, high-quality outputs
B-A-B Before (current state) + After (desired state) + Bridge (how to get there) Problem-solving, strategy
S-T-A-R Situation + Task + Action + Result Case studies, resumes, storytelling
Chain of Thought "Think step by step. First... then... finally..." Analysis, reasoning, math, logic
Few-Shot Give 2-3 examples of what you want, then ask for more Matching a specific style or format

Pro Tip: The single biggest improvement you can make to any prompt is adding specific context. "Write me a blog post" is weak. "Write a 500-word blog post for female entrepreneurs aged 25-35 who are launching their first Etsy shop, in a conversational tone with actionable tips" is strong. Context is everything.

Section 9: AI Power User Cheat Sheet

Do's and Don'ts

Do This Not This
Be specific: "Write 5 Instagram captions for a skincare brand targeting women 25-35" Be vague: "Write some social media posts"
Give examples of what you want Hope it reads your mind
Iterate: "Good, but make it shorter and more casual" Accept the first output as final
Set constraints: "Under 100 words, no jargon" Leave it open-ended
Assign a role: "Act as a marketing strategist" Skip the context
Break complex tasks into steps Ask for everything in one massive prompt
Ask it to explain its reasoning Blindly trust every output
Use it as a thinking partner Use it as a replacement for thinking

Hidden Features Most People Miss

  • "Critique this like a [role]" — Ask AI to poke holes in your own work from a specific perspective (investor, customer, competitor)
  • "Give me the contrarian view" — Forces AI to argue against your assumption. Great for stress-testing ideas.
  • "Explain this to a [audience]" — Adjusts complexity. "Explain blockchain to a 10-year-old" vs. "to a CTO" gives wildly different (and useful) results.
  • "What questions should I be asking?" — When you don't know what you don't know, this prompt surfaces blind spots.
  • "Create a decision matrix" — AI builds weighted comparison tables. Perfect for tool selection, vendor evaluation, career decisions.
  • Paste errors directly — Copy-paste error messages, screenshots descriptions, or broken code. AI is excellent at debugging when given the actual error.
