08 — Risk & Single-Points-of-Failure

Where the system can break, and how to mitigate

Summary

4 critical risks, 4 high risks, 3 medium risks, 1 low risk. The dominant pattern: external service auth failures and board-gated approvals create cascading blocks that prevent revenue generation and agent scaling. The single most impactful SPOF is Kenny as sole human approver—every budget decision, infrastructure change, and hire approval routes through one person.

Critical Path

Three of the four critical risks form a dependency chain: Gumroad access broken → storefront cannot be automated → $0 revenue persists. Resolving Gumroad storefront access (manual browser login) is the single highest-leverage action available. Meanwhile, HeyGen 401 errors block automated avatar video, removing the brand’s primary differentiator from the content pipeline.

Board-Gated Risks

Agent hire approvals (JAC-7, JAC-8) remain blocked at the board level. Until Banker and Investor agents are approved, the CEO agent manually handles treasury and deal-flow functions—a stopgap that does not scale.

⚠ 3 critical risks are currently ACTIVE. Revenue generation is blocked until Gumroad storefront access is resolved.
