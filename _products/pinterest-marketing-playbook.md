# Pinterest Marketing Playbook

> Complete Pinterest strategy for driving organic traffic. Pin design formulas, keyword research method, board structure, 30-day pinning schedule, SEO optimization, and seasonal calendar.

## SECTION 1: ACCOUNT SETUP & OPTIMIZATION

### Profile Optimization Checklist
```
- [ ] Business account (not personal) — switch in Settings > Account
- [ ] Claimed website (Settings > Claimed accounts)
- [ ] Rich Pins enabled (validates your site's metadata on pins)
- [ ] Profile name: [YOUR NAME or BRAND] | [KEYWORD] (e.g., "Sarah's Kitchen | Easy Meal Prep Recipes")
- [ ] Username: clean, memorable, matches your brand
- [ ] Profile photo: clear, branded, recognizable at small size
- [ ] About section: keyword-rich, describes what followers will find
- [ ] Website URL: linked and verified
```

### About Section Template (160 characters max display, 500 stored)
```
[WHO YOU HELP] find [WHAT CONTENT]. [KEYWORD-RICH DESCRIPTION]. [CTA — "Follow for daily [TOPIC]"]

Example: "Helping busy professionals find quick healthy recipes, meal prep guides, and kitchen organization tips. Follow for daily food inspiration that saves you time."
```

## SECTION 3: KEYWORD RESEARCH METHOD

### The 5-Step Pinterest Keyword Research Process

**Step 1: Seed Keywords**
```
List 10 topics/themes your target audience searches for:
1. [TOPIC]
2. [TOPIC]
3. [TOPIC]
... (list 10)
```

**Step 2: Pinterest Autocomplete Mining**
```
1. Go to Pinterest search bar
2. Type your seed keyword (e.g., "morning routine")
3. DO NOT press enter — look at the dropdown suggestions
4. Screenshot or record every suggestion
5. Click into one, look at the "guided search" bubbles below the search bar
6. Those bubbles = additional keywords Pinterest associates with your topic
7. Record ALL bubble keywords

Example for "morning routine":
- morning routine aesthetic
- morning routine checklist
- morning routine for women
- morning routine productive
- Bubbles: "for school" "healthy" "skincare" "workout" "5am" "self care"
```

**Step 3: Pinterest Trends**
```
1. Go to trends.pinterest.com
2. Search your seed keywords
3. Note: seasonal patterns (when searches peak)
4. Note: related trending terms
5. Note: rising vs. declining keywords
6. Priority: Create content for keywords trending UP before they peak
```

**Step 4: Competitor Pin Analysis**
```
1. Find 5-10 accounts in your niche with strong followings
2. Sort their pins by "Best" (most saves/engagement)
3. Read the text overlay on their top pins
4. Read their pin descriptions
5. Record the keywords they use repeatedly
6. Those keywords = proven demand in your niche
```

**Step 5: Organize into Keyword Map**
```
| Seed Keyword | Long-tail Variations | Monthly Trend | Board Assignment |
|---|---|---|---|
| morning routine | morning routine for women over 30 | Peaks Jan, Sept | Board: Self Care Routines |
| morning routine | 5am morning routine checklist | Steady | Board: Productivity Tips |
| morning routine | morning skincare routine order | Peaks year-round | Board: Skincare Routines |
```

## SECTION 4: PIN DESIGN FORMULAS

### Pin Dimensions & Specs
```
Standard Pin: 1000 x 1500px (2:3 ratio) — REQUIRED
Idea Pin: 1080 x 1920px (9:16 ratio)
Video Pin: 1080 x 1920px (9:16 ratio), 4-15 seconds optimal
Minimum text size: 20pt (must be readable on mobile)
File format: JPG or PNG (keep under 10MB)
```

### The 10 High-Performing Pin Templates

**Template 1: The Bold Title Pin**
```
Layout:
- Background: solid color or simple photo
- Large bold text (2-4 words): THE MAIN KEYWORD/HOOK
- Smaller subtitle below: context or specific angle
- Small logo or URL at bottom

Example:
[Photo of organized pantry]
"PANTRY ORGANIZATION"
"10 Dollar Store Hacks That Actually Work"
yoursite.com
```

**Template 2: The Numbered List Pin**
```
Layout:
- Bold number or "X Ways/Tips/Ideas" at top
- List of 3-5 items visible (not all of them — create curiosity)
- Clean background (solid or blurred photo)
- "Read more" or arrow suggesting there's more content

Example:
"7 Morning Habits"
"That Changed My Productivity:"
1. [visible]
2. [visible]
3. [visible]
"...see all 7 →"
```

**Template 3: The Before/After Pin**
```
Layout:
- Split vertically or horizontally
- "Before" clearly labeled on one side
- "After" clearly labeled on the other
- Minimal text — let the visual contrast do the work
- Small text at bottom with context

Works for: Home decor, fitness, organization, design, skincare
```

**Template 4: The Step-by-Step Pin**
```
Layout:
- Title at top: "How to [RESULT]"
- 3-4 numbered steps with small icons or photos
- Each step: brief 3-5 word description
- Branded footer

Works for: Tutorials, recipes, DIY, routines
```

**Template 5: The Quote/Tip Pin**
```
Layout:
- Single powerful statement or tip
- Large text, centered
- Decorative but minimal background
- Attributed if it's a quote

Works for: Motivational content, life tips, one-line advice
```

**Template 6: The Infographic Pin**
```
Layout:
- Title at top
- Data, icons, or visual hierarchy showing information
- Longer format (can be up to 1000x2100 for infographics)
- Sections clearly divided
- Footer with CTA

Works for: Educational content, comparisons, guides
```

**Template 7: The Product Pin**
```
Layout:
- Clean product photo (lifestyle or flat lay)
- Product name + price visible
- Brief benefit statement (not feature)
- "Shop now" or similar CTA
- Brand logo

Works for: E-commerce, physical products, digital products
```

**Template 8: The Collage Pin**
```
Layout:
- 3-6 photos arranged in a grid or collage
- Unifying title at top or center
- All photos share a color palette or theme
- Gives a "mood board" feel

Works for: Style roundups, recipe collections, room inspo
```

**Template 9: The Video Pin**
```
Layout:
- 4-15 seconds (short and looped)
- Text overlay for silent viewing
- First frame must be compelling (it's the thumbnail)
- Movement catches attention in the feed

Works for: Recipes, transformations, tutorials, before/afters
```

**Template 10: The Idea Pin (Multi-Page)**
```
Layout:
- Page 1: Hook/title (what they'll learn/see)
- Pages 2-5: Content delivered one point per page
- Last page: CTA or summary
- Each page: large text + supporting visual
- Voiceover optional but helps

Works for: Tutorials, tips, storytelling, guides
```

### Pin Design Rules
```
1. Use 2-3 fonts maximum (one headline, one body, one accent)
2. Text should cover 20-40% of the pin (not more)
3. Use high-contrast colors (text must be readable on mobile)
4. Consistent brand colors across all pins (builds recognition)
5. Leave "breathing room" — don't crowd every corner
6. Test: if you squint and can't read it, the text is too small
```

## SECTION 5: PIN DESCRIPTION SEO

### Pin Description Template (500 characters)
```
[PRIMARY KEYWORD] — [HOOK/BENEFIT STATEMENT]. Learn [WHAT THEY'LL GET] including [KEYWORD 2], [KEYWORD 3], and [KEYWORD 4]. Perfect for [TARGET AUDIENCE] looking for [RESULT]. Click to read the full [BLOG POST / GUIDE / TUTORIAL]. #[keyword] #[keyword] #[keyword]

Example:
"Easy meal prep ideas for beginners — save time and eat healthy all week with these 30-minute recipes. Includes shopping lists, storage tips, and batch cooking schedules for busy professionals. Perfect for anyone starting a meal prep routine on a budget. Click to get the free printable meal plan. #mealprep #healthyrecipes #mealprepforbeginners"
```

### Pin Title Template (100 characters)
```
[NUMBER] [KEYWORD] [FOR WHO/WHAT RESULT]

Examples:
"15 Easy Meal Prep Recipes for Busy Weeknights"
"Small Bedroom Organization Ideas That Actually Work"
"Beginner Yoga Routine for Flexibility (20 Minutes)"
```

### SEO Placement Priority
```
1. Pin title (highest weight)
2. Pin description (first sentence matters most)
3. Board name
4. Board description
5. Image file name (rename before uploading: "easy-meal-prep-recipes.jpg")
6. Alt text (fill this in — Pinterest reads it)
7. Your profile name
```

## SECTION 6: 30-DAY PINNING SCHEDULE

### Pinning Frequency
```
Minimum: 5 pins/day
Recommended: 10-15 pins/day
Maximum: 25 pins/day
Mix: 80% your content, 20% repins from others (curated quality)
Spacing: spread throughout the day (use a scheduler like Tailwind)
```

### Week 1: Foundation
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | Create 3 pins for your top blog post/product. Pin to most relevant board. |
| 2 | Create 3 pins for second piece of content. Pin to relevant board. |
| 3 | Repin 10 high-quality pins from others to your boards. Create 2 new pins. |
| 4 | Create 3 pins for third piece of content. Pin to relevant board. |
| 5 | Create your first Idea Pin (multi-page tutorial/tips). |
| 6 | Repin 10 quality pins. Create 2 fresh pins for older content. |
| 7 | Review first week analytics. Note which pins got impressions. |

### Week 2: Consistency
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 8 | Create 3 new pin designs for your best-performing content from Week 1 |
| 9 | Pin to all relevant boards (same content, different boards — space 24hr apart) |
| 10 | Create 1 Idea Pin + 2 standard pins |
| 11 | Create 3 pins for new content piece |
| 12 | Repin 10 quality pins. Create 2 fresh pins. |
| 13 | Create 1 video pin (even a simple slideshow works) |
| 14 | Review Week 2 analytics. Double down on what's working. |

### Week 3: Growth
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 15 | Create 5 pins targeting your top-performing keywords |
| 16 | Create 2 Idea Pins on related but different topics |
| 17 | Make 3 "test" pins: different titles/designs for same content |
| 18 | Repin 10 + create 3 fresh pins |
| 19 | Create 1 video pin + 2 standard pins |
| 20 | Pin to group boards (if relevant ones exist in your niche) |
| 21 | Review Week 3. Which pin DESIGNS perform best? Note style. |

### Week 4: Optimize
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 22 | Create 5 new pins using your best-performing design style |
| 23 | Create 3 pins targeting seasonal/trending keywords |
| 24 | Repin 10 + create 3 fresh pins for older content |
| 25 | Create 2 Idea Pins |
| 26 | Update board descriptions with new keywords discovered |
| 27 | Create 5 pins batch for next week |
| 28 | Full monthly review (see analytics section) |
| 29 | Plan next month's keyword targets based on trends |
| 30 | Create 10 pins batch for first week of next month |


### Key Metrics Definitions
```
Impressions: How many times your pins appeared in feeds/search (awareness)
Saves: How many people saved your pin to their board (interest signal to algorithm)
Pin clicks: Clicks on your pin to see it larger (mild interest)
Outbound clicks: Clicks TO YOUR WEBSITE (this is the money metric)
Engagement rate: (saves + clicks) / impressions x 100
```

### Optimization Rules
```
- Pin getting impressions but no clicks? → Title/text isn't compelling enough. Test new copy.
- Pin getting clicks but no saves? → Content is interesting but not "save-worthy." Make it more actionable.
- Board getting zero traction? → Board name might be wrong keyword. Rename it.
- Outbound clicks dropping? → Your link might be broken. Check all destination URLs.
- High saves, low outbound? → That's fine for brand awareness. Add clearer CTAs if you want clicks.
- One pin goes viral? → Create 5 variations of that exact pin (different titles, same topic).
```

## SECTION 9: PINTEREST-SPECIFIC BEST PRACTICES

### Do's
```
- Pin consistently (daily is better than batches with gaps)
- Use a scheduler (Tailwind, Later, Pinterest native scheduler)
- Create multiple pin designs for the same content (3-5 per blog post)
- Write unique descriptions for every single pin (no copy-paste)
- Use clear, readable text on pins (test on mobile)
- Link every pin to a relevant, working URL
- Engage with others' content (repin, comment)
- Update boards seasonally
```

### Don'ts
```
- Don't pin the same image to 10 boards in one day (spam signal)
- Don't use hashtags excessively (2-5 max in descriptions)
- Don't delete and re-upload pins (kills momentum)
- Don't ignore video/Idea Pins (algorithm favors them)
- Don't pin to irrelevant boards
- Don't use clickbait that doesn't match your landing page
- Don't neglect mobile optimization (85% of users are mobile)
- Don't give up before 3-6 months (Pinterest is SLOW to start but compounds)
```

### Timeline Expectations
```
Month 1: Setting up, creating pins, minimal traffic. Normal.
Month 2-3: Impressions start growing. Some clicks trickle in.
Month 4-6: Compound growth begins. Traffic becomes consistent.
Month 6-12: Established pins continue driving traffic for months/years.

Pinterest is a LONG GAME. A pin you create today can drive traffic 2 years from now.
This is NOT like Instagram where content dies in 24 hours.
```
