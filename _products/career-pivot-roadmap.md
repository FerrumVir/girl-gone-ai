# Career Pivot Roadmap — 90-Day Framework for Career Change

---

## How to Use This Roadmap

This is a 90-day structured plan for making a career change. It breaks down the overwhelming process into daily and weekly actions across four phases: Research, Build, Connect, and Launch.

**Who this is for:**
- You're unhappy in your current role and want to move to something different
- You know you want a change but don't know where to start
- You're mid-career and worried about "starting over"
- You want to pivot strategically (not just quit and hope)

**How the 90 days break down:**
- Weeks 1-3: Clarity & Research (know where you're going)
- Weeks 4-7: Skill Building & Portfolio (become qualified)
- Weeks 8-10: Networking & Positioning (get known)
- Weeks 11-13: Application & Launch (land the role)

Reach out to 5 people currently in your target role(s). Use this message:

```

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Questions to ask in informational interviews:**
1. "What does a typical day/week actually look like?"
2. "What surprised you most about this role?"
3. "What skills are most important (that might not be in the job description)?"
4. "How did you make the transition? What would you do differently?"
5. "What's the biggest challenge you face?"
6. "If someone wanted to break in with my background, what would you suggest?"
7. "Is there anyone else you'd recommend I speak with?"

### Week 6: Continue Building + Create Your Narrative

**The Career Pivot Narrative (Your Story)**

Every career changer needs a clear, confident answer to: "Why are you making this change?"

**Write your narrative (practice saying it out loud):**

```
"For the past [X years], I've been in [CURRENT FIELD] doing [WHAT YOU DO].
What I loved most about that work was [TRANSFERABLE ELEMENT —
e.g., problem-solving, working with people, building systems].

Over time, I realized that what energizes me most is [THING THAT ALIGNS
WITH NEW FIELD]. I started exploring [NEW FIELD] and found that
my [TRANSFERABLE SKILL 1] and [TRANSFERABLE SKILL 2] are directly
applicable — I just want to apply them in [NEW CONTEXT].

I've spent the past [X weeks/months] [WHAT YOU'VE DONE TO PREPARE:
completing a certification in X, building projects, talking to people in the field].

I'm now looking for [TARGET ROLE] where I can combine my [PREVIOUS EXPERIENCE]
with my new skills in [NEW AREA] to [CREATE VALUE]."
```

**The key elements:**
1. Acknowledge where you've been (don't erase your past)
2. Connect the dots (what transfers)
3. Show initiative (what you've done to prepare)
4. Be specific about what you want (not "something different")

### Week 9: Public Positioning

**Start Sharing Content in Your New Field:**

Post 2-3x per week on LinkedIn (or your relevant platform) about your new area. Ideas:

- "5 things I'm learning as I transition into [FIELD]"
- Share an insight from a course/book you're studying
- Comment thoughtfully on posts by leaders in your target field
- Document a portfolio project in progress
- Share a lesson from an informational interview (with permission)
- "My unpopular opinion about [TOPIC IN NEW FIELD]" (shows you're thinking deeply)

**Why this matters:** Hiring managers Google you. When they see thoughtful content about their field, you immediately stand out from other career changers.

### Week 12: Interview Preparation

**Career Pivot Interview Questions (prepare answers for all):**

1. "Tell me about yourself." (Use your pivot narrative — 2 minutes max)
2. "Why are you leaving [CURRENT FIELD]?" (Focus on what you're moving TOWARD, not away from)
3. "Why should we hire you over someone with direct experience?" (Your unique value proposition)
4. "How do your previous skills apply here?" (Specific transferable examples with results)
5. "What have you done to prepare for this transition?" (Courses, projects, networking — show investment)
6. "Where do you see yourself in 2-3 years?" (Show commitment to this new path)
7. "Aren't you overqualified / won't you get bored?" (Reframe: experienced + motivated = asset)

---

### Week 13: Close Strong

**Final Week Tasks:**
- [ ] Follow up on all outstanding applications (7+ days with no response)
- [ ] Send thank-you notes within 24 hours of every interview
- [ ] Continue applying (don't stop until you have a signed offer)
- [ ] Negotiate any offers received (use market research from Phase 1)
- [ ] Inform your network of your availability (sometimes the best jobs come through connections)
- [ ] Celebrate your progress regardless of outcome (you've transformed in 90 days)

---

## Ongoing: Career Pivot Maintenance

After landing a role (or while continuing the search beyond 90 days):

- [ ] First 90 days in new role: learn voraciously, deliver early wins, build relationships
- [ ] Continue skill building (1 hr/week minimum in your new field)
- [ ] Maintain network connections (monthly check-ins with key people)
- [ ] Update portfolio with real work from new role (with permission)
- [ ] Document your transition story — it inspires others and builds your brand
- [ ] Pay it forward: help others making similar pivots (informational interviews, mentoring)
