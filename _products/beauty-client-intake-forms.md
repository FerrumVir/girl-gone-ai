## FORM 5: AFTERCARE INSTRUCTION TEMPLATES

### General Facial Aftercare

```
POST-FACIAL CARE INSTRUCTIONS

Thank you for your treatment today! Follow these guidelines
for best results:

FIRST 24 HOURS:
- Do NOT touch your face with unwashed hands
- Do NOT apply makeup for at least 12 hours
- Do NOT use active ingredients (retinol, AHAs, BHAs,
  vitamin C) for 48 hours
- Do NOT exercise or use sauna/steam for 24 hours
- Do NOT wax or thread for 48 hours

DO:
- Apply SPF 30+ before going outside (even if cloudy)
- Use gentle, fragrance-free cleanser
- Apply hydrating moisturizer as needed
- Drink extra water
- Sleep on a clean pillowcase

NORMAL REACTIONS (no concern needed):
- Mild redness (resolves within 2-6 hours)
- Slight tingling or warmth
- Minor breakouts within 48 hours (purging)
- Temporary dryness or flaking

CONTACT US IF YOU EXPERIENCE:
- Severe redness lasting more than 48 hours
- Blistering, oozing, or crusting
- Significant swelling
- Signs of infection (heat, pus, fever)
- Severe allergic reaction

Your next appointment is recommended in: _____ weeks
Rebook at: [booking link]
Questions? Text/call: [phone number]
```

### Chemical Peel Aftercare

```
POST-CHEMICAL PEEL CARE INSTRUCTIONS

Peel Type: ____________________  Date: ____________

DAY 1-2:
- Skin may feel tight, warm, or slightly stinging — NORMAL
- Cleanse with lukewarm water only (no cleanser day 1)
- Apply recovery balm/moisturizer provided
- NO actives, NO makeup, NO sun

DAY 3-5:
- Peeling/flaking may begin — DO NOT PICK OR PULL
- Continue gentle cleanser + moisturizer only
- Apply SPF 30+ (mineral preferred) every 2 hours if outside
- Skin may look patchy — this is normal

DAY 5-7:
- Most peeling should be complete
- Continue hydrating routine
- May resume mineral makeup if needed
- Still avoid actives

DAY 7-14:
- Resume normal routine GRADUALLY
- Reintroduce one active at a time
- Continue SPF diligently
- Schedule follow-up if needed

AVOID FOR FULL 14 DAYS:
[ ] Direct sun exposure
[ ] Retinoids/retinol
[ ] Glycolic acid / AHAs
[ ] Physical scrubs
[ ] Hot water on face
[ ] Swimming pools/hot tubs
[ ] Excessive sweating
[ ] Waxing/threading
```

### Lash Extension Aftercare

```
LASH EXTENSION AFTERCARE

Congratulations on your new lashes!

FIRST 24-48 HOURS:
- NO water on lashes (avoid shower stream, swimming, steam)
- NO touching, rubbing, or pulling
- NO eye makeup
- Sleep on your back if possible

DAILY CARE:
- Brush lashes gently with spoolie each morning
- Wash lash line with oil-free cleanser 2-3x per week
- Pat dry — never rub
- Avoid cotton pads near eyes (fibers catch on lashes)

AVOID ALWAYS:
- Oil-based products near eyes (cleansers, moisturizers, sunscreen)
- Waterproof mascara (will damage bonds)
- Eyelash curlers
- Rubbing eyes
- Pulling or picking at lashes
- Sleeping face-down

FILLS:
- Schedule every 2-3 weeks to maintain fullness
- After 4 weeks without a fill, a full set may be required
- If lashes feel uncomfortable at any point, contact us

WHEN TO CALL:
- Persistent itching, redness, or swelling
- Eyes feel gritty or painful
- Lashes growing into eyelid
- Any signs of allergic reaction

Next fill recommended: _______________
Book at: [booking link]
```

## QUICK-START GUIDE

### Step 1: Customize Forms
Replace [BUSINESS NAME], contact info, and cancellation policy details with your own. Add or remove allergy items based on products you use.

### Step 2: Print and Organize
Print New Client Intake + Allergy Screening + General Consent as a packet for all new clients. Print specialized consents only when needed for specific services.

### Step 3: Digital Option
Convert forms to a digital format (Google Forms, Typeform, or your booking software's intake form builder). Send the link with booking confirmation so clients complete forms before arrival.

### Step 4: File System
Create a physical or digital folder for each client containing: intake form, skin analysis, consent forms, progress tracker, and photos. Update after every visit.

### Step 5: Compliance
Review consent forms with a local attorney to ensure compliance with your state's regulations. Requirements vary by state and license type.
