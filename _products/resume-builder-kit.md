## ATS-Optimized Resume Templates

### How ATS Works (What You Need to Know)

Applicant Tracking Systems scan your resume before a human ever sees it. Here's what matters:

**ATS-Friendly Formatting Rules:**
- Use standard section headers (Experience, Education, Skills — not creative alternatives)
- No tables, columns, text boxes, or graphics (ATS can't read them)
- No headers/footers (ATS often skips them — your name and contact info would be lost)
- Standard fonts: Arial, Calibri, Garamond, Helvetica, Times New Roman
- File format: .docx (preferred) or .pdf (test first — some ATS struggle with PDF)
- No images, icons, logos, or photos
- Use standard bullet points (solid circle or dash)

## Cover Letter Templates

### Template 1: Standard Professional Cover Letter

```
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[City, State ZIP]
[Your Email]
[Your Phone]
[Date]

[Hiring Manager Name — research this!]
[Their Title]
[Company Name]
[Company Address]
[City, State ZIP]

Dear [Mr./Ms./Mx. Last Name],

[PARAGRAPH 1 — THE HOOK: Why you're writing and why you're interested in THIS specific role at THIS specific company. Show you've done research. 2-3 sentences.]

I'm writing to express my interest in the [JOB TITLE] position at [COMPANY]. After [HOW YOU FOUND IT: reading about your recent initiative in X / seeing the posting on Y / speaking with Z], I was immediately drawn to [SPECIFIC THING ABOUT THE COMPANY OR ROLE that genuinely excites you]. With [X years] of experience in [RELEVANT FIELD], I believe I can [SPECIFIC CONTRIBUTION you'd make].

[PARAGRAPH 2 — THE PROOF: Your most relevant experience matched to their top requirements. Be specific. Use one concrete example with a result. 3-4 sentences.]

In my current role at [COMPANY], I [SPECIFIC ACCOMPLISHMENT that directly relates to what they need]. For example, I [DID X] which resulted in [MEASURABLE OUTCOME]. This experience gave me deep expertise in [SKILL THEY'RE LOOKING FOR] and taught me [RELEVANT LESSON/APPROACH]. I'm confident this background translates directly to [SPECIFIC ASPECT OF THEIR ROLE].

[PARAGRAPH 3 — THE FIT: Why you want to work at THIS company specifically (not just any company). Connect your values/goals to theirs. 2-3 sentences.]

What draws me to [COMPANY] specifically is [GENUINE REASON: your mission, your approach to X, your reputation for Y, a specific product/initiative]. My own professional philosophy centers on [RELEVANT VALUE], and I see a strong alignment with how [COMPANY] approaches [TOPIC]. I'd be excited to contribute to [SPECIFIC GOAL OR PROJECT].

[PARAGRAPH 4 — THE CLOSE: Confidence + next step. 2 sentences.]

I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience in [KEY AREA] can contribute to [TEAM/COMPANY]'s goals. Thank you for your time — I look forward to hearing from you.

Sincerely,
[Your Full Name]
```

## Resume Audit Checklist

### Run this checklist on your resume before submitting ANY application.

---

**FORMAT & ATS COMPLIANCE**

- [ ] File is .docx or properly formatted .pdf
- [ ] No tables, text boxes, columns, or graphics
- [ ] No content in headers or footers
- [ ] Standard font (Arial, Calibri, Garamond, Times New Roman) at 10-12pt
- [ ] Standard section headers: Professional Summary, Experience, Education, Skills
- [ ] Consistent formatting throughout (same bullet style, same date format, same spacing)
- [ ] Contact info at the TOP of the document body (not in header)
- [ ] File name: "FirstName-LastName-Resume.docx" (not "resume-final-v3.docx")
- [ ] 1 page (0-10 years experience) or 2 pages max (10+ years)
- [ ] 0.5-1 inch margins, no smaller

**CONTENT QUALITY**

- [ ] Professional summary is tailored to THIS job (not generic)
- [ ] Every bullet starts with a strong action verb (not "Responsible for...")
- [ ] At least 60% of bullets include a quantified result (numbers, percentages, dollar amounts)
- [ ] No first-person pronouns (I, me, my)
- [ ] No paragraphs — bullets only in the experience section
- [ ] Each bullet is 1-2 lines (not 3+)
- [ ] Most recent/relevant role has 4-6 bullets; older roles have 3-4
- [ ] Achievements, not just responsibilities (what you accomplished, not just what you did)
- [ ] Keywords from the job posting appear naturally throughout

**KEYWORD OPTIMIZATION**

- [ ] Read the job posting 3 times and highlighted key terms
- [ ] Hard skills from the posting are mentioned in your Skills section
- [ ] Soft skills from the posting are demonstrated (not just listed) in your bullets
- [ ] Job title variations are included (e.g., both "Project Manager" and "PM")
- [ ] Industry-specific terminology is present
- [ ] Tool/software names match exactly (e.g., "Salesforce" not "CRM tool")

**RED FLAGS TO ELIMINATE**

- [ ] No typos or grammatical errors (have someone else proofread)
- [ ] No unexplained employment gaps (address briefly if >6 months)
- [ ] No outdated skills (remove anything irrelevant or from 10+ years ago)
- [ ] No personal info: age, marital status, photo, social security, full address
- [ ] No "References available upon request" (outdated — remove it)
- [ ] No generic objective statement ("Seeking a challenging role...")
- [ ] No salary information
- [ ] No reasons for leaving previous jobs
- [ ] No excessive jargon or acronyms without context

**IMPACT TEST**

- [ ] If someone read ONLY the first bullet of each job, would they be impressed?
- [ ] Can a stranger understand what you did and why it mattered in 10 seconds?
- [ ] Does your resume tell a clear story of career progression?
- [ ] Would you interview this person based on this document alone?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### The 15 Most Common Resume Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

**1. Starting bullets with "Responsible for..."**
Fix: Replace with an action verb. "Responsible for managing 5 accounts" becomes "Managed 5 enterprise accounts generating $1.2M annually."

**2. No quantified results**
Fix: Add numbers to at least 60% of bullets. Ask yourself: How many? How much? How fast? What percentage?

**3. Listing duties instead of achievements**
Fix: Use the formula: [Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [Result/Impact]. "Processed invoices" becomes "Processed 200+ invoices monthly with 99.7% accuracy, reducing payment delays by 3 days."

**4. One resume for every application**
Fix: Customize your summary, skills, and top bullets for each role. Mirror the job posting's language.

**5. Including irrelevant experience**
Fix: If it doesn't support your candidacy for THIS role, cut it or reduce it to one line.

**6. Too long (3+ pages)**
Fix: One page for under 10 years of experience. Two pages maximum for senior professionals. Be ruthless with editing.

**7. Fancy design that breaks ATS**
Fix: Use a clean, single-column format. Save creativity for your portfolio, not your resume.

**8. Unexplained gaps**
Fix: Briefly note what you did (freelance, education, caregiving, travel). One line is enough.

**9. Using an objective statement**
Fix: Replace with a Professional Summary that sells your value, not states your wants.

**10. Including "References available upon request"**
Fix: Delete it. Everyone knows you'll provide references when asked.

**11. Outdated contact info or a non-professional email**
Fix: Use firstname.lastname@gmail.com. Include LinkedIn. Remove your home address (city/state only).

**12. Inconsistent formatting**
Fix: Same font, same bullet style, same date format, same spacing everywhere. Inconsistency signals carelessness.

**13. Burying the best stuff**
Fix: Your strongest, most relevant achievement should be the first bullet under each role.

**14. Listing every skill you've ever touched**
Fix: Only include skills relevant to the target role AND that you could confidently discuss in an interview.

**15. Not proofreading**
Fix: Read it backward (catches typos your brain auto-corrects). Have two other people read it. Use Grammarly as a final check.
