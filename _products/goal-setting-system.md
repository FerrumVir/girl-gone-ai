# SECTION 2: 90-DAY GOAL FRAMEWORK

> **The Rules:**
> - Maximum 3 goals per 90-day cycle. Non-negotiable.
> - Each goal must be specific and measurable — "get healthier" is not a goal.
> - Each goal connects back to your North Star.
> - Each goal breaks into monthly milestones, weekly targets, and daily actions.

---

## Goal Setting Framework

### Why 90 Days?

90 days is long enough to build something real. Short enough to maintain urgency. It's 13 weeks — approximately one quarter. Long enough that compound progress shows. Short enough that you can see the finish line.

Annual goals fail because 12 months feels abstract. 90 days is concrete.

# APPENDIX: GOAL SETTING PHILOSOPHY

**Why 3 goals maximum?**
Focus is not a productivity hack — it's the entire strategy. Every goal you add after the first dilutes your attention and energy for all the others. Three goals pursued with full focus outperform ten goals pursued with divided attention. Every time.

**Why 90 days instead of a year?**
Annual goals exist outside of daily reality. 90 days creates urgency (you can see the end) without the sprint mentality of monthly goals. Progress compounds in 90-day cycles in a way it rarely does in unfocused years.

**Why an obstacle log?**
Most people experience the same obstacles repeatedly and handle them the same ineffective way each time. The obstacle log interrupts that pattern. When you write down what blocked you and what worked, you build a personal playbook. The fifth time you face that obstacle, you'll have data.

**Why celebrate?**
Because the brain remembers what it rewards. A meaningful end-of-cycle celebration creates a positive association with the hard work of goal pursuit. It's not indulgent. It's how you ensure you'll do it again.
