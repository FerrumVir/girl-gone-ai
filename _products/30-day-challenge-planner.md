---

>
> 1. Fill in the **Day 0: Launch Pad** section below
> 2. Choose your 3-5 habits in the **Habit Stacking Framework**

>

---

## The Rules of This Challenge

1. **Show up every day.** A 50% day counts. A 10% day counts. The only day that doesn't count is the one where you don't open this planner.
2. **Perfection is not the goal.** Consistency is. You will miss things. The system accounts for it.
3. **Weekly recalibration is mandatory.** Every 7 days, you review and adjust. The plan you start with is not the plan you finish with — and that's by design.
4. **Reward yourself.** The milestone rewards are not optional. You earned them. Take them.
5. **If you miss a day, come back the next day.** Do not restart. Do not skip to "catch up." Just do today.

# HABIT STACKING FRAMEWORK

> The most effective way to build new habits is to attach them to habits you already have.
>
> **Formula:** "After I [EXISTING HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."

---

# MINDSET JOURNAL — 30 DAILY PROMPTS

> Spend 5-10 minutes on the day's prompt. Write freely. No editing. No judgment.

---

**Day 1:** What does "transformation" mean to me? Not the Instagram version — my version. What would my life look like 30 days from now if this worked?

**Day 2:** What's the story I tell myself about why I can't change? Write it down. Then ask: is that a fact or a belief?

**Day 3:** Who is the person I'm becoming through this challenge? Describe them. How do they start their morning? What do they eat? How do they handle a hard day?

**Day 4:** What am I most afraid will happen if I actually succeed? (This one matters more than you think.)

**Day 5:** What are three things my body can do right now that I take for granted? Write a thank-you to your body.

**Day 6:** When I've quit challenges before, what was the trigger? What was happening on the day I stopped?

**Day 7:** It's been one week. What's surprised me? What's been harder than expected? What's been easier?

**Day 8:** Write about a time you did something hard and finished it. What carried you through? How can you apply that here?

**Day 9:** What's one excuse I've used this week? Is it a reason or a rationalization? What would I do differently?

**Day 10:** Progress check: What's different today vs. Day 1? List everything — even tiny things. Energy, mood, sleep, strength, confidence.

**Day 11:** If I could give advice to Day 1 Me, what would I say?

**Day 12:** What does discipline feel like in my body? Not the idea of discipline — the actual physical sensation of choosing the hard thing.

**Day 13:** Who in my life supports this transformation? Who makes it harder? What boundaries do I need?

**Day 14:** Halfway point. Write a letter to Day 30 Me. What do I want to tell future me about what this has been like?

**Day 15:** What habit am I most proud of building so far? Why is that one sticking?

**Day 16:** What would I do today if I weren't afraid of failing?

**Day 17:** Describe a setback from this week and how you handled it. What would the old you have done vs. what you actually did?

**Day 18:** What does "good enough" look like today? Not perfect — good enough.

**Day 19:** If this challenge were easy, everyone would do it. What's one hard thing you're doing that most people won't? Own it.

**Day 20:** Twenty days. Two-thirds done. What have I proven to myself?

**Day 21:** Three weeks of showing up. What's one habit that now feels automatic instead of forced?

**Day 22:** What would I tell a friend who's on Day 5 and wants to quit? Write the pep talk.

**Day 23:** What am I learning about myself that has nothing to do with the physical results?

**Day 24:** Visualize the last day of this challenge. What does Day 30 feel like? Where are you? What are you doing? How do you feel?

**Day 25:** What's one thing I want to continue after this 30 days is over? Why?

**Day 26:** What's one thing I'm ready to let go of? A belief, a habit, a fear, an excuse?

**Day 27:** If someone watched my last 27 days as a documentary, what would the narrator say about me?

**Day 28:** Write about the hardest day so far. What made it hard? What made you come back the next day?

**Day 29:** Tomorrow is the last day. What would make Day 30 feel complete?

**Day 30:** You finished. Write to yourself — freely, honestly. What happened over these 30 days? Who are you now vs. who you were? What comes next?
