## 30-Day Pre-Launch Checklist

### Week 4: Product Finalization (Days 30-22)

**Day 30:**
- [ ] Confirm product is 90%+ complete
- [ ] Define your launch date and put it on the calendar
- [ ] Write a one-sentence description of your product (the "elevator pitch")
- [ ] Identify your ideal buyer persona — who specifically is this for?

**Day 29:**
- [ ] Recruit 5-10 beta testers (friends, existing audience, community members)
- [ ] Create a shared folder or access link for beta testers
- [ ] Set up the beta tester feedback form (template below)

**Day 28:**
- [ ] Send product to beta testers with instructions and feedback deadline
- [ ] Set feedback deadline for Day 23 (give them 5 days)
- [ ] Start drafting your landing page copy (template below)

**Day 27:**
- [ ] Complete the pricing strategy worksheet (template below)
- [ ] Research 3-5 comparable products and their pricing
- [ ] Decide on your launch price and any launch discount

**Day 26:**
- [ ] Finalize your product name and tagline
- [ ] Create or commission product mockups/screenshots
- [ ] List all the platforms where you'll promote (email, Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.)

**Day 25:**
- [ ] Set up your sales platform (Gumroad, Shopify, Teachable, etc.)
- [ ] Upload a draft product listing
- [ ] Test the purchase flow yourself (use a test card or coupon)

**Day 24:**
- [ ] Write your FAQ (anticipate 5-6 questions buyers will ask)
- [ ] Collect any social proof: testimonials from beta testers, relevant credentials, or audience size stats
- [ ] Start outlining your 7-email launch sequence

**Day 23:**
- [ ] Beta tester feedback deadline — review all feedback
- [ ] Make critical fixes based on feedback
- [ ] Request testimonial quotes from beta testers who loved it

**Day 22:**
- [ ] Finalize product based on beta feedback
- [ ] Product is DONE. No more feature additions after today.
- [ ] Begin writing email launch sequence (template below)

---

### Week 3: Audience Warming (Days 21-15)

**Day 21:**
- [ ] Post teaser #1 on social media: hint at what's coming without revealing the product
- [ ] Example: "I've been working on something for the past [X weeks]. Launching soon. Stay tuned."
- [ ] Continue drafting email launch sequence

**Day 20:**
- [ ] Share a behind-the-scenes look at your product creation process
- [ ] Ask your audience a question related to the problem your product solves
- [ ] Finish drafting Email 1 (Announcement) and Email 2 (Value/Story)

**Day 19:**
- [ ] Post teaser #2: share a specific benefit or feature preview
- [ ] Example: "One thing I'm including in [Product Name]: [specific feature]. Here's why..."
- [ ] Finish drafting Emails 3-4

**Day 18:**
- [ ] Share a personal story about why you created this product
- [ ] Connect the story to the problem your audience faces
- [ ] Finish drafting Emails 5-7

**Day 17:**
- [ ] Post teaser #3: share a testimonial or beta tester quote
- [ ] Create a waitlist or "notify me" page if your platform supports it
- [ ] Customize all 20 social media templates (below)

**Day 16:**
- [ ] Finalize landing page copy
- [ ] Review landing page on mobile — ensure it looks good on all devices
- [ ] Set up payment processing and test the full purchase flow again

**Day 15:**
- [ ] Announce the launch date publicly: "Launching [Product Name] on [Date]"
- [ ] Share what the product is (no more teasing — give the full picture)
- [ ] Send a "save the date" email to your list (or include in your regular newsletter)

---

### Week 2: Landing Page & Email Prep (Days 14-8)

**Day 14:**
- [ ] Landing page is LIVE (even if hidden/unlisted for now)
- [ ] All email sequences are drafted and loaded into your email platform
- [ ] Schedule all launch emails with correct send times

**Day 13:**
- [ ] Schedule social media posts for launch week (use templates below)
- [ ] Prepare a "launch day" graphic or image for social media
- [ ] Write your launch day announcement post

**Day 12:**
- [ ] Proofread EVERYTHING: landing page, emails, product, social posts
- [ ] Have someone else proofread too (fresh eyes catch things you miss)
- [ ] Test all links: purchase link, download link, email links, social links

**Day 11:**
- [ ] Do a complete dry run: go through the entire purchase experience as a customer
- [ ] Verify: payment works, product delivery works, confirmation email sends, access is granted
- [ ] Fix any issues found

**Day 10:**
- [ ] Share another teaser or preview — show a specific section or feature of the product
- [ ] Engage with anyone who comments or asks questions
- [ ] Confirm all scheduled emails are correct and ready

**Day 9:**
- [ ] Post a countdown: "[X] days until [Product Name] launches"
- [ ] Share one more behind-the-scenes or "making of" post
- [ ] Prepare a list of people/communities to notify on launch day

**Day 8:**
- [ ] Final review of all launch materials
- [ ] Create a launch day operations checklist (template below) with specific times
- [ ] Set up your KPI tracker (template below)

---

### Week 1: Final Testing & Hype Building (Days 7-1)

**Day 7:**
- [ ] One week countdown post on social media
- [ ] Share a "sneak peek" of the product content
- [ ] Reach out to 5-10 people who might share your launch (collaborators, friends, supporters)

**Day 6:**
- [ ] Share a customer/beta tester success story or testimonial
- [ ] Respond to any questions or DMs about the product
- [ ] Review your launch day checklist one more time

**Day 5:**
- [ ] Post: "5 days until launch. Here's exactly what you'll get..."
- [ ] List the key deliverables/features in a simple, scannable format
- [ ] Confirm all tech is working (payment, delivery, emails, tracking)

**Day 4:**
- [ ] Share the "why" behind the product — your motivation, the problem that inspired it
- [ ] Engage authentically with your audience in comments and DMs
- [ ] Prep your launch day workspace: tabs open, copy ready, energy high

**Day 3:**
- [ ] 3-day countdown post
- [ ] Share a final testimonial or social proof element
- [ ] Last chance to fix any issues with product or landing page

**Day 2:**
- [ ] "Tomorrow is the day" post
- [ ] Send a reminder email to your list: "Launching tomorrow at [time]"
- [ ] Get a good night's sleep. Seriously.

**Day 1 (Launch Eve):**
- [ ] Final gut check: Is everything ready? Landing page, emails, social posts, payment, delivery?
- [ ] Set your alarm for launch time
- [ ] Trust the system. You've prepared for this.

**Price range:** $____ to $____

---

### Step 2: Value-Based Pricing

**What problem does your product solve?**
>

**How much is this problem costing your customer?** (in time, money, stress, or missed opportunities)
>

**What's the value of the transformation?** (If they implement everything in your product, what result do they get? What's that result worth?)
>

**Rule of thumb:** Price your product at 1/10th to 1/5th of the value it creates. If your product saves someone $1,000 or helps them earn $1,000, pricing at $100-200 is defensible.

## Beta Tester Feedback Form

### Instructions for Beta Testers

Thank you for testing [Product Name]! Your feedback directly shapes the final product. Please spend 10-15 minutes going through the product and answering these questions honestly. There are no wrong answers — critical feedback is the most valuable kind.

**Deadline:** [Date]

---

### Questions

**1. First Impressions (before diving deep)**

What was your first impression when you opened [Product Name]?
>

On a scale of 1-10, how professional/polished does it look? ___

**2. Content Quality**

Which section or component was the MOST valuable to you? Why?
>

Which section or component was the LEAST valuable? Why?
>

Was there anything confusing or unclear?
>

Was there anything missing that you expected to find?
>

**3. Usability**

How easy was it to navigate and use? (1-10) ___

Did you encounter any technical issues (broken links, formatting problems, missing files)?
>

How long did it take you to get value from the product? (e.g., "I started using the [template] within 10 minutes")
>

**4. Value Perception**

If you saw this product for sale, how much would you expect to pay? $___

At our planned price of $[Price], would you buy this? (Yes / Maybe / No)

If "Maybe" or "No," what would need to change?

## Launch Day Operations Checklist

### Pre-Launch (Morning, Before Going Live)

**2 hours before launch:**
- [ ] Final check: landing page is live and looks correct on desktop and mobile
- [ ] Final check: payment processing works (test purchase if possible)
- [ ] Final check: product delivery works (download link, access email, etc.)
- [ ] Final check: all email sequences are loaded and scheduled
- [ ] Final check: social media posts for today are scheduled or drafted
- [ ] Clear your calendar — minimize meetings and distractions today

**1 hour before launch:**
- [ ] Open all relevant tabs: email platform, sales platform, analytics, social media
- [ ] Set up your KPI tracker with today's column ready to fill
- [ ] Take a deep breath. You've prepared for this.

### Launch (Go Live)

**Launch time:**
- [ ] Make the landing page/product publicly available (remove any password or "hidden" setting)
- [ ] Send Email 1 (Announcement) to your list — or confirm it sent automatically
- [ ] Post launch announcement on primary social platform (Post 6)
- [ ] Share in any relevant communities, Slack groups, or Discord servers
- [ ] Update your social media bio with the product link

**1 hour after launch:**
- [ ] Check for any error reports or issues (email bounces, payment failures, delivery problems)
- [ ] Respond to any questions, comments, or DMs
- [ ] Post the "What's Inside" breakdown (Post 7)
- [ ] Record initial metrics in KPI tracker: page views, sales, revenue

**Midday:**
- [ ] Post the personal story (Post 8)
- [ ] Check email open rate and click rate for Email 1
- [ ] Respond to any new questions or messages
- [ ] Update KPI tracker

**Afternoon:**
- [ ] Post social proof (Post 9) — share any early feedback or testimonials
- [ ] Monitor for technical issues
- [ ] Thank anyone who shares or recommends your product

**End of Day:**
- [ ] Post the casual/vulnerable post (Post 10)
- [ ] Record end-of-day metrics: total page views, sales, revenue, email stats

- [ ] Celebrate. You launched a product today. That's a big deal.
