### Dashboard Formulas

**GCI Remaining to Goal**
= Annual GCI Goal − GCI Collected YTD

**Pipeline Value (GCI)**
= Sum of estimated commission on all deals currently under contract (before closing)

**List-to-Contract Rate**
= (Listings Gone Under Contract in Last 12 Months ÷ Total Listings Taken in Last 12 Months) × 100

**Show-to-Offer Rate**
= (Number of Offers Written ÷ Total Showings Conducted) × 100

**Lead-to-Client Rate**
= (New Clients Signed ÷ Total New Leads Contacted) × 100

# APPENDIX: KEY FORMULAS REFERENCE

> All formulas in one place. Use this section as a quick reference when you need to calculate or define a metric.

---

## Business Performance Formulas

**Gross Commission Income (GCI)**
= Sale Price × Commission Rate

**Agent Net Commission**
= Gross Commission − Referral Fees − Brokerage Split − Team Split − Transaction Fees

**Annual GCI Required**
= Desired Annual Income ÷ Agent Net Commission Rate
*(e.g., if you keep 60% after all splits and want $120,000 income, you need $200,000 GCI)*

**Transactions Needed to Hit GCI Goal**
= Annual GCI Goal ÷ Average Net Commission Per Transaction

**Monthly GCI Target**
= Annual GCI Goal ÷ 12

**YTD GCI Pace (Annualized)**
= (YTD Net GCI ÷ Months Elapsed) × 12

---

## Listing Performance Formulas

**Days on Market (DOM)**
= Closing Date − Original List Date
*(or Today − List Date for active listings)*

**Sale-to-List Price Ratio**
= (Sale Price ÷ Original List Price) × 100
*Above 100% = sold over asking. Below 95% = significant discount.*

**Average DOM (Portfolio)**
= Sum of all DOM values ÷ Number of listings

**List-to-Contract Rate**
= (Listings That Went Under Contract ÷ Total Listings Taken) × 100
*Industry benchmark: depends on market, but track yours over 12 months.*

**Price Reduction Rate**
= (Listings That Required a Price Reduction ÷ Total Listings Taken) × 100
*High rate signals pricing strategy needs adjustment at the listing appointment.*

---

## Buyer Performance Formulas

**Show-to-Offer Rate**
= (Offers Written ÷ Total Properties Shown) × 100
*A rate below 5% may signal misalignment on criteria or pricing.*

**Offer-to-Acceptance Rate**
= (Accepted Offers ÷ Total Offers Written) × 100
*Tracks your negotiation effectiveness and offer competitiveness.*

**Buyer Representation Conversion Rate**
= (Signed Buyer Agreements ÷ Buyer Consultations Conducted) × 100

---

## Open House Formulas

**Visitor-to-Lead Capture Rate**
= (Visitors Who Signed In ÷ Total Visitors Estimated) × 100

**Open House Lead-to-Client Rate**
= (Clients Signed from Open House Leads ÷ Total Open House Leads) × 100

**Open House ROI**
= Net GCI Traced to Open House Leads ÷ (Hours Invested × Hourly Rate Equivalent)

---

## Commission Split Formulas

**Standard Split (No Team)**
= Gross Commission × Agent Split %

**Team Agent Split**
= Gross Commission × Team Split % × Agent's Share of Team Split %

**Post-Cap Split**
= Once annual brokerage cap is reached, agent keeps 100% of gross commission minus per-transaction fees

**Referral Fee (Paid)**
= Gross Commission Side × Referral Fee %
*(Common referral fees: 20–35% of the receiving agent's commission side)*

**Effective Commission Rate**
= Agent Net Commission ÷ Sale Price × 100
*Tells you your real per-dollar yield on each transaction after all splits and fees.*

# QUICK-START GUIDE

---

## Get Running in Under 45 Minutes

---

### Step 1 — Fill in the Dashboard (5 minutes)

Open the Dashboard section. Enter your name, brokerage, the current year, and your annual GCI goal. If you don't have an exact goal number, use this benchmark:

- $2M–$5M volume / $50K–$120K GCI: You're an active agent building your business
- $5M–$15M volume / $120K–$350K GCI: You're a producing agent ready to systemize
- $15M+ volume / $350K+ GCI: You're running a real estate business, not just selling homes

Don't overthink it. Put a number in and adjust it in 30 days.

---

### Step 2 — Add Your Active Listings (10 minutes)

Go to Section 1. Add every listing you currently have active to the Active Listings table. Fill in address, list price, list date, and status. Then calculate the DOM column for each (today's date minus list date). You don't need to fill in the full Listing Detail Card for every listing today — add those one at a time over the next week as you review each property.

---

### Step 3 — Add Your Active Clients (10 minutes)

Go to Section 2. Add every active buyer and seller to their respective overview tables. Name, phone, budget/price range, and status. The full profile cards come later. Today you're just making sure no one is missing from your dashboard.

---

### Step 4 — Add Your Active Transactions (10 minutes)

Go to Section 3. Every deal currently under contract gets a row in the Active Transactions Overview. Then fill in the Critical Deadlines section for each deal — this is the most important 10 minutes you'll spend with this system. Missing a contingency deadline can cost your client a deal and expose you to liability.

---

### Step 5 — Calculate Your YTD GCI (5 minutes)

Go to Section 5. Open the Closed Transactions log in Section 3 and total your net commissions from closed deals this year. Enter that number in the Monthly GCI Log and the Dashboard. Now you know where you stand against your annual goal.

---

### Step 6 — Run Your First Weekly Review (5 minutes)

Go to the Weekly Agent Review section at the bottom. Fill it in for this week. This is the ritual that keeps the entire system alive — without the weekly review, the system becomes a record-keeping tool. With it, it becomes a business management tool.

---

### Ongoing Workflow

**Every time you take a new listing:** Create a Listing Detail Card in Section 1. Fill in the full profile. Set your price reduction conversation trigger (usually 14–21 days without a contract, depending on your market).

**Every time you take on a new buyer:** Create a Buyer Client Profile in Section 2. Fill in search criteria and financial profile before the first showing.

**Every time you get an accepted offer:** Create a Transaction Detail Card in Section 3 immediately. Fill in every deadline before you do anything else.

**Every open house:** Prep the Event Card 48 hours before. Work the follow-up log within 24 hours after.

**Every Monday:** Run the Weekly Review. 30 minutes. Non-negotiable.

**Every deal that closes:** Log it in the Closed Transactions archive in Section 3 and update the Monthly GCI Log in Section 5.

---

### Making It Yours

This template was built as a complete system, but you don't have to use every section at once. Start where the pain is highest — if you're struggling to track active transactions and deadlines, start with Section 3. If you've been losing buyer leads, start with Section 2. If you don't know your GCI year-to-date, start with Section 5.

Add rows to any table. Remove sections you don't use. Rename columns to match your brokerage's terminology. Duplicate the Event Card for every open house you hold. The system is designed to be edited, not preserved.

The only thing that doesn't work is not using it.
