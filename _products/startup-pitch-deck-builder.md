## 12-Slide Pitch Deck Template

### How to Use This Template
Replace all bracketed text `[like this]` with your company's information. Guidance notes appear in *italics* below each section — remove them before presenting. Each slide should take 30-60 seconds to present, keeping your full deck under 10 minutes.

---

### Slide 1: Title Slide

**[Company Name]**
[One-line description of what you do]

[Your Name], [Title]
[Contact Email] | [Website]
[Month Year]

*Guidance: Keep it clean. Logo, name, one-liner, your name. No paragraphs. The one-liner should pass the "mom test" — could someone outside your industry understand what you do?*

---

### Slide 2: Problem

**The Problem**

[Describe the problem in 2-3 sentences. Be specific. Use a real scenario or data point.]

- [Pain point 1 — what's broken, expensive, or slow]
- [Pain point 2 — who suffers and how often]
- [Pain point 3 — what happens if this isn't solved]

**[Stat or quote that makes the problem feel urgent]**

*Guidance: Lead with empathy. Investors need to FEEL the problem before they care about your solution. Avoid jargon. If possible, tell a brief story about a real person experiencing this pain. The stat should be from a credible source.*

---

### Slide 3: Solution

**Our Solution**

[Describe your solution in 2-3 sentences. Focus on the outcome, not the technology.]

**How It Works:**
1. [Step 1 — what the user does first]
2. [Step 2 — what happens next]
3. [Step 3 — the result they get]

*Guidance: Show, don't tell. If you have a product screenshot or demo GIF, this is where it goes. The "how it works" should be dead simple — three steps max. An investor should understand your solution in under 30 seconds.*

---

### Slide 4: Market Size

**Market Opportunity**

| Metric | Size | Source |
|--------|------|--------|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | $[X]B | [Source] |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | $[X]B | [Source] |
| **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | $[X]M | [Your calculation] |

**Key Market Trends:**
- [Trend 1 — why this market is growing]
- [Trend 2 — regulatory, behavioral, or tech tailwind]

*Guidance: Use a top-down AND bottom-up approach. TAM is the big dream number. SAM is the realistic segment you can reach. SOM is what you can capture in 3-5 years. Bottom-up example: "[X] potential customers x $[Y] annual contract value = $[Z]M SOM." Always cite sources for TAM/SAM.*

### Slide 5: Product / Demo

**The Product**

[Screenshot, mockup, or demo GIF here]

**Key Features:**
- [Feature 1] — [One-line benefit]
- [Feature 2] — [One-line benefit]
- [Feature 3] — [One-line benefit]

**What Makes Us Different:**
[One paragraph on your unique approach, technology, or insight]

*Guidance: This is the "show me" slide. A great screenshot is worth 100 words. If your product isn't built yet, show a high-fidelity mockup. Highlight 3 features max — the ones that make investors say "oh, that's clever."*

### Slide 6: Business Model

**How We Make Money**

**Revenue Model:** [Subscription / Transaction fee / Marketplace / Licensing / etc.]

| Pricing Tier | Price | Target Segment |
|-------------|-------|----------------|
| [Tier 1] | $[X]/mo | [Who it's for] |
| [Tier 2] | $[X]/mo | [Who it's for] |
| [Tier 3] | $[X]/mo | [Who it's for] |

**Unit Economics:**
- **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost): $[X]
- **LTV** (Lifetime Value): $[X]
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** [X]:1
- **Gross Margin:** [X]%
- **Payback Period:** [X] months

*Guidance: Investors want to see that you've thought about HOW you make money, not just how much. If you're pre-revenue, show your planned pricing with evidence it'll work (competitor pricing, willingness-to-pay surveys, beta feedback). LTV:CAC should be 3:1 or better for SaaS. If you don't have real CAC data yet, show your assumptions.*

**Key Metrics:**

- [Metric 2]: [Number] — [Growth rate or context]
- [Metric 3]: [Number] — [Growth rate or context]

**Milestones Achieved:**

*Guidance: This is where you prove you're not just an idea. Show your best growth metric in a chart — up and to the right. If you're pre-revenue, show user growth, waitlist signups, LOIs, pilot customers, or engagement metrics. For pre-seed, even "we talked to 100 potential customers and 80% said they'd pay" counts as traction.*

**Current Channels:**

- [Channel 2] — [How it works, current results]

**Planned Channels (Next 12 Months):**

**Customer Acquisition Funnel:**
1. **Awareness:** [How people find you]
2. **Interest:** [How you capture attention]

### Slide 9: Competitive Landscape

**Competitive Positioning**

| Feature / Attribute | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|---------------------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| [Key differentiator 1] | Yes | No | Partial | No |
| [Key differentiator 2] | Yes | Partial | No | Yes |
| [Key differentiator 3] | Yes | No | No | No |
| [Price point] | $[X] | $[X] | $[X] | $[X] |

**Our Unfair Advantage:**
[1-2 sentences on what makes you defensible — proprietary data, network effects, domain expertise, technical moat, regulatory advantage, etc.]

*Guidance: NEVER say "we have no competitors." Investors will stop listening. Alternatives exist even if direct competitors don't. Use a 2x2 matrix or comparison table — not a paragraph. Your unfair advantage should be something that gets stronger over time, not something a competitor could copy next month.*

**Financial Overview**

|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Revenue | $[X] | $[X] | $[X] |
| Gross Margin | [X]% | [X]% | [X]% |
| Customers | [X] | [X] | [X] |
| Burn Rate (monthly) | $[X] | $[X] | $[X] |
| Headcount | [X] | [X] | [X] |

**Key Assumptions:**

**What We're Raising**

**Round:** [Pre-Seed / Seed / Series A]
**Raising:** $[X] on a $[X] [valuation / SAFE / convertible note]

**Use of Funds:**
| Category | Allocation | What It Gets Us |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Product & Engineering | [X]% | [Specific milestone] |
| Sales & Marketing | [X]% | [Specific milestone] |
| Operations & Hiring | [X]% | [Specific milestone] |
| Buffer | [X]% | [Runway extension] |

**This Gets Us To:**
- [Key milestone 1 this funding enables]
- [Key milestone 2]
- [Key milestone 3]

**Runway:** [X] months at current burn rate

*Guidance: Be specific about the amount and terms. Show exactly how you'll spend the money and what milestones it'll unlock. "We're raising $1.5M to get to $100K MRR and 500 customers, positioning us for a Series A in 18 months." Investors want to know what the NEXT raise looks like and how this money gets you there.*

---

### Slide 13 (Appendix — Optional)

**Appendix**

Include any of the following as backup slides:
- Detailed financial model
- Additional market research
- Technical architecture diagram
- Customer testimonials or case studies
- Press coverage or awards
- Detailed competitive analysis
- Product roadmap
- Key patents or IP

*Guidance: Appendix slides are for Q&A, not for presenting. Have them ready but don't plan to show them unless asked. 5-10 appendix slides is normal.*

## Deck Review Checklist

Use this checklist before every pitch. Go through each item and check it off.

### Narrative & Story
- [ ] 1. The deck tells a clear story: Problem → Solution → Why Now → Why Us → Why You Should Invest
- [ ] 2. A non-technical person can understand the problem and solution in 30 seconds
- [ ] 3. The problem slide creates emotional urgency (not just intellectual interest)
- [ ] 4. The solution slide focuses on outcomes, not features
- [ ] 5. There's a clear "why now" — a market shift, technology change, or behavioral trend

### Data & Credibility
- [ ] 6. Market size numbers come from credible sources (cited)
- [ ] 7. TAM/SAM/SOM is calculated bottom-up, not just top-down
- [ ] 8. Traction metrics are current (updated within the last 30 days)
- [ ] 9. Financial projections have clearly stated assumptions
- [ ] 10. Competitive analysis is honest — you acknowledge real competitors

### The Ask
- [ ] 11. The raise amount is specific (not a range)
- [ ] 12. Use of funds is broken down by category with milestones
- [ ] 13. The funding gets you to a clear next milestone (not just "18 months of runway")
- [ ] 14. Terms are stated clearly (SAFE, priced round, valuation cap)
- [ ] 15. You can articulate what the next raise looks like

### Design & Readability
- [ ] 16. Total slide count is 10-15 (not counting appendix)
- [ ] 17. No slide has more than 30 words (excluding data tables)
- [ ] 18. Font size is 24pt+ for body text, 32pt+ for headers
- [ ] 19. Consistent color scheme throughout (2-3 colors max)
- [ ] 20. Charts and graphs are labeled and easy to read at a distance
- [ ] 21. No clip art, stock photos, or cheesy graphics
- [ ] 22. Company logo appears on every slide (subtle, in corner)

### Common Mistakes
- [ ] 23. No typos or grammatical errors (have someone else proofread)
- [ ] 24. No "we have no competitors" claim
- [ ] 25. No hockey stick projections without clear assumptions
- [ ] 26. No jargon that an investor outside your industry wouldn't understand
- [ ] 27. No slides that could be cut without losing information
- [ ] 28. No animations or slide transitions (they break when shared as PDF)

### Delivery Prep
- [ ] 29. You can present the full deck in under 10 minutes
- [ ] 30. You have answers prepared for the 10 most likely investor questions

## 50+ Talking Points by Slide

### Slide 1: Title Slide — Talking Points

1. "We're [Company Name], and we [one sentence on what you do] for [who you do it for]."
2. "The simplest way to describe what we do: [analogy]. Think of us as [known company] for [your market]."
3. "Before I walk you through the deck, here's the 10-second version: [problem] is costing [market] $[X] billion a year, and we've built a [solution type] that [key outcome]."
4. "We started [Company Name] [X months/years] ago because [personal connection to the problem]."

### Slide 2: Problem — Talking Points

5. "Here's the reality for [target customer]: [specific pain point with a real-world scenario]."
6. "The average [target customer] spends [X hours/dollars] per [time period] dealing with [problem], and the existing solutions are [inadequate because...]."
7. "We talked to over [X] potential customers before writing a single line of code. The number one thing we heard was [direct quote from customer research]."
8. "This isn't a nice-to-have problem. When [problem] goes unsolved, [consequence with dollar amount or scale]."
9. "The market has tried to solve this with [existing approach], but it fails because [specific limitation]."

### Slide 3: Solution — Talking Points

10. "Our solution is simple: [one sentence]. Here's how it works in practice."
11. "What used to take [X hours/days/dollars] now takes [X minutes/fraction of the cost]."
12. "Let me show you what this looks like for a real customer. [Walk through a use case.]"
13. "The key insight behind our approach is [unique insight]. Nobody else is doing it this way because [reason — technical moat, domain expertise, data access]."
14. "We didn't start with the technology and look for a problem. We started with [X] customer conversations and built exactly what they asked for."

### Slide 4: Market Size — Talking Points

15. "The total addressable market is $[X]B, but let me show you why we think the real opportunity is even bigger."
16. "Here's our bottom-up calculation: there are [X] potential customers in our initial segment, each paying $[X]/year, giving us a near-term SOM of $[X]M."
17. "This market is growing at [X]% CAGR driven by [trend 1] and [trend 2]."
18. "We're entering through [initial niche], which gives us a beachhead to expand into [adjacent market] and [adjacent market]."
19. "The timing is right because [market shift, regulatory change, technology enabler, behavioral trend]."

### Slide 5: Product / Demo — Talking Points

20. "Let me show you the product. This is what a customer sees on day one."
21. "The feature our customers love most is [feature], because it [specific outcome]."
22. "We built this to be ridiculously simple. Our average onboarding time is [X minutes], with no training required."
23. "What you're seeing is our [current version]. On the roadmap, we're building [next major feature] which will [unlock new capability or market]."
24. "The magic happens under the hood: [brief technical differentiator — AI model, algorithm, data pipeline, architecture decision]."

### Slide 6: Business Model — Talking Points

25. "We charge $[X]/month per [user/seat/transaction]. Here's why that price point works."
26. "Our gross margin is [X]%, which is [above/in-line with] SaaS benchmarks because [reason]."
27. "Customer acquisition cost is $[X], and lifetime value is $[X], giving us a [X]:1 LTV:CAC ratio."
28. "We've tested pricing with [X] customers and found the sweet spot at $[X]. Willingness to pay actually increased when we [pricing insight]."
29. "The beauty of our model is [network effect / platform dynamics / compounding data advantage] — it gets better and more defensible as we scale."

### Slide 7: Traction — Talking Points

30. "We launched [X months ago] and are at $[X] MRR, growing [X]% month-over-month."
31. "We've acquired [X] customers with [zero / minimal] paid marketing. This is almost entirely [organic / word-of-mouth / inbound]."
32. "Our churn rate is [X]%, which tells us customers are finding real value."
33. "Let me give you a concrete example: [Customer name or type] was [doing X before] and now [does Y with your product], saving them [quantified benefit]."
34. "We hit [milestone] faster than [comparable company] did at the same stage."
35. "Our NPS is [X], which puts us in the top [X]% of [industry] companies."

### Slide 8: Go-to-Market — Talking Points

36. "Our primary acquisition channel right now is [channel], and it's working because [reason with data]."
37. "Our go-to-market is founder-led sales today, but we're building the playbook to hand off to our first sales hire in [timeframe]."
38. "We've identified [X] channels to test. We've validated [channel 1] and [channel 2], and plan to add [channel 3] post-funding."
39. "Our ideal customer profile is [specific description], and we find them through [specific method]."
40. "The land-and-expand motion looks like this: we close a [small team / department], deliver results in [X weeks], and then expand to [full organization / additional teams]."

### Slide 9: Competitive Landscape — Talking Points

41. "Our main competitors are [A], [B], and [C]. Here's why we win."
42. "The current alternatives are [approach 1] and [approach 2], but both fail because [specific limitation]. We're the first to [unique approach]."
43. "We win on [dimension 1] and [dimension 2]. We're honest about the fact that [Competitor] is stronger on [dimension 3], but our customers tell us [dimensions 1 and 2] matter more."
44. "Our moat is [specific advantage: proprietary data, network effects, switching costs, regulatory compliance, technical IP]. Here's why it compounds over time."
45. "We're not trying to win the whole market on day one. We're dominating [niche] first, and then expanding from a position of strength."

### Slide 10: Team — Talking Points

46. "I've spent [X years] in [industry], specifically focused on [relevant area]. My co-founder [Name] brings [complementary skill] from [Company/background]."
47. "This is a team that's built for this specific problem. [Founder] came from [relevant company], where [he/she/they] [relevant achievement]."
