# Skincare Routine Builder — Client-Facing Template

> A complete routine-building system for estheticians to use with clients OR for individuals to build their own evidence-based skincare routine. Includes AM/PM routines by skin type, product layering guide, and ingredient compatibility chart.

---

## SECTION 1: AM ROUTINE TEMPLATES BY SKIN TYPE

### Normal Skin — Morning

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Example Ingredients | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | Gentle Cleanser | Remove overnight buildup | Glycerin, ceramides, gentle surfactants | Water rinse alone is fine if skin isn't oily in AM |
| 2 | Toner (optional) | Hydration, pH balance | Hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, rosewater | Skip if moisturizer is hydrating enough |
| 3 | Antioxidant Serum | Protect from environmental damage | Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid 10-20%), vitamin E, ferulic acid | Apply to damp skin for better absorption |
| 4 | Moisturizer | Lock in hydration, support barrier | Ceramides, squalane, peptides, hyaluronic acid | Medium weight — not too heavy, not too light |
| 5 | SPF (non-negotiable) | Prevent UV damage, aging, hyperpigmentation | Zinc oxide, titanium dioxide (mineral) OR avobenzone, octinoxate (chemical) | SPF 30 minimum. Reapply every 2 hours in sun |

### Dry Skin — Morning

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Example Ingredients | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | Cream/Oil Cleanser | Cleanse without stripping | Squalane, ceramides, oat extract, milky texture | Avoid foaming cleansers — too stripping |
| 2 | Hydrating Toner/Essence | Layer hydration | Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, beta-glucan, centella | Can apply 2-3 layers ("7-skin method") |
| 3 | Hydrating Serum | Deep hydration | Hyaluronic acid (multi-weight), B5, ceramides | Apply to damp skin — HA pulls water in |
| 4 | Facial Oil (optional) | Seal moisture, nourish | Rosehip, squalane, marula, jojoba | Mix into moisturizer or apply over it |
| 5 | Rich Moisturizer | Barrier protection, prevent TEWL | Shea butter, ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids | Cream texture, not gel |
| 6 | SPF (non-negotiable) | UV protection | Mineral SPF with moisturizing base preferred | Look for "hydrating" or "dewy" SPF formulas |

### Oily/Acne-Prone Skin — Morning

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Example Ingredients | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | Gel/Foam Cleanser | Remove excess oil, debris | Salicylic acid (0.5-2%), niacinamide, tea tree | Don't over-cleanse — stripping causes MORE oil |
| 2 | BHA Toner (optional) | Exfoliate inside pores | Salicylic acid, willow bark, niacinamide | Use 2-3x/week, not daily if new to actives |
| 3 | Niacinamide Serum | Oil control, pore refinement | Niacinamide 5-10%, zinc PCA | Reduces oil production over 4-8 weeks |
| 4 | Lightweight Moisturizer | Hydrate without clogging | Gel-cream texture, hyaluronic acid, aloe | YES you still need moisturizer even with oily skin |
| 5 | SPF (non-negotiable) | UV protection | Lightweight, matte-finish SPF | Look for "oil-free" or "mattifying" formulas |

### Combination Skin — Morning

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Example Ingredients | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | Gentle Gel Cleanser | Balance without over-drying | Amino acid surfactants, glycerin, centella | Foam just slightly — not stripping |
| 2 | Balancing Toner | Hydrate + refine | Niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, green tea | Good for all-over without irritation |
| 3 | Serum (targeted) | Address primary concern | Vitamin C (for glow) or niacinamide (for balance) | One serum — don't overcomplicate AM |
| 4 | Gel-Cream Moisturizer | Light hydration everywhere | Squalane, ceramides, lightweight texture | Apply more on dry areas, less on T-zone |
| 5 | SPF (non-negotiable) | UV protection | Hybrid formula (not too heavy, not too matte) | One SPF for whole face |

### Sensitive/Reactive Skin — Morning

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Example Ingredients | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | Cream Cleanser (ultra-gentle) | Cleanse without irritation | Oat extract, allantoin, ceramides, zero fragrance | Fragrance-free is NON-NEGOTIABLE |
| 2 | Calming Toner/Mist | Soothe, prep skin | Centella asiatica, aloe, chamomile, panthenol | Spray or pat on — no cotton pads (dragging irritates) |
| 3 | Barrier Repair Serum | Strengthen compromised barrier | Ceramides, fatty acids, cholesterol, niacinamide 2-5% | Focus on repair before treating other concerns |
| 4 | Simple Moisturizer | Protect and seal | Petrolatum (Vaseline), shea butter, squalane, minimal ingredients | Fewer ingredients = less chance of reaction |
| 5 | Mineral SPF | UV protection without irritation | Zinc oxide only (least likely to irritate) | Physical/mineral sunscreen — avoid chemical filters |

## SECTION 2: PM ROUTINE TEMPLATES BY SKIN TYPE

### Normal Skin — Evening

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Oil/Balm Cleanser | Dissolve makeup, SPF, oil-based debris | First cleanse — melts everything off |
| 2 | Water-Based Cleanser | Remove remaining residue | Second cleanse — fresh, clean canvas |
| 3 | Exfoliant (2-3x/week) | Cell turnover | Alternate: AHA one night, retinol another |
| 4 | Treatment Serum | Target concerns | Retinol (aging) OR vitamin C (brightening) OR peptides (firming) |
| 5 | Eye Cream | Hydrate delicate eye area | Peptides, caffeine, vitamin K |
| 6 | Moisturizer | Overnight repair and hydration | Can be slightly richer than AM version |
| 7 | Facial Oil (optional) | Seal everything in | Last step — oil goes OVER moisturizer |

### Dry Skin — Evening

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Oil/Balm Cleanser | Dissolve makeup + SPF | Rich, nourishing first cleanse |
| 2 | Cream Cleanser | Gentle second cleanse | Don't strip what little oil you have |
| 3 | Exfoliant (1-2x/week) | Gentle cell turnover | Lactic acid (hydrating AHA) or PHA (ultra-gentle) |
| 4 | Hydrating Serum | Layer moisture | Hyaluronic acid, squalane, peptides |
| 5 | Treatment (2-3x/week) | Retinoid for aging/texture | Start low-strength, buffer with moisturizer if needed |
| 6 | Rich Night Cream | Deep overnight repair | Shea butter, ceramides, oils |
| 7 | Facial Oil | Seal hydration | Rosehip, marula, or squalane |
| 8 | Sleeping Mask (1-2x/week) | Intense overnight hydration | Occlusive layer on top of everything |

### Oily/Acne-Prone Skin — Evening

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Oil Cleanser (yes, even for oily skin) | Dissolve SPF + sebum | Oil dissolves oil — this won't make you more oily |
| 2 | Gel Cleanser | Deep clean pores | Salicylic acid or niacinamide-based |
| 3 | Chemical Exfoliant (3-4x/week) | Unclog pores, smooth texture | BHA (salicylic acid) for inside pores, AHA for surface |
| 4 | Treatment Serum | Target breakouts or aging | Retinol (3-4x/week) OR azelaic acid OR benzoyl peroxide (spot) |
| 5 | Lightweight Moisturizer | Repair barrier, hydrate | Gel texture with niacinamide |
| 6 | Spot Treatment (as needed) | Target active breakouts | Benzoyl peroxide 2.5% or salicylic acid patch |

### Sensitive Skin — Evening

| Step | Product Type | Purpose | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Micellar Water or Balm Cleanser | Remove makeup without friction | No rubbing — press and hold, then wipe |
| 2 | Gentle Cream Cleanser | Mild second cleanse | Fragrance-free, minimal ingredients |
| 3 | (NO exfoliant until barrier is healthy) | — | Once stable: PHA or enzyme 1x/week maximum |
| 4 | Calming Serum | Reduce inflammation | Centella, azelaic acid (gentle), licorice root |
| 5 | Barrier Cream | Repair and protect overnight | Ceramides + cholesterol + fatty acids |
| 6 | Occlusive (optional) | Seal if very dry/damaged | Thin layer of Aquaphor or Vaseline on worst areas |

## SECTION 3: PRODUCT LAYERING GUIDE

### The Layering Rule

**General rule:** Apply products from thinnest to thickest consistency.

```
THINNEST → THICKEST

Toner/Essence (water-thin)
    ↓
Serum (lightweight liquid)
    ↓
Treatment/Active (varies)
    ↓
Eye Cream
    ↓
Moisturizer (cream/lotion)
    ↓
Facial Oil (if using)
    ↓
SPF (AM only — always last skincare step before makeup)
```

### Wait Times Between Steps

| Between... | Wait Time | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Cleanser → Toner | None | Apply to damp skin |
| Toner → Serum | 30-60 seconds | Let toner absorb |
| Serum → Serum (if layering) | 30-60 seconds | Prevent pilling |
| Serum → Moisturizer | 1-2 minutes | Let actives penetrate first |
| Vitamin C → Next step | 5-10 minutes ideal | Acidic pH needs time to work |
| Retinol → Moisturizer | 5-10 minutes ideal | Reduces irritation risk |
| AHA/BHA → Next step | 5-10 minutes | Low pH needs time to exfoliate |
| Moisturizer → SPF | 1-2 minutes | Let moisturizer set before SPF layer |
| SPF → Makeup | 10-15 minutes | SPF needs time to form protective film |

### When NOT to Layer (Conflicts)

| Do NOT use together | Why | What to do instead |
|--------------------|-----|-------------------|
| Vitamin C + Niacinamide (high concentrations) | Can cause flushing in some (debated but play safe) | Use one AM, one PM |
| Retinol + AHA/BHA | Over-exfoliation, irritation | Alternate nights |
| Retinol + Vitamin C | Both are strong actives, can destabilize each other | Vitamin C in AM, retinol in PM |
| Retinol + Benzoyl Peroxide | BP can deactivate retinol | Apply at different times or alternate |
| AHA/BHA + Vitamin C | Too much acid, pH conflict | Vitamin C in AM, acids in PM |
| Multiple acids (AHA + BHA + vitamin C same routine) | Over-exfoliation guaranteed | One acid per routine maximum |
| Retinol + Physical scrub | Thinned + irritated + scrubbing = damage | Never combine |

## SECTION 6: INGREDIENT NOTES & RULES

### Starting New Actives — The Rules

1. **Introduce ONE new product at a time** — Wait 2 weeks before adding another
2. **Patch test first** — Inside of wrist or behind ear for 24-48 hours
3. **Start slow** — New retinol? Use 1x/week for 2 weeks, then 2x, then 3x
4. **Buffer if irritated** — Apply moisturizer FIRST, then the active on top (reduces strength without eliminating benefit)
5. **Purging is real** — New retinoids and AHAs can cause breakouts for 4-6 weeks. If it gets worse after 8 weeks, stop.
6. **Irritation is NOT purging** — Redness, burning, peeling, or rash = stop immediately. Purging = breakouts in normal breakout areas only.

### Product Shelf Life

| Product Type | Opened Shelf Life | Signs It's Gone Bad |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Vitamin C serums | 3-6 months | Turned brown/orange, smells metallic |
| Retinol | 6-12 months | Changed color, smells off |
| SPF | 12 months (or expiration date) | Separated, changed texture |
| Cleansers | 12 months | Changed smell or texture |
| Moisturizers | 12 months | Separated, mold, off-smell |
| Hyaluronic acid | 12 months | Cloudy, particles floating |
| Face oils | 6-12 months | Smells rancid |
| Eye cream | 6-9 months | Changed color, smells off |

### When to Expect Results

| Active Ingredient | Noticeable Results | Full Results |
|------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Hyaluronic Acid | Immediately (surface hydration) | 2-4 weeks (deep hydration) |
| Niacinamide | 2-4 weeks (oil reduction) | 8-12 weeks (pore size, tone) |
| Vitamin C | 2-4 weeks (brightness) | 8-12 weeks (dark spots) |
| Retinol | 4-6 weeks (texture) | 12-24 weeks (wrinkles, acne clearing) |
| AHA/BHA | 1-2 weeks (smoother) | 4-8 weeks (significant improvement) |

## SECTION 7: SEASONAL ADJUSTMENTS

### Routine Modifications by Season

| Season | Swap This | For This | Why |
|--------|-----------|----------|-----|
| Summer | Rich cream moisturizer | Lightweight gel moisturizer | Less occlusion needed, humid weather |
| Summer | Heavy SPF | Lightweight/matte SPF | Less pilling under sweat |
| Summer | Thick cleansing balm | Foaming or gel cleanser | More sweat/oil to remove |
| Winter | Gel moisturizer | Rich cream with occlusives | Prevent transepidermal water loss |
| Winter | Foaming cleanser | Cream or oil cleanser | Less stripping in dry air |
| Winter | Daily AHA | 1-2x/week AHA | Skin barrier is more compromised |
| Winter | — | Add facial oil | Extra barrier protection |
| Spring | Heavier products | Medium-weight transition | Gradually lighten as humidity rises |
| Fall | Light products | Start adding richer layers | Prep for drier months ahead |

### Adjusting for Lifestyle Events

| Event | Adjustment | Timeline |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Beach vacation | Increase SPF to 50+, add antioxidant, simplify routine | Start 1 week before |
| Wedding/event | No new products, extra hydration, gentle only | 2 weeks before (NO new actives for 4 weeks) |
| Starting retinol | Reduce all other actives, increase moisturizer | 2 weeks adjustment period |
| Post-facial/peel | Gentle cleanser + moisturizer + SPF only | 3-7 days post-treatment |
| Stressful period | Simplify routine, focus on barrier support | Duration of stress |
| Pregnancy | Remove retinoids, high-dose salicylic acid, hydroquinone | Immediately upon knowing |

## QUICK-START GUIDE

### Step 1: Identify Your Skin Type
Use the descriptions in Section 1 to determine your primary skin type. If unsure, default to "sensitive" until you learn more.

### Step 2: Pick Your AM Routine
Choose the AM template for your skin type. You need a minimum of: cleanser, moisturizer, SPF. Everything else is beneficial but optional.

### Step 3: Pick Your PM Routine
Choose the PM template. Minimum: double cleanse (if wearing makeup/SPF), moisturizer. Add ONE active at a time.

### Step 4: Check Compatibility
Before adding actives, check the Compatibility Matrix to make sure you're not combining things that conflict.

### Step 5: Build Your Weekly Schedule
Use the Weekly Treatment Schedule to map which actives you use on which nights. Never use all actives every night.

### Step 6: Be Patient
Skincare is a long game. Give new products 6-12 weeks before judging. Take monthly progress photos in the same lighting.
