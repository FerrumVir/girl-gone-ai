# Daily Stoic Journal — Guided Journaling Template

> Make a copy of this document to start using it. A structured daily journaling practice rooted in Stoic philosophy with morning reflections, evening reviews, 30 curated quotes with exercises, weekly virtue focus, and monthly practices.

---

> **SETUP GUIDE — Start in 5 Minutes**
>
> 1. Create a new Google Doc or Sheets document (or print this template)
> 2. Use the Morning Reflection each day upon waking (5 minutes)
> 3. Use the Evening Review each night before sleep (5 minutes)
> 4. Follow the Weekly Virtue Focus to deepen one area per week
> 5. Complete the 30 Stoic Quotes & Exercises section over your first month
> 6. Return to the Gratitude & Memento Mori sections as ongoing practices
>
> **Tip:** Consistency matters more than length. Two honest sentences beat two perfect paragraphs you'll never write. Show up daily, even if brief.

# SECTION 3: 30 STOIC QUOTES WITH JOURNALING EXERCISES

> One quote per day for 30 days. Read the quote, sit with it, then complete the exercise.

---

## Week 1: Control & Acceptance

**Day 1 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 5.20**
"The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way."

*Exercise:* Name your biggest obstacle right now. Write 3 ways this obstacle could become your teacher or make you stronger.

---

**Day 2 — Epictetus, Enchiridion 1**
"Some things are within our power, while others are not. Within our power are opinion, motivation, desire, aversion — in a word, whatever is of our own doing."

*Exercise:* List 5 things causing you stress. Circle those you can actually control. Draw a line through those you cannot. Release the ones you crossed out.

---

**Day 3 ��� Seneca, Letters 13.4**
"We suffer more in imagination than in reality."

*Exercise:* What are you currently worrying about? Write the worst-case scenario. Then write the most likely scenario. Notice the gap between them.

---

**Day 4 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 4.3**
"People seek retreats for themselves — in the country, by the sea, in the mountains. But it is within your power to retire into yourself whenever you wish."

*Exercise:* Describe your inner retreat. When you close your eyes and breathe, what does your calm place look like? Practice going there for 2 minutes.

---

**Day 5 — Epictetus, Discourses 1.1**
"It is not events that disturb people, it is their judgments concerning them."

*Exercise:* Take a recent event that upset you. Separate the event (factual) from your judgment (interpretation). Rewrite your judgment in a neutral way.

---

**Day 6 — Seneca, On the Shortness of Life 1.3**
"It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste much of it."

*Exercise:* Audit yesterday. How many hours did you spend on things that truly matter? How many were wasted? What will you cut?

---

**Day 7 ��� Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 2.1**
"When you wake up in the morning, tell yourself: the people I deal with today will be meddling, ungrateful, arrogant, dishonest, jealous, and surly."

*Exercise:* Who is difficult in your life right now? Write down why they might be acting the way they are. Practice empathy without excusing behavior.

---

## Week 2: Virtue & Character

**Day 8 — Seneca, Letters 76.15**
"No man is good by accident. Virtue has to be learned."

*Exercise:* Which virtue do you most need to develop right now? Write 3 small daily actions that would build it.

---

**Day 9 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 6.6**
"The best revenge is not to be like your enemy."

*Exercise:* Think of someone who wronged you. Instead of wishing them harm, define who you want to be in response. Write that identity down.

---

**Day 10 — Epictetus, Discourses 2.18**
"No man is free who is not master of himself."

*Exercise:* Where do you lack self-mastery? (Food, phone, anger, procrastination?) Choose one area and write a 7-day challenge for yourself.

---

**Day 11 — Seneca, Letters 28.2**
"Wherever you go, there you are."

*Exercise:* Are you running from something by staying busy, moving, or changing circumstances? Name it. Then write what facing it would require.

---

**Day 12 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 8.47**
"If you are distressed by anything external, the pain is not due to the thing itself, but to your own estimate of it; and this you have the power to revoke at any moment."

*Exercise:* What external thing is causing you pain? Write the story you're telling yourself about it. Now rewrite the story from a position of power.

---

**Day 13 — Epictetus, Enchiridion 46**
"On no occasion call yourself a philosopher, and do not talk of principles among the uninstructed."

*Exercise:* Are you living your philosophy or just reading about it? Write 3 examples from this week where you lived your values, and 3 where you didn't.

---

**Day 14 — Seneca, Letters 6.1**
"I feel myself making progress. This is more than progress — I am being transformed."

*Exercise:* Compare who you are today to who you were one year ago. Name 3 specific ways you have grown. Acknowledge transformation.

---

## Week 3: Mortality & Perspective

**Day 15 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 2.4**
"Think of yourself as dead. You have lived your life. Now take what's left and live it properly."

*Exercise:* If today was your last day, what would you regret not having done? Write your top 3 regrets. Now — start one of them today.

---

**Day 16 — Seneca, On the Shortness of Life 3.2**
"You act like mortals in all that you fear, and like immortals in all that you desire."

*Exercise:* What are you postponing because you assume you have unlimited time? Write a deadline for starting that thing. Make it within 7 days.

---

**Day 17 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 4.48**
"Think of the life you have lived until now as over and, as a dead man, see what's left as a bonus."

*Exercise:* Everything from this moment forward is a bonus. How would you live differently if you truly believed this? Write your "bonus life" plan.

---

**Day 18 — Epictetus, Discourses 3.24**
"I must die. Must I then die lamenting? I must be imprisoned. Must I then also grieve?"

*Exercise:* What inevitable difficulty are you adding unnecessary suffering to? Separate the event from the suffering. Can you accept the event without the drama?

---

**Day 19 — Seneca, Letters 49.3**
"We are always getting ready to live, but never living."

*Exercise:* What conditions are you setting before you allow yourself to be happy? (When I get the job, when I lose weight, when I find a partner...) Can you be content now, before those conditions are met?

---

**Day 20 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 3.10**
"Waste no more time arguing about what a good man should be. Be one."

*Exercise:* Stop theorizing. Write one concrete action you will take today to be the person you aspire to be. Do it within the next 3 hours.

---

**Day 21 — Seneca, Letters 1.1**
"While we are postponing, life speeds by."

*Exercise:* What is one conversation you need to have that you keep delaying? Commit to having it within 48 hours. Write your opening words.

---

## Week 4: Action & Purpose

**Day 22 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 6.2**
"The things you think about determine the quality of your mind. Your soul takes on the color of your thoughts."

*Exercise:* What are your 5 most frequent thoughts? Are they serving you? Replace 2 negative recurring thoughts with intentional alternatives.

---

**Day 23 — Epictetus, Discourses 1.2**
"Circumstances don't make the man, they reveal him."

*Exercise:* Think of a recent challenge. What did it reveal about your character — both strengths and weaknesses? Write honestly.

---

**Day 24 — Seneca, Letters 71.3**
"If a man knows not to which port he sails, no wind is favorable."

*Exercise:* What is your purpose right now? (Not forever — just this season of life.) Write it in one sentence. Does today's schedule serve it?

---

**Day 25 ��� Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 5.16**
"The soul becomes dyed with the color of its thoughts."

*Exercise:* For the next 24 hours, notice every negative thought without acting on it. Simply observe. Tally them tonight. The number will surprise you.

---

**Day 26 — Epictetus, Enchiridion 5**
"Men are disturbed not by things, but by the views which they take of things."

*Exercise:* Take your most stressful situation and write 3 completely different interpretations of it. At least one must be positive. Which is most true?

---

**Day 27 — Seneca, On the Happy Life 7.3**
"True happiness is to enjoy the present, without anxious dependence upon the future."

*Exercise:* Put your phone away for 1 hour. Do one activity with complete presence — eating, walking, conversing. Write what you noticed.

---

**Day 28 — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 7.69**
"Perfection of character is this: to live each day as if it were your last, without frenzy, without apathy, without pretense."

*Exercise:* How would you act today — not with urgency, but with intention — if it were your last? What would you say to the people you love?

---

**Day 29 — Epictetus, Discourses 4.1**
"Freedom is the only worthy goal in life. It is won by disregarding things that lie beyond our control."

*Exercise:* What are you still trying to control that you cannot? Name it. Write a statement of release: "I accept that I cannot control ___ and I am free."

---

**Day 30 — Seneca, Letters 44.7**
"It is not the man who has too little that is poor, but the one who hankers after more."

*Exercise:* List everything you already have that others pray for. Practice feeling rich with what you have. Write a letter of gratitude to your life as it is today.

# SECTION 4: WEEKLY VIRTUE FOCUS

> The four Stoic virtues: Wisdom, Courage, Justice, Temperance. Focus on one each week.

---

## Four-Week Virtue Rotation

| Week | Virtue | Focus Area | Daily Practice |
|:----:|--------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Wisdom** (Sophia) | Clear thinking, good judgment, seeing reality | Before each decision: "Is this based on reason or emotion?" |
| 2 | **Courage** (Andreia) | Facing difficulty, doing what's right despite fear | Do one thing each day that makes you uncomfortable |
| 3 | **Justice** (Dikaiosyne) | Fairness, kindness, duty to others | Perform one act of service or fairness daily |
| 4 | **Temperance** (Sophrosyne) | Self-control, moderation, discipline | Practice saying no to one impulse each day |

# SECTION 5: GRATITUDE PRACTICE (STOIC STYLE)

> The Stoics practiced "negative visualization" — imagining loss to appreciate what remains. This is not pessimism; it is profound gratitude through awareness of impermanence.

---

## Daily Gratitude (Negative Visualization Method)

Instead of listing things you're grateful for, imagine them gone:

| What I Have | If This Were Gone Tomorrow... | My Gratitude Level (1-10) |
|-------------|-------------------------------|:-------------------------:|
| My health | I would give everything to feel normal again | /10 |
| My home | I would long for this roof and these walls | /10 |
| [Person's name] | I would ache for one more conversation | /10 |
| My work/income | I would worry about basic survival | /10 |
| Clean water | I would walk miles for what I turn a tap for | /10 |

---

## Weekly Gratitude Deeper Dive

| Week | Theme | Reflection Prompt |
|:----:|-------|:-----------------|
| 1 | People | Who enriches my life that I take for granted? Write them a message (even if you don't send it). |
| 2 | Body | What can my body do today that it might not always? Celebrate its function, not its appearance. |
| 3 | Past suffering | What past hardship made me stronger? Can I be grateful for the difficulty? |
| 4 | Simple things | Running water, literacy, freedom, a full stomach — what "basics" would devastate you to lose? |

# SECTION 6: MEMENTO MORI REFLECTION

> "Remember you will die." This is not morbid — it is the most life-affirming practice in Stoicism.

---

## Monthly Memento Mori Exercise

**Date:** ____________

### The Audit

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| If I die in one year, what would I regret not doing? | |
| Who would I want to have said "I love you" to? | |
| What would I want to have created or contributed? | |
| What petty conflicts would I wish I'd released? | |
| What fear would I wish I'd faced? | |

---

### The Action

Based on the above, choose ONE thing to do this month:

"Before my next Memento Mori review, I will: _______________________________________________"

---

### The Perspective

"I have lived _____ years. If I am fortunate, I may have _____ more. That is approximately _____ more Mondays. _____ more summers. _____ more conversations with [person]. This is not cause for despair — it is cause for full attention."

---

## Death Contemplation (Marcus Aurelius style)

"Consider that before long you will be nobody and nowhere, nor will any of the things exist that you now see, nor any of those who are now living. For all things are formed by nature to change and be turned and to perish, in order that other things in continuous succession may exist." — Meditations 12.21

*Weekly prompt:* What will matter about this week in 100 years? Focus only on that.
