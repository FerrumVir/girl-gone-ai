**AI Delegation Checklist — Ask yourself:**

- Is this task repetitive or template-based? → Delegate to AI

- Is it research or data-gathering? → 100% delegate to AI

## SECTION 2: Email Prompts (12 Templates)

### Professional Cold Outreach
```
Write a professional cold email to [person/company] about [topic].
Keep it under 150 words. Tone: confident but not pushy.
Include: 1 specific compliment about their work, 1 clear value proposition, 1 low-commitment CTA.
Do NOT use generic phrases like "I hope this email finds you well."
```

### Follow-Up Email (No Response)
```
Write a follow-up email. Context: I sent [original email topic] [X days] ago with no response.
Keep it under 100 words. Be helpful, not desperate.
Add new value (a relevant article, insight, or quick win for them).
End with a yes/no question to make replying easy.
```

### Difficult Conversation Email
```
Help me write an email about [sensitive topic] to [recipient + relationship].
My goal: [what you want to achieve].
Constraints: Must remain professional, preserve the relationship, and be direct without being aggressive.
Give me 2 versions: one more diplomatic, one more direct. I'll pick the right tone.
```

### Meeting Recap Email
```
Turn these meeting notes into a professional recap email:
[paste notes]

Format:
- 1-sentence summary of what was decided
- Bullet list of action items (who + what + by when)
- Next meeting date/agenda
- Keep it under 200 words
```

### Saying No Gracefully
```
Write a polite but firm "no" email to [person] who asked me to [request].
Reason I'm declining: [reason — or "I don't need to give a reason"].
Tone: warm but boundaried. No over-apologizing.
If appropriate, suggest an alternative or redirect.
```

### Client Update Email
```
Write a project update email to my client [name].
Project: [description]
Status: [on track / slightly delayed / ahead of schedule]
Completed since last update: [items]
Next steps: [items]
Any blockers or things I need from them: [items]
Tone: professional, proactive, reassuring.
```

### Thank You / Testimonial Request
```
Write a thank you email to [client/customer] who just [completed project/purchased/etc].
Then naturally transition to asking for a testimonial or review.
Make the ask feel easy — give them a template or 2-3 questions to answer.
Platform I want the review on: [Google/LinkedIn/specific site].
```

### Newsletter Welcome Email
```
Write a welcome email for new subscribers to my [topic] newsletter.
My brand voice: [casual/professional/playful/authoritative]
Include: what they'll get, how often, a quick win they can use TODAY.
End with a question to encourage replies (builds deliverability).
Keep it under 200 words.
```

### Apology / Mistake Email
```
Help me write an apology email for [what happened].
Recipient: [who]
What went wrong: [details]
How I'm fixing it: [solution]
Rules: Take ownership. No "sorry if you felt..." language. Be specific about the fix. Keep it brief.
```

### Price Increase Announcement
```
Write an email announcing a price increase for [product/service].
Old price: [X] → New price: [Y]
Effective date: [date]
Reason (optional): [expanded features / inflation / increased value]
Give existing customers [a grace period / locked rate / bonus] to soften it.
Tone: confident, not apologetic. Frame it as a reflection of increased value.
```

### Partnership Proposal
```
Write a partnership/collaboration proposal email to [person/brand].
My business: [what you do]
Their business: [what they do]
Proposed collaboration: [your idea]
What's in it for them: [specific benefits]
Keep it concise. Include 1 specific example of how this could work.
```

### Out of Office Auto-Reply
```
Write a professional out-of-office reply.
Dates I'm away: [dates]
Reason (optional): [vacation/conference/personal]
Emergency contact: [name + email OR "there is none"]
Tone: [warm/professional/playful]
Keep it under 75 words.
```

## SECTION 4: Research & Analysis Prompts (8 Templates)

### Deep Research Brief
```
Research [topic] and give me a comprehensive brief covering:
1. Current state (what's happening now)
2. Key players / thought leaders
3. Recent developments (last 6 months)
4. Opposing viewpoints or controversies
5. Data/statistics that support key claims
6. Gaps or opportunities I should know about
Format as an executive summary (500 words max) followed by detailed sections.
```

### Competitive Analysis
```
Help me analyze my competitor [name/URL].
My business: [what you do]
What I want to understand:
- Their positioning and messaging
- Their pricing strategy
- Their content strategy (topics, frequency, platforms)
- Their strengths I should learn from
- Their weaknesses I can exploit
- How I can differentiate
Base this on publicly available information only.
```

### Data Interpretation
```
Here's a dataset / set of numbers:
[paste data]

Help me understand:
1. What's the key trend or story in this data?
2. What are the outliers and why might they exist?
3. If I had to present this to [audience], what's the 1 headline takeaway?
4. What questions should I be asking next based on this data?
Present the insights in plain English, not jargon.
```

### Decision Framework
```
Help me make this decision: [describe the decision]
Option A: [description]
Option B: [description]
Option C (if applicable): [description]

My priorities (rank 1-5): [list what matters most — money, time, risk, growth, etc.]
My constraints: [budget, timeline, resources]

Create a weighted pros/cons matrix and give me your recommendation with reasoning.
```

### Book / Article Summary
```
Summarize [book title / article URL / topic] in this format:
1. One-sentence thesis (what's the core argument?)
2. 5 key ideas (1-2 sentences each)
3. 3 actionable takeaways I can implement this week
4. The strongest counter-argument or limitation
5. Who should read this (and who shouldn't)

### Market Research Brief
```
Help me understand the market for [product/service/niche].
I need:
- Target audience demographics and psychographics
- Market size estimate (if possible)
- Current trends driving demand
- Common objections or barriers to purchase
- Price sensitivity (what competitors charge)

```

### SWOT Analysis
```
Create a SWOT analysis for [my business/project/idea].
Context: [brief description]
Industry: [your industry]
Stage: [startup/growing/established]

Be brutally honest. For each quadrant:
- Strengths: things I genuinely do better than competitors
- Weaknesses: real vulnerabilities, not soft platitudes
- Opportunities: specific, actionable market gaps
- Threats: concrete risks, not vague "competition"

Then: give me 3 strategic priorities based on this analysis.
```

### Trend Forecasting
```
Based on current trends in [industry/niche], help me understand:
1. What's peaking right now (and may decline soon)?
2. What's emerging (early stage, high potential)?
3. What's stable (reliable, not going anywhere)?
4. What's dying (declining, don't invest here)?

For each trend you identify, rate it: hype vs. substance (1-10).
Give me 3 "bet on this" recommendations for the next 12 months.

## SECTION 7: 30-Day AI Adoption Plan

### Week 1: Foundation

**Day 1** — Sign up for ChatGPT (free) or Claude (free tier). Send 10 random questions to learn how it responds.

**Day 2** — Use AI to draft 3 emails you'd normally write from scratch. Compare time spent.

**Day 3** — Ask AI to summarize a long article or document you've been putting off reading.

**Day 4** — Use AI to brainstorm ideas for a project you're stuck on. Generate 20 options, pick 3.

**Day 5** — Create your first prompt template (pick one from Section 2 above). Save it somewhere accessible.

**Day 6** — Use AI to explain something complex in your industry in simple terms. Test it on a colleague.

**Day 7** — Weekly review: What saved you the most time? What felt awkward? What do you want to try next week?

### Week 2: Integration

**Day 8** — Identify your top 3 repetitive tasks. Write AI prompts for each one.

**Day 9** — Use AI for meeting prep: summarize a person/company before a call.

**Day 10** — Draft a full piece of content (blog post, newsletter, social post) with AI assisting 80%.

**Day 11** — Use AI to create a checklist or SOP for a process you repeat monthly.

**Day 12** — Try a second AI tool (if you started with ChatGPT, try Claude, or vice versa). Compare.

**Day 13** — Use AI to proofread and improve something you already wrote. Note what it catches.

**Day 14** — Weekly review: Calculate hours saved. Identify your #1 use case so far.

### Week 3: Optimization

**Day 15** — Build a "prompt library" — save your 10 best-performing prompts in a doc/note.

**Day 16** — Chain prompts: use output from one prompt as input to the next (research → outline → draft).

**Day 17** — Use AI to analyze data: paste a spreadsheet, ask for trends and insights.

**Day 18** — Experiment with AI for something creative: names, taglines, color palettes, gift ideas.

**Day 19** — Teach AI your brand voice (see Brand Voice prompt in Section 3). Test it on 3 pieces.

**Day 20** — Use AI to prepare for a difficult conversation (negotiation, feedback, pitch).

**Day 21** — Weekly review: Where is AI now a natural part of your workflow? Where does it still feel forced?

### Week 4: Mastery

**Day 22** — Build a custom GPT or save a system prompt that encodes your role, preferences, and context.

**Day 23** — Connect AI to another tool (Zapier, Make, or native integrations). Automate one workflow.

**Day 24** — Use AI to audit something: your website copy, your resume, your pricing page, your calendar.

**Day 25** — Train a colleague: show them your top 3 AI workflows. Teaching deepens your own skill.

**Day 26** — Use AI for strategic thinking: quarterly planning, goal-setting, decision-making.

**Day 27** — Explore an advanced feature: file uploads, image analysis, code generation, or API access.

**Day 28** — Full AI workday: use AI as your assistant for every possible task. Note where it helps vs. hinders.

**Day 29** — Build your "AI stack" — document which tools you use for what, and which prompts are essential.

**Day 30** — Final review: Calculate total hours saved this month. Set your AI goals for next month.

## Quick Reference: Prompt Engineering Tips

**The RACE Framework for Better Prompts:**

- **R**ole — Tell AI who to be ("Act as a senior marketing strategist...")
- **A**ction — Say exactly what to do ("Write a 500-word blog post...")
- **C**ontext — Give background ("My audience is small business owners who...")
- **E**xpectation — Define the output ("Format as bullet points, keep under 200 words")

**5 Ways to Improve Any Prompt:**
1. Add "Give me 3 options" — forces variety, you pick the best
2. Add "Ask me clarifying questions first" — AI will dig deeper before answering
3. Add "Explain your reasoning" — reveals logic you can correct
4. Specify what NOT to do — "Don't use jargon" / "No cliches" / "Don't be generic"
5. Give examples of what good looks like — "Here's a sample of the tone I want: [paste]"

**When AI Gives Bad Output:**

- Don't start over — say "That's too [generic/long/formal]. Try again but [specific fix]"
- Feed it your own writing as a reference: "Match this style: [paste example]"
- Break complex tasks into steps: "First do X, then do Y, then combine them"
