## 1. Marketing Copy Prompts

### 1.1 — Facebook/Instagram Ad Copy

```
You are an expert direct-response copywriter. Write 3 variations of a Facebook/Instagram ad for [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME].

Target audience: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL CUSTOMER — age, interests, pain points]
Product/Service: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF WHAT YOU SELL]
Key benefit: [THE #1 RESULT YOUR CUSTOMER GETS]
Price point: [PRICE OR PRICE RANGE]
Tone: [e.g., conversational, professional, edgy, friendly]

For each variation, include:
- A scroll-stopping hook (first line)
- 3-4 lines of body copy that agitate the problem and present the solution
- A clear call-to-action
- 2-3 suggested headline options for the ad creative

Format each variation with a header (Variation 1, 2, 3) and separate the hook, body, CTA, and headlines clearly.
```

### 1.2 — Google Ads Headlines & Descriptions

```
You are a Google Ads specialist. Generate 15 Google Search Ad headlines (max 30 characters each) and 4 descriptions (max 90 characters each) for [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME].

Business: [WHAT YOU DO]
Target keyword themes: [LIST 3-5 KEYWORDS YOU'RE TARGETING]
Unique selling proposition: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]
Target audience: [WHO YOU'RE TRYING TO REACH]

Requirements:
- Headlines must be under 30 characters (count carefully)
- Descriptions must be under 90 characters

- Include at least 2 headlines with a call-to-action

Present in a clean table format with character counts.
```

## 2. Business Strategy Prompts

### 2.1 — SWOT Analysis

```
You are a business strategy consultant. Conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis for [BUSINESS NAME].

Business: [DESCRIBE YOUR BUSINESS — what you sell, how you make money, current size]
Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY]
Main competitors: [LIST 2-4 COMPETITORS]
Current challenges: [WHAT YOU'RE STRUGGLING WITH]
Goals: [WHAT YOU'RE TRYING TO ACHIEVE IN THE NEXT 12 MONTHS]

Provide:
1. SWOT Matrix — organized in a 2x2 format with 5-7 bullet points per quadrant
2. Key Insights — 3-4 paragraphs analyzing the most important findings
3. Strategic Recommendations — 5 specific, actionable next steps based on the analysis, prioritized by impact
4. Risk Watch List — 3 threats that need monitoring with suggested tripwires (when to act)

Be specific, not generic. Reference real industry dynamics. Avoid vague statements like "strong brand" unless backed by reasoning.
```

### 2.2 — Competitive Landscape Report

```
You are a competitive intelligence analyst. Create a competitive landscape report for [BUSINESS NAME].

My business: [WHAT YOU DO, YOUR POSITIONING]
Competitors to analyze: [LIST 3-5 COMPETITORS WITH THEIR WEBSITES IF POSSIBLE]
Key comparison factors: [e.g., pricing, features, target market, brand positioning, distribution channels]

Provide:
1. Executive summary (1 paragraph)
2. Competitor profiles: For each competitor, include:
   - Overview (2-3 sentences)
   - Estimated positioning (premium, mid-market, budget)
   - Key strengths (3 bullets)
   - Key weaknesses (3 bullets)
   - Notable recent moves (product launches, funding, partnerships)
3. Comparison matrix: Table comparing all competitors across [KEY FACTORS] — rate each 1-5
4. Gap analysis: Where is there white space in the market that no competitor owns?
5. Strategic implications: 3-5 recommendations for how to position against this competitive set

Note: Base analysis on generally known information about these companies. Flag any areas where you're making assumptions.
```

### 2.5 — OKR Generator

```

  - Current baseline (placeholder for me to fill in)

### 2.8 — Risk Assessment Matrix

```
You are a risk management consultant. Create a risk assessment matrix for [BUSINESS NAME / PROJECT NAME].

Context: [DESCRIBE THE BUSINESS SITUATION, PROJECT, OR INITIATIVE]
Stakeholders: [WHO IS AFFECTED]
Timeline: [RELEVANT TIMEFRAME]
Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY]

   - Technology (2-3 risks)

4. Mitigation plans: For each Critical and High risk, provide:
   - Prevention strategy
   - Detection method
   - Response plan
   - Risk owner (role)
5. Monitoring cadence: How often to review each risk tier
```

```
You are an HR specialist and employer branding expert. Write a compelling job description for a [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY NAME].

Nice-to-have skills: [LIST 3-5 PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS]

Write the job description with these sections:

## Tips for Getting the Best Results

1. **Be specific with variables.** "Small business owners" is okay. "Female entrepreneurs running e-commerce businesses doing $10K-$50K/month in revenue" is much better.

2. **Iterate on outputs.** If the first result is 80% right, tell the AI what to change: "Make the tone more casual," "Add more specific numbers," "Shorten the introduction."

3. **Chain prompts together.** Use the output of one prompt as input for another. Example: Generate a SWOT analysis, then use the findings in a business plan prompt.

4. **Save your best outputs as examples.** When you get a result you love, save it and reference it: "Write something similar in tone and structure to this: [paste example]."

5. **Update the variables for each use case.** The same prompt structure works across industries — just change the specifics.
