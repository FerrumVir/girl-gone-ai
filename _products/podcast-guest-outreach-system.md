
### Guest Research Checklist
```
Before reaching out, gather this information:

- [ ] Full name and title
- [ ] Company/brand/project they're known for
- [ ] Their primary platform (where they're most active)
- [ ] Their website and contact/booking info
- [ ] Social media handles (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram)
- [ ] Previous podcast appearances (listen to at least 1)
- [ ] Their latest content/projects (what are they promoting now?)
- [ ] Shared connections (mutual follows, mutual guests)
- [ ] Their communication style (formal? casual? humor-driven?)
- [ ] Topics they're passionate about (from social media posts)
- [ ] Topics they HAVEN'T been asked about yet (your unique angle)
- [ ] Best way to reach them (email? DM? agent/PR?)

NOTES FROM THEIR PREVIOUS PODCAST APPEARANCE:
Episode listened to: [NAME OF SHOW + EPISODE]
Key stories/points they made: [LIST]
What the host did well: [NOTE — so you can do it too]
What was missing that you could explore: [YOUR UNIQUE ANGLE]
```

## SECTION 3: FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCES

### Follow-Up Timeline
```
Day 0: Initial pitch sent
Day 4: Follow-up #1 (gentle reminder)
Day 10: Follow-up #2 (add value)
Day 21: Follow-up #3 (last attempt)
Day 30+: Move to "Circle Back Later" list (3 months)
```

### Follow-Up Email #1 (Day 4)
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL SUBJECT]

Hi [NAME],

Just floating this back to the top of your inbox — I know it's easy to miss.

Would love to have you on [SHOW NAME] to discuss [TOPIC]. Happy to make it as easy as possible (I handle all production, you just show up and talk).

Let me know if you're interested or if there's a better time to revisit this.

[YOUR NAME]
```

### Follow-Up Email #2 (Day 10)
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL SUBJECT]

Hey [NAME],

Wanted to add some context in case it helps:

[CHOOSE ONE:]
- Our episode with [SIMILAR GUEST] just hit [DOWNLOADS] — proof the audience is hungry for this topic.
- I just published a related episode on [TOPIC] and the #1 comment was asking for more on [THEIR EXPERTISE].
- I saw your recent [POST/ARTICLE] and it reinforced why I think this conversation would be gold.

Totally understand if the timing isn't right — but if you're open to it, I'd love to make this happen.

[YOUR NAME]
```

### Follow-Up Email #3 (Day 21 — Final)
```
Subject: Last one from me

Hi [NAME],

I'll keep this short — this is my last follow-up on the podcast invitation.

If you're interested in being a guest on [SHOW NAME] in the future, just reply "later" and I'll circle back in a few months.

If it's a no, totally fine too — no hard feelings.

Either way, keep doing great work. I'll be following along.

[YOUR NAME]
```

## SECTION 4: INTERVIEW PREP SHEETS

### Pre-Interview Prep Document (Send to Guest)
```
─────────────────────────────────────
EPISODE PREP SHEET
─────────────────────────────────────

SHOW: [SHOW NAME]
HOST: [YOUR NAME]
EPISODE TOPIC: [TOPIC]
RECORDING DATE: [DATE/TIME + TIMEZONE]
FORMAT: [AUDIO ONLY / VIDEO + AUDIO]
LENGTH: [TARGET MINUTES]
RECORDING PLATFORM: [ZOOM/RIVERSIDE/SQUADCAST — INCLUDE LINK]

TECH REQUIREMENTS:
- Quiet room (no echo, no background noise)
- Wired headphones preferred (AirPods are OK, speaker is not)
- Stable internet connection
- If video: good lighting on face (window or desk lamp in front of you)
- Close unnecessary tabs/applications
- Phone on silent

WHAT TO EXPECT:
- We'll chat informally for 2-3 minutes before recording
- I'll give a brief intro, then ask my first question
- Conversation style — not scripted, but I'll guide the flow
- If you want to redo an answer, just say "let me redo that" and we'll edit
- No trick questions — this is meant to make you look great

TOPICS WE'LL COVER (roughly in order):
1. [TOPIC/QUESTION AREA 1]
2. [TOPIC/QUESTION AREA 2]
3. [TOPIC/QUESTION AREA 3]
4. [TOPIC/QUESTION AREA 4]
5. [RAPID FIRE / CLOSING QUESTIONS]

WHAT I WON'T ASK WITHOUT WARNING:
[ANYTHING OFF-LIMITS OR ANYTHING YOU'D WARN ABOUT]

YOUR CTA:
At the end, I'll ask "where can people find you?" — have your preferred link/handle/offer ready.

ANYTHING ELSE?
Reply to this email if you have questions or want to adjust anything. Looking forward to it!
```

### Host Interview Question Sheet
```
─────────────────────────────────────
INTERVIEW GUIDE — [GUEST NAME]
─────────────────────────────────────
Episode: [WORKING TITLE]
Date: [DATE]
Guest: [NAME] — [TITLE/COMPANY]
Research notes: [KEY FACTS FROM YOUR RESEARCH]

PRE-RECORDING NOTES:
- Pronunciation of their name: [PHONETIC IF NEEDED]
- Don't ask about: [ANYTHING OFF-LIMITS]
- Do ask about: [THEIR PASSION TOPICS]
- What they're promoting: [THEIR CURRENT THING — give them a chance to mention it]

INTRO SCRIPT:
"Today I'm joined by [NAME], who [1-2 SENTENCE BIO]. I'm excited about this conversation because [WHY THIS TOPIC MATTERS TO YOUR AUDIENCE]. [NAME], welcome to the show."

OPENING QUESTION (warm-up):
"For anyone who doesn't know you yet, give us the 60-second version of who you are and what you're working on right now."

CORE QUESTIONS:
1. [QUESTION] — [YOUR NOTE: what you're hoping they'll say / where this should lead]
2. [QUESTION] — [NOTE]
3. [QUESTION] — [NOTE]
4. [QUESTION] — [NOTE]
5. [QUESTION] — [NOTE]
6. [QUESTION] — [NOTE]
7. [QUESTION] — [NOTE]
8. [QUESTION] — [NOTE]

FOLLOW-UP PROMPTS (use when an answer is interesting but needs more):
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "What did that actually look like day-to-day?"
- "If someone's listening and wants to do the same, what's step one?"
- "What's the mistake most people make with that?"
- "What would you do differently if you started over?"

CLOSING QUESTIONS:
- "What's one thing you wish more people understood about [TOPIC]?"
- "If someone takes away just one thing from this conversation, what should it be?"
- "Where can people find you and what are you working on that they should check out?"

SIGN-OFF SCRIPT:
"[NAME], this was incredible. Thank you for your time and generosity. Everyone listening — links to everything we discussed are in the show notes. And [NAME], genuinely, thanks for being here."
```

### Monthly Outreach Goals
```
WEEKLY TARGETS:
- Research 5 new potential guests
- Send 3-5 new pitch emails
- Follow up on all pending pitches
- Book 2-4 recordings per month (adjust to your publishing frequency)
- Send thank-you + assets on publish day

CONVERSION BENCHMARKS:
- Pitch-to-response rate: 30-50% (healthy for warm outreach)
- Response-to-booking rate: 50-70%
- Overall pitch-to-booking: 15-35%
- If below these: improve pitch personalization or target more accessible guests
```

## SECTION 7: OUTREACH BEST PRACTICES

### Do's
```
- Personalize every pitch (reference specific work they've done)
- Listen to at least one of their previous podcast appearances before pitching
- Make the pitch about THEM and THEIR audience, not just your show
- Include your show stats upfront (downloads, audience, notable guests)
- Make it easy to say yes (clear next step, flexible scheduling)
- Follow up (most bookings come from follow-up #1 or #2, not the initial email)
- Send episode assets (clips, graphics) so sharing is effortless for them
- Respect "no" — gracefully. They might say yes later.
```

### Don'ts
```
- Don't send generic mass emails (people can tell)
- Don't lead with "I'd love to have you on my podcast" without context
- Don't pitch without stats or credibility markers
- Don't book a guest you haven't researched (makes for bad interviews)
- Don't skip the pre-interview prep doc (prevents awkward surprises)
- Don't delay publishing (guests lose enthusiasm if they wait 3 months)
- Don't forget to promote THEIR work, not just yours, on publish day
- Don't burn bridges — the podcast world is small
```
