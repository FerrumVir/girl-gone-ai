# TEMPLATE 1: MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT (MSA)

> GUIDANCE: The MSA is your foundational contract. It covers the ongoing business relationship between you and a client. Attach project-specific Scope of Work documents as addenda for each engagement. This means you only negotiate the MSA once per client.

---

## MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Between:**

**Service Provider:**
[YOUR FULL LEGAL NAME / BUSINESS NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
[YOUR PHONE]

**Client:**
[CLIENT FULL LEGAL NAME / BUSINESS NAME]
[CLIENT ADDRESS]
[CLIENT EMAIL]
[CLIENT PHONE]

---

### 1. RELATIONSHIP OF THE PARTIES

The Service Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employer-employee relationship, partnership, or joint venture between the parties. The Service Provider retains full control over the methods, timing, and tools used to perform the work, subject to the deliverables and deadlines defined in each Scope of Work.

> GUIDANCE: This clause establishes you as an independent contractor, not an employee. This is critical for tax purposes and liability protection. Do not remove it.

---

### 2. SCOPE OF SERVICES

The Service Provider will perform services as described in one or more Scope of Work documents ("SOW") attached to this Agreement as addenda. Each SOW will define the specific deliverables, timeline, fees, and payment terms for that engagement.

No work will begin on any project until the corresponding SOW is signed by both parties.

> GUIDANCE: This keeps the MSA flexible. You can add new projects by attaching new SOWs without renegotiating the master agreement.

---

### 3. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

3.1 **Fees.** The Client agrees to pay the Service Provider the fees specified in each SOW.

3.2 **Payment Terms.** Unless otherwise specified in a SOW, all invoices are due within [15 / 30] calendar days of the invoice date.

3.3 **Late Payment.** Invoices unpaid after the due date will incur a late fee of [1.5]% per month (or the maximum permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower). The Service Provider reserves the right to pause all work on any active project until overdue invoices are paid in full.

3.4 **Expenses.** Pre-approved expenses incurred by the Service Provider on behalf of the Client will be reimbursed at cost within [15] days of submission with receipts.

> GUIDANCE: The late payment clause is your leverage. Most freelancers don't include one, and that's why clients pay them last. The work-pause clause is equally important — it gives you the right to stop working without breaching the contract if they don't pay.

---

### 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 **Ownership Transfer.** Upon receipt of full and final payment for a completed project, the Service Provider assigns to the Client all rights, title, and interest in the final deliverables as defined in the applicable SOW.

4.2 **Pre-Existing IP.** Any tools, frameworks, code libraries, methodologies, or other intellectual property owned by the Service Provider prior to or independent of this engagement ("Pre-Existing IP") remain the property of the Service Provider. The Client receives a non-exclusive, perpetual license to use Pre-Existing IP solely as embedded in the final deliverables.

4.3 **Portfolio Rights.** The Service Provider retains the right to display the work in their portfolio, website, case studies, and social media unless explicitly restricted in writing by the Client.

4.4 **No Transfer Before Payment.** For the avoidance of doubt, no intellectual property rights transfer to the Client until all associated invoices are paid in full.

> GUIDANCE: Clause 4.4 is your most important protection. If a client doesn't pay, they don't own the work. This gives you real leverage in payment disputes.

---

### 5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Each party agrees to keep confidential any proprietary or non-public information disclosed by the other party during the course of this engagement ("Confidential Information").

5.2 Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party; (b) was known to the receiving party before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without reference to the Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law.

5.3 This confidentiality obligation survives termination of this Agreement for a period of [2] years.

> GUIDANCE: If the client requires a more comprehensive NDA, use the standalone NDA template in this kit instead of or in addition to this section.

---

### 6. WARRANTIES AND LIABILITY

6.1 **Service Provider Warranty.** The Service Provider warrants that the deliverables will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner and will materially conform to the specifications in the applicable SOW.

6.2 **Limitation of Liability.** The Service Provider's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Client under the applicable SOW. In no event shall the Service Provider be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages.

6.3 **Client Warranty.** The Client warrants that any materials, content, or information provided to the Service Provider for use in the project do not infringe on any third-party intellectual property rights.

> GUIDANCE: The limitation of liability caps your exposure at the project fee. This is standard in professional services agreements and protects you from disproportionate claims.

---

### 7. TERMINATION

7.1 **Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement with [30] days' written notice. The Client will pay for all work completed through the termination date.

7.2 **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure the breach within [15] days of written notice.

7.3 **Kill Fee.** If the Client terminates a project after work has begun, the applicable kill fee as defined in the SOW shall apply. If no kill fee is specified, the Client shall pay for all work completed through the termination date plus [25]% of the remaining project fee.

7.4 **Survival.** Sections 4 (Intellectual Property), 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Warranties and Liability), and this Section 7 survive termination of this Agreement.

> GUIDANCE: The kill fee clause protects you from clients who cancel after you've turned down other work or committed time to their project. Adjust the percentage based on your industry and project size.

---

### 8. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

8.1 The parties agree to attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith negotiation for a period of [30] days before pursuing formal resolution.

8.2 Any unresolved dispute shall be resolved by [binding arbitration / mediation] in [YOUR CITY/STATE/JURISDICTION] under the rules of [APPLICABLE ARBITRATION BODY, e.g., the American Arbitration Association].

8.3 The prevailing party in any dispute shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

> GUIDANCE: Arbitration is typically faster and cheaper than litigation. The attorney's fees clause discourages frivolous disputes. Choose a jurisdiction in your state/country.

---

### 9. GENERAL PROVISIONS

9.1 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with all attached SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

9.2 **Amendments.** This Agreement may only be modified by written agreement signed by both parties.

9.3 **Severability.** If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.

9.4 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of [YOUR STATE/COUNTRY].

# TEMPLATE 2: PROJECT CONTRACT — FIXED PRICE

> GUIDANCE: Use this for standalone projects with a defined deliverable and a fixed fee. For ongoing retainer work, use Template 3 instead.

---

## FIXED-PRICE PROJECT AGREEMENT

**Project Name:** [PROJECT NAME]
**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Service Provider:** [YOUR NAME / BUSINESS NAME]
**Client:** [CLIENT NAME / BUSINESS NAME]

---

### 1. PROJECT SCOPE

The Service Provider agrees to provide the following deliverables:

| # | Deliverable | Description | Due Date |
|---|-------------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | [e.g., Homepage Design] | [Brief description of what's included] | [Date] |
| 2 | [e.g., Interior Page Templates x5] | [Brief description] | [Date] |
| 3 | [e.g., Mobile Responsive Build] | [Brief description] | [Date] |
| 4 | [e.g., Final Handoff Package] | [Brief description] | [Date] |

**Included:** [List what IS included — e.g., 2 rounds of revisions per deliverable, source files, 30-day post-launch support]

**Excluded:** [List what is NOT included — e.g., stock photography, copywriting, hosting setup, ongoing maintenance]

> GUIDANCE: The "Excluded" section is as important as the "Included" section. Being explicit about what's not covered prevents 90% of scope creep arguments.

---

### 2. TIMELINE

**Project Start Date:** [DATE]
**Estimated Completion Date:** [DATE]
**Total Duration:** [X] weeks

| Phase | Description | Duration | Start | End |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------|-----|
| Discovery | Kickoff call, brief review, research | [X] days | [Date] | [Date] |
| Design | Concepts, revisions, final approval | [X] days | [Date] | [Date] |
| Build | Development, testing, QA | [X] days | [Date] | [Date] |
| Delivery | Final review, handoff, training | [X] days | [Date] | [Date] |

**Client Feedback Windows:** The Client has [5] business days to provide feedback at each milestone. Delayed feedback extends the project timeline by an equal number of days.

> GUIDANCE: The feedback window clause prevents clients from sitting on reviews for weeks and then blaming you for a late delivery.

---

### 3. COMPENSATION

**Total Project Fee:** $[AMOUNT]

| Milestone | Amount | Due Date |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Project Deposit (upon signing) | $[AMOUNT] (50%) | [Date] |
| Midpoint Milestone (upon [MILESTONE]) | $[AMOUNT] (25%) | [Date] |
| Final Delivery (upon completion) | $[AMOUNT] (25%) | [Date] |

**Payment Method:** [Bank transfer / PayPal / Stripe / etc.]
**Payment Terms:** Due within [15] days of invoice date.
**Late Payment Fee:** [1.5]% per month on overdue balances.

> GUIDANCE: Never start work without a deposit. The 50/25/25 split is standard, but you can adjust to 50/50 or 33/33/34 depending on the project. The key principle: never have more than 25-50% of the project fee outstanding at any point.

---

### 4. REVISIONS

The project fee includes [2] rounds of revisions per deliverable. A "revision" is defined as a set of changes communicated in a single, consolidated feedback document.

Additional revision rounds beyond the included [2] will be billed at $[HOURLY RATE] per hour, with a minimum of [1] hour per additional round.

> GUIDANCE: Defining what a "revision" means prevents the client from sending feedback in 15 separate emails and calling each one a "revision." Consolidated feedback = one round.

---

### 5. SCOPE CHANGES

Any work requested by the Client that falls outside the scope defined in Section 1 requires a signed Change Order (see Change Order Form template in this kit). No out-of-scope work will begin without a signed Change Order and, if applicable, additional payment.

---

### 6. KILL FEE

If the Client terminates this project after signing:

- **Before work begins:** Full refund of deposit minus a $[250] administrative fee
- **During Phase 1 (Discovery):** Client pays for work completed; remaining deposit is non-refundable
- **During Phase 2+ (Design/Build):** Client pays for all work completed plus [25]% of the remaining unpaid project fee

---

### 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

All IP provisions from the Master Service Agreement apply. If no MSA is in place: the Service Provider retains all rights to the work until full and final payment is received, at which point all rights to the final deliverables transfer to the Client.

---

### 8. ACCEPTANCE

8.1 Upon delivery of each milestone, the Client has [5] business days to review and either (a) approve the deliverable, (b) request revisions within the included revision rounds, or (c) identify specific defects that deviate from the agreed scope.

8.2 If the Client does not respond within [5] business days, the deliverable is deemed accepted.

> GUIDANCE: The auto-acceptance clause prevents a project from sitting in limbo indefinitely because the client never formally approves anything.

---

# TEMPLATE 6: NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT (MUTUAL NDA)

---

## MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Between:**

**Party A:** [YOUR NAME / BUSINESS NAME] ("Provider")
**Party B:** [CLIENT NAME / BUSINESS NAME] ("Client")

Collectively referred to as the "Parties."

---

### 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore a potential or ongoing business relationship involving [brief description of engagement — e.g., website redesign, brand strategy, software development]. In connection with this relationship, each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other.

---

### 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, whether in writing, orally, visually, or in any other form, including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, and financial information
- Client lists, customer data, and vendor relationships
- Product designs, prototypes, specifications, and source code
- Marketing plans, pricing strategies, and sales data
- Trade secrets, proprietary methods, and processes
- Any information marked "Confidential" or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential

---

### 3. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party
(b) Was already known to the receiving Party before disclosure, as documented
(c) Is received from a third party who is not bound by confidentiality obligations
(d) Is independently developed by the receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information
(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order (with prompt written notice to the disclosing Party)

---

### 4. OBLIGATIONS

Each Party agrees to:

- Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential
- Use Confidential Information only for the purpose described in Section 1
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
- Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used for its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care
- Limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, or advisors who need to know and who are bound by similar confidentiality obligations

---

### 5. TERM

This NDA is effective for [2] years from the Effective Date. The confidentiality obligations survive termination for an additional [1] year.

---

### 6. RETURN OF MATERIALS

Upon termination or upon written request, each Party will promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information in its possession, including copies, notes, and summaries.

---

### 7. REMEDIES

Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this NDA may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages are insufficient. The non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to any other available legal remedies.

---

### 8. GENERAL

- **Governing Law:** [YOUR STATE/COUNTRY]
- **Entire Agreement:** This NDA constitutes the entire agreement regarding confidentiality between the Parties.
- **Severability:** If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.

# TEMPLATE 9: PAYMENT TERMS CLAUSE LIBRARY

> GUIDANCE: Drop these clauses into any contract or SOW. Choose the payment structure that fits your project and client.

---

## PAYMENT CLAUSE VARIATIONS

### Variation 1: Net-15

> All invoices are due within fifteen (15) calendar days of the invoice date. Late payments will incur a fee of 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance.

### Variation 2: Net-30

> All invoices are due within thirty (30) calendar days of the invoice date. Late payments will incur a fee of 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance.

### Variation 3: Milestone-Based

> Fees are billed upon completion of each milestone as defined in the Scope of Work. Each milestone invoice is due within [15] days of the invoice date. Work on the subsequent milestone will not begin until the current milestone invoice is paid.

### Variation 4: 50/50 Split

> 50% of the total project fee is due upon signing this agreement (the "Deposit"). The remaining 50% is due upon delivery of the final deliverables. The Deposit is non-refundable once work has begun.

### Variation 5: Deposit + Final

# CUSTOMIZATION GUIDE

## How to Adapt These Templates for Your Business

### Step 1: Choose Your Contract Stack
Most freelancers need two or three documents per client:

**For new, ongoing clients:** MSA + Detailed SOW (+ NDA if needed)
**For one-time projects:** Fixed-Price Project Contract (includes scope)
**For retainer work:** Hourly/Retainer Contract
**For small, quick projects:** Simple SOW only

### Step 2: Customize Your Defaults
Before your first client, go through each template and fill in your standard defaults:

- Your legal name and business details
- Your standard payment terms (Net-15 or Net-30)
- Your hourly rate (for overage and change order pricing)
- Your jurisdiction for governing law and dispute resolution
- Your standard revision limit (2-3 rounds is typical)
- Your standard kill fee percentage (20-30% is standard)

### Step 3: What to Change Per Client
For each new client, you only need to customize:

- Client name, company, and contact details
- Project-specific deliverables, timeline, and pricing
- Any client-specific terms (e.g., they require a longer NDA)

### Step 4: What NOT to Change (Unless You Have a Reason)
- IP clauses — these protect you
- Late payment penalties — these get you paid
- Limitation of liability — this caps your risk
- Kill fee language — this protects your time
- Auto-acceptance clauses — these prevent indefinite review cycles

### Step 5: Before You Send
Run through this checklist:

- [ ] All [BRACKETS] have been replaced with real information
- [ ] All guidance notes ("> GUIDANCE:") have been removed
- [ ] Payment terms are clearly stated
- [ ] Revision limits are specified
- [ ] Exclusions are listed
- [ ] Kill fee or termination terms are included
- [ ] Both parties' names and details are correct
- [ ] Signature lines are in place
- [ ] You've read it from the client's perspective — does it make sense?
- [ ] You've saved a copy for your records before sending
