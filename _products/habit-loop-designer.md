## Habit Loop Mapping Worksheets

### How Habit Loops Work

Every habit follows the same neurological pattern: **Cue → Routine → Reward**. The cue triggers the behavior. The routine is the behavior itself. The reward is what your brain gets out of it. Understanding this loop is the key to both building new habits and breaking old ones.

**To build a habit:** Design a clear cue, make the routine easy, and ensure the reward is immediate and satisfying.

**To break a habit:** Identify the cue and reward of the unwanted behavior, then substitute a new routine that delivers the same reward.

---

### Example 1: Building a Morning Exercise Habit

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Habit Goal** | Exercise for 20 minutes every morning |
| **CUE** | Alarm goes off at 6:30 AM → I see my workout clothes laid out on the chair |
| **ROUTINE** | Put on workout clothes → Do 20-minute bodyweight circuit in living room |
| **REWARD** | Immediate: Post-workout smoothie + 5 minutes of a favorite podcast. Long-term: More energy, better mood, physical fitness |
| **Friction Removal** | Clothes laid out the night before, workout mat stays unrolled, phone alarm is across the room (forces getting out of bed) |
| **Streak Anchor** | Mark calendar with X after each workout |

---

### Example 2: Breaking a Social Media Scrolling Habit

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Habit to Break** | Scrolling Instagram/TikTok for 30+ minutes before bed |
| **Current CUE** | Getting into bed → picking up phone from nightstand |
| **Current ROUTINE** | Open social media → scroll for 30-60 minutes |
| **Current REWARD** | Entertainment, novelty, stress relief, winding down |
| **SUBSTITUTION CUE** | Getting into bed → pick up Kindle (phone charges in kitchen) |
| **New ROUTINE** | Read fiction on Kindle for 15-20 minutes |
| **New REWARD** | Same relaxation/wind-down feeling, plus better sleep, sense of progress through book |
| **Friction Added** | Phone charges in kitchen (not bedroom), social media apps moved to second screen on phone, screen time limit of 10 min/day |

---

### Example 3: Building a Daily Writing Habit

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Habit Goal** | Write 500 words every day |
| **CUE** | After pouring morning coffee → sit at desk → open writing document (doc stays open on computer overnight) |
| **ROUTINE** | Write for 25 minutes (Pomodoro timer) without editing |
| **REWARD** | Immediate: Update word count tracker, enjoy coffee during writing (temptation bundle). Long-term: Completed manuscript, creative fulfillment |
| **Friction Removal** | Document stays open, writing app opens on startup, phone in another room during writing time |
| **Streak Anchor** | Daily word count logged in tracker |

---

### Example 4: Breaking a Nail Biting Habit

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Habit to Break** | Biting nails during stress or boredom |
| **Current CUE** | Feeling stressed or bored → hand moves to mouth unconsciously |
| **Current ROUTINE** | Bite nails → pick at cuticles |
| **Current REWARD** | Stress relief, tactile stimulation, something to do with hands |
| **SUBSTITUTION CUE** | Notice hand moving toward mouth (awareness is the first step) |
| **New ROUTINE** | Squeeze a stress ball or rub a smooth worry stone in pocket |
| **New REWARD** | Same tactile stimulation and stress relief, plus healthy nails |
| **Friction Added** | Apply bitter nail polish, keep stress ball visible on desk, wear a rubber band on wrist as awareness cue |

---

### Example 5: Building a Daily Meditation Habit

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Habit Goal** | Meditate for 10 minutes daily |
| **CUE** | After brushing teeth in the morning → sit on meditation cushion (cushion stays in bathroom doorway) |
| **ROUTINE** | Set timer for 10 minutes → guided meditation via app |
| **REWARD** | Immediate: Feeling of calm, cup of favorite tea afterward. Long-term: Reduced anxiety, better focus, emotional regulation |
| **Friction Removal** | Cushion visible in doorway (impossible to miss), app pre-loaded with next meditation, timer preset to 10 min |
| **Streak Anchor** | Mark meditation app streak + physical calendar |

## Habit Stacking Planner

### What Is Habit Stacking?

Habit stacking uses the formula: **"After I [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."**

You attach a new behavior to an existing one, using the completion of the old habit as the cue for the new one. This works because existing habits already have strong neural pathways — you're borrowing that momentum.

---

## Implementation Intention Worksheets

### What Are Implementation Intentions?

An implementation intention is a specific plan for WHEN, WHERE, and HOW you'll perform a behavior. The formula is:

**"When [SITUATION], I will [BEHAVIOR]."**

Research by Peter Gollwitzer shows that people who form implementation intentions are approximately **2-3x more likely** to follow through compared to those who only set goals.

## Identity-Based Habit Design Prompts

### The Core Idea

Most people set goals like "I want to lose 20 pounds" or "I want to read more books." These are outcome-based goals. They work in the short term but fade because they don't change how you see yourself.

Identity-based habits flip the script: **Instead of "I want to run," you become "I am a runner."** Every action becomes a vote for the type of person you want to become.

## Temptation Bundling Planner

### What Is Temptation Bundling?

Temptation bundling (coined by researcher Katy Milkman) pairs an activity you NEED to do with an activity you WANT to do. The formula:

**"I will only [THING I WANT] while/after I [THING I NEED TO DO]."**

This links the reward of the enjoyable activity to the completion of the important one.

## Environment Design Checklist

### The Principle

Your environment is the invisible hand that shapes your behavior. Make good habits **obvious and easy**. Make bad habits **invisible and hard**. You don't need more discipline — you need better defaults.

---

### Bedroom

**For better sleep and morning habits:**

- [ ] Phone charges OUTSIDE the bedroom (or across the room, not on nightstand)
- [ ] Alarm clock is not your phone (or phone alarm requires getting out of bed)
- [ ] Workout clothes are laid out and visible the night before
- [ ] Water bottle on nightstand
- [ ] Blackout curtains or sleep mask accessible
- [ ] Room temperature set to 65-68°F (18-20°C) for optimal sleep
- [ ] No TV in bedroom (if reducing screen time)
- [ ] Book or Kindle on nightstand (replacing phone)
- [ ] Journal and pen on nightstand (if building journaling habit)
- [ ] Meditation cushion visible (if building meditation habit)

### Kitchen

**For better nutrition habits:**

- [ ] Healthy snacks at eye level in fridge and pantry
- [ ] Unhealthy snacks moved to hard-to-reach places (or not in the house)
- [ ] Fruit bowl visible on counter
- [ ] Water filter/pitcher prominently placed
- [ ] Meal prep containers washed and visible
- [ ] Vitamins/supplements next to coffee maker or in daily path
- [ ] Cookbooks or meal plan visible on counter
- [ ] Junk food in opaque containers (out of sight, out of mind)
- [ ] Plates are 9-inch, not 12-inch (smaller plates = smaller portions)
- [ ] Tall thin glasses for high-calorie drinks (visual illusion of more)

### Workspace

**For focus and productivity habits:**

- [ ] Phone is in another room or in a locked drawer during deep work
- [ ] Noise-cancelling headphones accessible
- [ ] Website blocker installed and configured (Freedom, Cold Turkey, etc.)
- [ ] Desk is clear of clutter at end of each day
- [ ] Task list or planner is open and visible
- [ ] Water bottle on desk
- [ ] Timer visible for Pomodoro or time-blocking
- [ ] Standing desk option or movement reminder set
- [ ] Good lighting (natural light preferred)
- [ ] One tab open = one task (close everything else)

### Phone / Digital

**For reducing mindless scrolling and digital distraction:**

- [ ] Social media apps moved off home screen (to a folder on second page)
- [ ] Notifications turned off for non-essential apps
- [ ] Screen time limits set for addictive apps
- [ ] Grayscale mode enabled (reduces visual appeal)
