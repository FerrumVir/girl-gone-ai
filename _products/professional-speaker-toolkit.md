## Speaker One-Sheet Template

### What It Is

A speaker one-sheet is your resume for event organizers. It's a single page (front and back) that sells you as a speaker. Send it when pitching, attach it to proposals, and have it ready on your website.

### Front Page Layout

---

**[YOUR FULL NAME]**
[Your Title / Positioning Statement — e.g., "Marketing Strategist & Keynote Speaker"]

[PROFESSIONAL HEADSHOT — high-resolution, approachable but authoritative]

---

**ABOUT**

[2-3 sentences positioning you. Focus on: who you help, what you speak about, and your credibility. This is not a bio — it's a value proposition for event organizers.]

Example:
> [YOUR NAME] helps [AUDIENCE] [ACHIEVE OUTCOME] through [YOUR APPROACH]. With [X] years of experience in [INDUSTRY] and having [IMPRESSIVE CREDENTIAL: keynoted X events, built a company to $X, trained X people, published in X], [he/she/they] delivers talks that are equal parts actionable and entertaining.

---

## Event Proposal Template

### Use This When: Proactively pitching a talk to an event, conference, or organization

---

**Proposed by:** [YOUR NAME]

**Event:** [EVENT NAME] (if known)
**Proposed talk title:** [TITLE]
**Format:** [Keynote / Breakout / Workshop]
**Duration:** [XX minutes]

---

**TALK DESCRIPTION (For Event Program)**

[Write this as if it will appear in the event program. 100-150 words. Third person. Focus on what attendees will learn/gain.]

Example:
> In this [FORMAT], [SPEAKER NAME] breaks down [TOPIC] into [NUMBER] actionable strategies that [AUDIENCE] can implement immediately. Drawing from [EXPERIENCE/RESEARCH/CASE STUDIES], attendees will leave with [SPECIFIC OUTCOMES: a framework, a plan, clarity on X, tools they can use Monday morning]. Whether you're [SITUATION A] or [SITUATION B], this session will [PROMISE].

---

**THREE KEY TAKEAWAYS**

Attendees will walk away with:

1. **[TAKEAWAY 1]** — [One sentence expanding on what they'll learn and why it matters]
2. **[TAKEAWAY 2]** — [One sentence]
3. **[TAKEAWAY 3]** — [One sentence]

---

**TALK OUTLINE**

| Time | Section | Content |
|------|---------|---------|
| 0-5 min | Opening Hook | [How you'll grab attention — story, statistic, demo] |
| 5-15 min | Context/Problem | [Set up the challenge the audience faces] |
| 15-30 min | Framework/Solution | [Your main teaching — the core methodology] |
| 30-40 min | Examples/Stories | [Case studies, real-world applications, demos] |
| 40-45 min | Action Steps | [What they should do immediately after leaving] |
| 45-50 min | Q&A | [Open floor for questions] |

*(Adjust timing for your proposed duration)*

---

[2-3 sentences on why this topic is timely and relevant for their specific audience. Reference industry trends, challenges, or opportunities.]

---

**WHY ME**

[2-3 sentences on why you're the right person to deliver this talk. Reference specific experience, results, or unique perspective.]

---

[Third-person bio for event program use]

## Speaking Fee Calculator

### Step 1: Determine Your Base Rate

**Experience Level Assessment:**

| Level | Experience | Typical Range |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Emerging | 0-10 paid talks, building portfolio | $0-2,000 |
| Established | 10-50 talks, some notable events | $2,000-7,500 |
| Professional | 50-100+ talks, strong reputation | $7,500-15,000 |
| Expert/Celebrity | In-demand, recognizable name | $15,000-50,000+ |

**Your base rate: $___________**

---

### Step 2: Adjust for Event Type

| Factor | Multiplier | Reasoning |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Nonprofit/community event | 0.5x - 0.75x | Reduced budgets, goodwill |
| Conference breakout session | 0.75x - 1x | Smaller audience, less prep |
| Conference keynote | 1x - 1.5x | Main stage, highest visibility |
| Corporate private event | 1.5x - 3x | Larger budgets, higher value |
| Multi-day commitment | 2x - 3x | Longer time away, more prep |
| Virtual/webinar | 0.5x - 0.75x | Less travel, less commitment |
| International (travel required) | 1.25x + expenses | Time zone disruption, travel time |

---

### Step 3: Factor in Additional Value

Add fees for:

| Add-On | Typical Additional Fee |
|--------|----------------------|
| Custom talk (requires new content creation) | +$1,000-5,000 |
| Workshop (half-day, hands-on) | +$2,000-10,000 |
| Pre-event consulting call | +$500-1,000 |
| Post-event Q&A/VIP session | +$500-2,000 |
| Attendee workbooks/materials created | +$500-2,000 |
| Recording rights (for their use) | +$1,000-5,000 |
| Exclusivity (won't speak at competitor events) | +20-50% |

---

### Step 4: Calculate Your Quote

```
Base Rate:                      $_________
x Event Type Multiplier:        x_________
= Adjusted Base:                $_________
+ Add-On 1 (___________):      $_________
+ Add-On 2 (___________):      $_________
+ Travel & Expenses:            $_________
= TOTAL QUOTE:                  $_________
```

---

### Step 5: What to Include (vs. Charge Extra)

**Always include in your fee:**
- Talk preparation and customization
- Slides/presentation materials
- One pre-event call with organizer
- Being available day-of for photos, networking
- Post-event thank you and resource sharing

**Charge separately (or have them cover):**
- Travel (flights, hotel, ground transport)
- Meals during travel days
- Specific A/V equipment you need to bring
- Additional appearances beyond the agreed talk
- Rush requests (less than 30 days notice)

---

### Step 6: Fee Communication Scripts

**When asked "What's your fee?"**

> "My fee for a [DURATION] [FORMAT] is [AMOUNT], plus travel expenses. This includes [WHAT'S INCLUDED]. I'm happy to discuss the specifics of your event to see if there's flexibility."

**When their budget is lower than your fee:**

> "I appreciate you sharing the budget. My standard fee for this type of engagement is [AMOUNT]. Here are a few options: I could deliver a shorter session ([REDUCED DURATION]) for [REDUCED AMOUNT], or if there's flexibility with [RECORDING RIGHTS / PRODUCT SALES / PROMOTION TO YOUR AUDIENCE], I can work within your budget. What matters most to you?"

**When you want to speak for free (strategically):**

> "I'm happy to waive my fee for this event because [HONEST REASON: it's aligned with my mission, the audience is perfect, it's a portfolio-building opportunity]. In exchange, would you be able to [PROVIDE VIDEO RECORDING / TESTIMONIAL / PROMOTE MY PRODUCT TO ATTENDEES / COVER TRAVEL]?"

## Talk Preparation Checklist

### 4 Weeks Before

- [ ] Confirm all logistics with organizer (date, time, room, format, audience size)
- [ ] Complete pre-event discovery call with organizer
- [ ] Research the audience: who are they, what do they care about, what's their experience level?
- [ ] Review event theme and other speakers — position your talk to complement (not repeat) others
- [ ] Outline your talk structure (don't write a full script yet)
- [ ] Identify 2-3 stories/examples specific to THIS audience
- [ ] Book travel if applicable

### 2 Weeks Before

- [ ] Complete first draft of slides
- [ ] Practice full talk from start to finish (time it)
- [ ] Adjust content to hit your time slot (cut if over, expand if under)
- [ ] Prepare any handouts or follow-up resources
- [ ] Send slides or one-sheet to organizer if requested
- [ ] Confirm all tech requirements have been communicated and acknowledged
- [ ] Research venue — what does the room look like? How far is the hotel?

### 1 Week Before

- [ ] Practice talk 2-3 more times (ideally in front of a friend/colleague for feedback)
- [ ] Finalize slides — no more changes after this point
- [ ] Prepare backup of slides (USB drive, email to yourself, cloud link)
- [ ] Pack: outfit, tech (laptop, adapters, clicker, charger), water bottle
- [ ] Confirm travel details (flights, hotel, ground transport)
- [ ] Review your opening hook — practice it until it's natural and confident
- [ ] Plan your outfit (test it standing, walking, with a mic clipped to it)

### Day Before

- [ ] Travel to venue city (if applicable)
- [ ] Do a light practice — run through your opening and closing (not the full talk — avoid over-rehearsing)
- [ ] Lay out your outfit and tech
- [ ] Get a good night's sleep (seriously — rest beats extra rehearsal)
- [ ] Eat well, hydrate, avoid alcohol
- [ ] Review your "emergency card" — 3 key points you'll hit no matter what

### Day Of

- [ ] Eat a proper meal 2-3 hours before your talk (not immediately before)
- [ ] Arrive 60-90 minutes early
- [ ] Tech check: slides, audio, video, clicker, confidence monitor
- [ ] Walk the stage — know the space, test your movement areas
- [ ] Test your voice in the room — volume, clarity, mic positioning
- [ ] Find a quiet space for 10 minutes before you go on (deep breaths, visualization)
- [ ] Put phone on airplane mode
- [ ] Take a photo of the room for your records/social media
- [ ] Go deliver an incredible talk

### Immediately After

- [ ] Thank the organizer and AV team personally
- [ ] Be available for audience questions, photos, and networking (at least 15-20 minutes)
- [ ] Collect business cards or contact info from valuable connections
- [ ] Post on social media while the energy is fresh (thank the organizer, share a photo)
- [ ] Make notes: what worked, what fell flat, what to change next time

## Post-Event Follow-Up System

### Within 24 Hours

- [ ] Send thank-you email to organizer (Template: Email 8 above)
- [ ] Post event recap on social media (tag organizer, event, venue)
- [ ] Send promised follow-up resources to attendees (via organizer or directly)
- [ ] Connect on LinkedIn with key people you met

### Within 1 Week

- [ ] Request testimonial from organizer (Template: Email 9 above)
- [ ] Follow up with anyone who expressed interest in your work/services
- [ ] Write a brief debrief for yourself: what went well, what to improve, new material that emerged
- [ ] Update your one-sheet with new event listing
- [ ] Send speaker reel request if the event was recorded

### Within 1 Month

- [ ] Add testimonial to your materials (once received)
- [ ] Add event video/photos to your speaker page
- [ ] Update your fee if this event leveled you up
- [ ] Reach out to [5] other events in similar category — use this event as social proof

### Ongoing (Every Quarter)

- [ ] Touch base with past organizers (Template: Email 10 above)
- [ ] Update your one-sheet with latest talks, testimonials, and credentials
- [ ] Pitch [5-10] new events per quarter
- [ ] Review and raise your fee annually based on demand
- [ ] Develop 1 new talk topic per year to keep offerings fresh
