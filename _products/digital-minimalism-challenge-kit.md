# Digital Minimalism Challenge Kit

> A 30-day program for taking back control of your screen time. Daily challenges, screen time tracker, app audit worksheet, notification audit, digital boundaries templates, and weekly reflection prompts.

---

## SECTION 1: HOW TO USE THIS KIT

### Before You Start
```
1. Screenshot your current screen time stats (Settings > Screen Time on iOS / Digital Wellbeing on Android)
2. Write down your "why" — what made you want to do this challenge?
3. Tell one person you're doing it (accountability)
4. Don't try to change everything at once — this is progressive by design
5. If you miss a day, continue the next day — don't restart from Day 1
```

### What to Expect
```
Week 1: Awareness (notice your habits without judgment)
Week 2: Friction (add small barriers between you and mindless use)
Week 3: Replacement (fill the gaps with intentional alternatives)
Week 4: System Design (build structures that make this permanent)
```

## SECTION 2: 30-DAY CHALLENGE CALENDAR

### WEEK 1: AWARENESS

**Day 1: The Baseline**
```
CHALLENGE: Record your current state. Don't change anything yet.

ACTION:
- Screenshot your screen time report
- Write down: total daily average, top 3 apps by time, number of pickups
- Notice: When do you reach for your phone without thinking?

REFLECTION: "My phone currently gets [X] hours of my day. That's [X] hours per week / [X] hours per month. If I could get back even half of that, I would use it for ________."
```

**Day 2: The First Pickup**
```
CHALLENGE: Track every time you pick up your phone for the first 4 hours of your day.

ACTION:
- Keep a tally sheet (paper or simple note)
- Every pickup: make a mark + note WHY (bored? notification? habit? intentional?)
- Don't restrict yourself — just notice

REFLECTION: "I picked up my phone [X] times before noon. [X] of those were intentional. [X] were automatic."
```

**Day 3: The Notification Audit**
```
CHALLENGE: Review and disable non-essential notifications.

ACTION:
- Go to Settings > Notifications
- For each app, ask: "Does this notification require my IMMEDIATE attention?"
- Keep: calls, texts from close people, calendar reminders, security alerts
- Disable: social media, news, promotional, most email, games
- Goal: under 10 apps with notifications enabled

REFLECTION: "I disabled notifications from [X] apps. The ones I kept: ________"
```

**Day 4: The Home Screen Detox**
```
CHALLENGE: Redesign your phone's home screen.

ACTION:
- Remove social media apps from your home screen (move to a folder or second page)
- Home screen keeps ONLY: tools you use intentionally (camera, maps, calendar, notes)
- Replace social media shortcuts with: podcast app, kindle, meditation, or a blank widget
- Optional: set your wallpaper to a question ("What are you looking for?")

REFLECTION: "Without apps staring at me, I reached for my phone [MORE/LESS/SAME] today."
```

**Day 5: The App Timer Experiment**
```
CHALLENGE: Set daily time limits on your top 3 time-wasting apps.

ACTION:
- Use built-in Screen Time (iOS) or Digital Wellbeing (Android)
- Set limits: your current usage minus 30 minutes for each app
- When the timer goes off, respect it (at least for today)

LIMITS SET:
App 1: [NAME] — current avg [X] min/day — limit set: [X] min
App 2: [NAME] — current avg [X] min/day — limit set: [X] min
App 3: [NAME] — current avg [X] min/day — limit set: [X] min

REFLECTION: "When the timer blocked me, I felt ________"
```

**Day 6: The Charging Station**
```
CHALLENGE: Designate a phone "parking spot" that isn't your bedroom or desk.

ACTION:
- Choose a location: kitchen counter, entryway table, hallway
- Charge your phone there overnight (not on your nightstand)
- Buy a $10 alarm clock if you use your phone as one
- When you're home, your phone lives at its station (you go to it — it doesn't come to you)

REFLECTION: "Without my phone in [BEDROOM/WORKSPACE], I noticed ________"
```

**Day 7: Week 1 Reflection**
```

### WEEK 2: FRICTION

**Day 8: The Grayscale Test**
```
CHALLENGE: Turn your phone display to grayscale for the full day.

ACTION:
- iOS: Settings > Accessibility > Display > Color Filters > Grayscale
- Android: Settings > Digital Wellbeing > Bedtime Mode (or Accessibility > Color Correction)
- Leave it on for 24 hours

WHY: Color is a dopamine trigger. Social media is designed with colors that demand attention. Grayscale makes your phone boring — and boring is the point.

REFLECTION: "In grayscale, the apps I still used were ________ (actually useful). The ones I stopped opening: ________ (only fun because of color/stimulation)."
```

**Day 9: The Social Media Log-Out**
```
CHALLENGE: Log out of all social media apps (don't delete — just log out).

ACTION:
- Open each social media app
- Go to settings and log out
- This means: next time you open it, you'll have to type your password
- That 10-second friction is enough to break the autopilot

APPS LOGGED OUT OF: ________
Times I started to open one and stopped because of the login: ________

REFLECTION: "The login barrier stopped me [X] times today. I used that time for ________ instead."
```

**Day 10: The Phone-Free Morning**
```
CHALLENGE: Don't look at your phone for the first 60 minutes after waking up.

ACTION:
- Phone stays at charging station (from Day 6)
- Morning routine without phone: [YOUR PLAN — coffee, journal, walk, stretch, read, shower]
- If you need an alarm: use the $10 alarm clock
- After 60 minutes: check phone intentionally

MORNING ROUTINE (phone-free):
1. ________
2. ________
3. ________
4. ________

REFLECTION: "My morning without my phone felt ________. I was [MORE/LESS] anxious, [MORE/LESS] rushed, and [MORE/LESS] present."
```

**Day 11: The Phone-Free Evening**
```
CHALLENGE: No phone after 9pm (or 1 hour before bed).

ACTION:
- Set a "wind-down" alarm at your chosen time
- Phone goes to charging station
- Replace with: reading, conversation, journaling, stretching, skincare, puzzle, audiobook
- If you need entertainment: choose ONE analog or passive option (book, bath, music without screen)

WIND-DOWN ROUTINE:
Time phone goes away: ________
I replaced scrolling with: ________

REFLECTION: "I fell asleep [FASTER/SAME/HARDER]. My sleep quality was ________. I wish I'd done this [SOONER/I'm struggling with it]."
```

**Day 12: The Batch Check**
```
CHALLENGE: Check email and messages only at designated times (not reactively).

ACTION:
- Set 2-3 check times (e.g., 9am, 1pm, 5pm)
- Outside those times: email and message apps stay closed
- If urgent contact is needed: people can call you
- Turn off email badges so you don't see the red number

CHECK SCHEDULE:
Check 1: ________ AM
Check 2: ________ PM
Check 3: ________ PM (optional)

REFLECTION: "I checked [FEWER/SAME] times today. Nothing was actually urgent. The thing I was worried about missing: ________ (did it matter?)"
```

**Day 13: The Single-Task Day**
```
CHALLENGE: When using your phone, do ONE thing at a time.

ACTION:
- Open one app. Use it. Close it. Then decide what's next.
- No tabbing between apps, no mid-text switches to Instagram, no background scrolling
- Before each pickup ask: "What am I opening my phone to do?" and ONLY do that thing

HOW IT WENT:
Times I caught myself multi-tasking: ________
Times I successfully single-tasked: ________

REFLECTION: "When I focus on one phone task at a time, I spend [MORE/LESS] total time on my phone."
```

```

**Day 17: The Walk Without Phone**
```
CHALLENGE: Take a 20+ minute walk without your phone.

ACTION:
- Leave your phone at home (or in the car/bag on silent)
- Walk somewhere familiar (you don't need maps)
- Notice: sounds, smells, people, textures, your own thoughts
- If you feel anxious without it: that IS the point. Sit with it.

WHAT I NOTICED (without phone as buffer):
1. ________
2. ________
3. ________

REFLECTION: "Without my phone, my brain did ________. I felt ________ about not having it with me."

- Move (yoga, stretching, dance, exercise, clean)
- Connect (face-to-face conversation, phone call with full attention, play with kids/pets)
- Rest (nap, meditation, sit outside, do nothing)

HOW IT FELT: ________
```

### WEEK 4: SYSTEM DESIGN

**Day 22: The Rules of Engagement**
```
CHALLENGE: Write your personal digital boundaries document.

ACTION:
Fill out these boundaries (these become your long-term rules):

MY PHONE RULES:
- Phone-free times: ________ to ________ (morning) and ________ to ________ (evening)
- Phone-free zones: [LIST — bedroom? dinner table? car? bathroom?]
- Social media time limit per day: ________ minutes
- Email check times: ________, ________, ________
- Notifications allowed from: [LIST APPS]
- Phone lives at: [CHARGING STATION LOCATION]
- Before picking up phone, I ask myself: ________

Print or display these somewhere visible.
```

**Day 23: The App Audit**
```
CHALLENGE: Audit every app on your phone and make final keep/delete decisions.

ACTION:
Go through EVERY app. For each one, answer:

[APP NAME]:
- Does this add real value to my life? [YES / NO / UNCERTAIN]
- Could I get this value another way (computer only, once a week)? [YES / NO]
- Does this respect my attention or exploit it? [RESPECT / EXPLOIT]
- When I use this, do I feel better or worse afterward? [BETTER / WORSE / NEUTRAL]
- DECISION: [KEEP / DELETE / KEEP BUT LIMIT / MOVE TO COMPUTER ONLY]

After audit:
Apps deleted: ________
Apps moved to computer-only: ________
Apps kept with time limits: ________
Apps kept unrestricted: ________
```

**Day 24: The Subscription Purge**
```
CHALLENGE: Unsubscribe from every email list, notification, and digital subscription that doesn't serve you.

ACTION:
- Open your email and scroll through the last 2 weeks
- Unsubscribe from every newsletter you don't actually read
- Cancel streaming services you haven't used in 30 days
- Leave group chats that drain you
- Mute or unfollow social accounts that make you feel worse
- Delete unused accounts entirely where possible

UNSUBSCRIBED FROM: [COUNT] email lists
CANCELLED: [LIST]
MUTED/UNFOLLOWED: [COUNT] accounts
GROUP CHATS LEFT: [COUNT]

REFLECTION: "Digital decluttering feels like ________"
```

### If You're Slipping (Emergency Reset)
```
QUICK RESET (15 minutes):
1. Delete the app causing the most damage right now
2. Log out of everything on your phone
3. Turn on grayscale
4. Put phone at charging station
5. Set timer for 24 hours of minimal use
6. Re-read your Day 29 digital philosophy
7. Text your accountability person: "I'm resetting today"

You don't need to restart the full 30 days.
Just restart ONE habit. Today. Right now.
```
