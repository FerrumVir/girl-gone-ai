### Compliance Checklist (US Products)

- [ ] FDA regulations reviewed for your product category
- [ ] Ingredient list formatted correctly (INCI names, descending order)
- [ ] Required label elements: product name, net weight, ingredient list, company name + address, directions
- [ ] No prohibited claims (cannot claim to cure, treat, or prevent disease unless it's a drug)
- [ ] Fragrance/color additives compliant
- [ ] Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) confirmed with manufacturer
- [ ] Product liability insurance obtained
- [ ] State cosmetics registration (if required by your state)
- [ ] If making claims like "organic" or "natural" — verify compliance with FTC guidelines

### Pricing Formula

```
Retail Price = Total COGS x 4-5x (industry standard for beauty)

Example:
COGS = $8.50/unit
Retail Price = $8.50 x 4 = $34
Retail Price = $8.50 x 5 = $42.50

Choose pricing based on:
- Competitor comparison
- Perceived value
- Target customer's budget
- Brand positioning (mass market vs. prestige)
```

## QUICK-START GUIDE

### Step 1: Define Your Brand (Week 1-2)
Complete the Brand Identity Worksheet. Every decision you make after this should align with these answers. Don't skip this — it prevents expensive pivots later.

### Step 2: Develop Your Product (Week 3-12)
Research manufacturers, request samples, and test rigorously. Don't rush this. A bad first product kills a brand faster than anything.

### Step 3: Build Your Packaging (Week 8-14)
Start packaging design while waiting for final product approval. Order packaging samples before committing to large orders.

### Step 4: Set Your Pricing (Week 12-14)
Use the pricing worksheet to ensure profitability. Price with confidence — your expertise is part of the value.

### Step 5: Prepare Your Launch (Week 14-18)
Build the website, create content, grow your waitlist, send PR packages. Everything should be ready 2 weeks before launch day.

### Step 6: Launch and Iterate (Week 18+)
Execute the launch timeline. Collect feedback. Adjust. Plan product #2 based on what you learn from product #1.
