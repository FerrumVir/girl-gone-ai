**Purpose:** Turn a new subscriber into an engaged reader who trusts you and eventually buys from you.
**Timing:** Emails sent over 10-14 days after signup.

**Subject:** Here's your [LEAD MAGNET NAME]

---

**Here's your download:** [LINK]

- Start with [SPECIFIC SECTION] — it's the most immediately useful
- Don't try to do everything at once — pick one thing and implement it today

**What to expect from me:**

I send emails [FREQUENCY] about [TOPICS]. Every email will either:
- Teach you something useful about [TOPIC]
- Share a story or lesson from my experience

**One question for you:** What's your biggest challenge with [TOPIC] right now? Just hit reply — I read every response and it helps me write better emails for you.

Talk soon,
[YOUR NAME]

## Weekly Newsletter Framework

### The "Value First" Newsletter Template

**Send day:** [Pick one day and be consistent: Tuesday and Thursday mornings perform well]
**Length:** 400-800 words
**Structure:** Hook → Value → CTA

---

**Subject line formula options:**
- "The [LESSON/TIP/FRAMEWORK] that [RESULT]"
- "[NUMBER] things I learned about [TOPIC] this week"
- "What [OBSERVATION] taught me about [YOUR NICHE]"
- "Stop [COMMON MISTAKE]. Do [BETTER APPROACH] instead."
- "The [TOOL/METHOD/IDEA] nobody's talking about"

---

**Template:**

```
[FIRST LINE — The Hook]
(This must be compelling enough to earn the second line.
One sentence. Bold statement, surprising fact, or intriguing question.)

[CONTEXT — 2-3 sentences]
(What prompted this email? A personal experience, observation, reader question, or timely event.)

[THE MEAT — 150-300 words]
(Your main insight, tip, framework, or story. This is where the value lives.
Make it actionable — they should be able to DO something after reading.)

Options:
- Teach one thing well (the "golden nugget")
- Tell a story with a clear lesson
- Share a framework/system they can apply
- Curate 3-5 resources with your commentary
- Do a "quick hits" format: 5 short items

[THE BRIDGE — 1-2 sentences]
(Transition from free value to your ask. This should feel natural, not jarring.)

[THE CTA — Clear and singular]
(One thing you want them to do: reply, click, buy, share, save.
NEVER include multiple CTAs — pick one.)

[SIGN-OFF]
(Consistent, brief, your personality.)
```

## Segmentation Strategy Guide

### Segment Your List For Better Results

| Segment | How to Identify | What to Send Them |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| New subscribers (0-30 days) | Signup date | Welcome sequence, more education, slower sell |
| Engaged (open 50%+ emails) | Email platform data | Your best offers, VIP access, early releases |
| Lurkers (open <20%) | Email platform data | Re-engagement sequence, then clean if unresponsive |
| Buyers (purchased anything) | Purchase history | Upsells, loyalty offers, testimonial requests |
| Non-buyers (never purchased) | No purchase + 90+ days on list | Different angles, more proof, lower-ticket offers |
| By interest/topic | Which lead magnet they downloaded, which links they click | Targeted content matching their interest |
| By stage | Self-reported (beginner/intermediate/advanced) | Content at appropriate complexity level |

## Deliverability & List Health Checklist

### Monthly List Maintenance

- [ ] Remove hard bounces (automatic in most platforms)
- [ ] Identify soft bounces (3+ consecutive — consider removing)
- [ ] Segment inactive subscribers (no opens in 90 days)
- [ ] Send re-engagement sequence to inactives
- [ ] Remove unresponsive subscribers after re-engagement fails
- [ ] Check spam complaint rate (keep under 0.1%)
- [ ] Verify no purchased/scraped emails on list (NEVER buy lists)
- [ ] Test deliverability (send test to Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo)
- [ ] Check that all automated sequences are still functioning
- [ ] Review and update any outdated links in evergreen sequences

### Deliverability Best Practices

- Authenticate your domain (SPF, DKIM, DMARC records)
- Send from a consistent "From" name and address
- Never use ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation in subject lines
- Include a plain-text version of every email
- Keep image-to-text ratio balanced (not image-heavy)
- Always include an easy unsubscribe link
- Warm up new sending domains gradually (start with engaged subscribers)
- Avoid spam trigger words in subject lines: "FREE," "Act now," "Limited time," "Click here"
- Send consistently (irregular volume looks suspicious to spam filters)
- Engage your best subscribers first (high opens signal good sender reputation)
