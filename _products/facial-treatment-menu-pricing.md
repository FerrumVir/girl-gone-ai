### Upsell Scripts (Natural, Not Pushy)

**During skin analysis:**
"I'm noticing [observation — texture on your forehead / dullness / congestion]. I'd love to add [ADD-ON] today — it specifically targets that. It's $[X] and adds [X] minutes. Want me to include it?"

**During booking confirmation:**
"For your [SERVICE] on [DATE] — would you like to add [ADD-ON] for $[X]? A lot of my clients love pairing these together for [benefit]."

**At checkout (retail):**
"Your skin responded really well to the [PRODUCT] I used today. I'd recommend using it at home [frequency] to maintain these results between visits. Want me to add it to your total?"

## SECTION 7: PRICING REVIEW SCHEDULE

### Annual Pricing Calendar

| Month | Action |
|-------|--------|
| January | Annual pricing review: compare to market, adjust costs, raise if needed |
| April | Q1 review: are packages selling? Adjust if not |
| July | Mid-year check: review add-on uptake, adjust upsell strategy |
| October | Pre-holiday: consider holiday packages, gift card pricing |
| Ongoing | Track cost increases from suppliers — adjust when COGS rises 10%+ |

### Price Increase Decision Tree

```
Is your effective hourly rate below $100?
  YES → Raise prices now (you're undercharging)
  NO ↓

Are you booked 3+ weeks out?
  YES → Raise prices 10-15%
  NO ↓

Has it been 12+ months since last increase?
  YES → Raise prices at least to match inflation (3-5%)
  NO ↓

Have your product/supply costs increased?
  YES → Raise prices to maintain margins
  NO → Hold prices, focus on filling schedule
```

## QUICK-START GUIDE

### Step 1: Audit Your Current Menu
List every service you offer with current pricing. Calculate true cost per service using Section 2.

### Step 2: Identify Margin Problems
If any service has less than 60% profit margin, either raise the price or reduce costs (switch products, reduce time, or eliminate).

### Step 3: Research Your Market
Complete the competitive positioning table. Know where you stand relative to others in your area.

### Step 4: Restructure Your Menu
Organize into clear tiers. Add a compelling description for each service. Make it easy for clients to choose.

### Step 5: Build Packages
Create 2-3 packages that bundle services at 10-20% savings. This increases commitment and average lifetime value.

### Step 6: Implement Upsells
Train yourself to naturally suggest add-ons at every appointment. Even a 30% add-on rate at $35/add-on adds $1,000+/month on 100 clients.
