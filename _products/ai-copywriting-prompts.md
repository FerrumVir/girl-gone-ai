# AI Copywriting Prompt Pack — 120+ Ready-to-Use Prompts

---

## How to Use This Pack

This pack contains 125 prompts organized across 8 categories covering every type of copy you need to write. Every prompt is copy-paste ready — just replace the bracketed [PLACEHOLDER] variables with your specific details before submitting to your AI assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.).

**Tips for best results:**

1. **Replace every placeholder.** The more specific your inputs, the more usable the output. "Women aged 25-34 who just started freelancing" beats "my audience."
2. **Start with strategy prompts.** Before writing copy, use the positioning and research prompts to clarify your angle.
3. **Layer prompts for full campaigns.** Use a headline prompt, then a body copy prompt, then a CTA prompt to build a complete piece.
4. **Test multiple versions.** Run the same prompt 2-3 times or ask for variations — A/B testing starts at the draft stage.
5. **Edit for your voice.** AI gives you structure and speed. You add personality, brand voice, and specifics that only you know.

**How placeholders work:**

- `[PRODUCT]` — your product or service name
- `[AUDIENCE]` — who you're writing for (e.g., "busy entrepreneurs," "first-time homebuyers," "SaaS marketers")
- `[BENEFIT]` — the primary outcome or transformation
- `[PAIN POINT]` — the problem your audience faces
- `[TONE]` — voice style (e.g., "urgent and direct," "friendly and conversational," "luxury and aspirational")
- `[PRICE]` — your product's price point
- `[CTA]` — the action you want them to take

## Category 1: Sales Page Copy (20 Prompts)

### 1. Full Sales Page Framework

```
Write a complete sales page for [PRODUCT] targeting [AUDIENCE]. The product costs [PRICE] and the primary benefit is [BENEFIT]. Structure it as:
- Headline (attention-grabbing, benefit-driven)
- Subheadline (clarifies the headline)
- Problem section (3-4 pain points the reader recognizes)
- Agitation (what happens if they don't solve this problem)
- Solution introduction (position the product as the answer)
- What's included (bullet list of features with benefit-driven descriptions)
- Social proof section (placeholder for testimonials)
- Price justification (compare to alternatives)
- FAQ (5 common objections answered)
- Final CTA with urgency element
Tone: [TONE]. Length: 1500-2000 words.
```

### 2. Sales Page Headline Generator

```
Generate 20 headline options for a sales page selling [PRODUCT] to [AUDIENCE]. The main benefit is [BENEFIT] and the main pain point is [PAIN POINT].

Create 4 headlines in each style:
- Direct benefit (state the outcome clearly)
- Question-based (ask a question they can't ignore)
- How-to (promise a method or system)
- Curiosity gap (hint at a secret or little-known approach)
- Social proof (reference results or numbers)

Each headline should be under 12 words. Bold your top 3 recommendations.
```

### 3. Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS) Section

```
Write a PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) section for a sales page selling [PRODUCT] to [AUDIENCE].

Problem: Describe [PAIN POINT] in vivid, specific language the reader will recognize. Use "you" language. Make them feel seen. (3-4 sentences)

Agitate: Make the problem feel urgent. What are the consequences of NOT solving this? What are they missing out on? What's it costing them in time, money, or stress? (3-4 sentences)

Solve: Introduce [PRODUCT] as the relief. Don't list features — paint the picture of life after the problem is solved. (3-4 sentences)

Tone: [TONE]. Total length: 250-350 words.
```

### 5. Price Justification Section

```

- Break the price down to a daily/per-use cost
- Frame it as an investment with ROI
- Address the "I can find this for free" objection
- End with a confident CTA

Tone: confident but not pushy. 200-300 words.
```

```
- Version B: "Better than money back" guarantee (promise a specific result or refund)

Each should be 50-100 words. Tone: confident and generous — the guarantee should make the purchase feel risk-free.
```

### 7. Urgency and Scarcity Elements
