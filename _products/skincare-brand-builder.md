# SECTION 4: REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

---

## FDA Compliance Checklist (United States)

### Cosmetics vs. Drugs — Know the Difference

**Your product is a COSMETIC if it:**
- Cleanses, beautifies, promotes attractiveness, or alters appearance
- Does NOT claim to treat, cure, or prevent disease/conditions

**Your product is a DRUG (and requires FDA approval) if it:**
- Claims to treat acne, eczema, psoriasis, or any medical condition
- Contains active drug ingredients (e.g., salicylic acid as acne treatment)
- Uses drug claims in marketing ("cures," "treats," "heals")

**My products are classified as:** [ ] Cosmetic [ ] Drug [ ] OTC Drug [ ] Unsure — need legal review

### Compliance Checklist

- [ ] All products properly classified (cosmetic vs. drug)
- [ ] Claims reviewed — no drug claims in marketing or labels
- [ ] All ingredients verified safe and approved for cosmetic use
- [ ] No banned/restricted ingredients used
- [ ] Labels comply with FDA Fair Packaging and Labeling Act
- [ ] Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) followed
- [ ] Product safety testing completed (patch testing, stability)
- [ ] Facility registered with FDA (Voluntary Cosmetic Registration Program)
- [ ] Product ingredient listing filed with FDA (voluntary but recommended)
- [ ] MoCRA (Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act 2022) requirements met:
  - [ ] Facility registration
  - [ ] Product listing
  - [ ] Adverse event reporting system
  - [ ] Safety substantiation documented
  - [ ] GMP compliance
