# AI Beauty Business Automation — Prompt Pack

> 100+ copy-paste AI prompts designed for beauty professionals. Organized by use case. Works with ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI assistant. Replace bracketed text with your specific details.

## CATEGORY 1: CLIENT COMMUNICATION (20 Prompts)

### Appointment Confirmations

**Prompt 1 — Standard Confirmation:**
```
Write a friendly appointment confirmation text message for my beauty business. The client's name is [NAME], their appointment is [SERVICE] on [DAY] at [TIME]. Include: a brief reminder to arrive 5 minutes early, one relevant aftercare prep tip for this service, and my cancellation policy (24-hour notice required). Keep it under 160 characters if possible, or 3 short sentences max. Tone: professional but warm.
```

**Prompt 2 — New Client Welcome:**
```
Write a welcome message for a new client named [NAME] who just booked their first [SERVICE] appointment with me on [DATE]. Include: what to expect during their visit, how to prepare (arrive with clean skin/no makeup/etc.), parking instructions at [LOCATION], and let them know they'll need to fill out an intake form. Tone: warm, reassuring, excited to meet them. Keep it concise — this will be sent via text or DM.
```

**Prompt 3 — Reminder (24 Hours Before):**
```
Write a short appointment reminder for [CLIENT NAME] who has a [SERVICE] tomorrow at [TIME]. Include: a friendly "see you tomorrow!" opener, the address if needed, one quick prep reminder for this specific service, and ask them to let me know ASAP if anything changes. Under 100 words.
```

### Follow-Up Messages

**Prompt 4 — Post-Appointment Follow-Up:**
```
Write a follow-up text to send 24 hours after a [SERVICE] appointment. Client name: [NAME]. Include: ask how they're feeling/how their [service result] is looking, 2-3 specific aftercare reminders for this service, let them know to reach out with any questions, and gently mention booking their next appointment in [TIMEFRAME]. Warm and caring tone. Under 100 words.
```

**Prompt 5 — Rebooking Nudge (Overdue Client):**
```
Write a rebooking message for a client named [NAME] who usually comes every [FREQUENCY] for [SERVICE] but hasn't booked in [TIME SINCE LAST VISIT]. Tone: friendly, not pushy or guilt-tripping. Mention you miss seeing them, note it's been a while, and offer to find a time that works. Include a soft CTA to book. Keep it casual and short — like texting a friend who happens to be your client.
```

**Prompt 6 — Review Request:**
```
Write a message asking [CLIENT NAME] for a Google review. Context: they just had a [SERVICE] and were very happy with their results. Include: genuine appreciation for their trust, explain that reviews help small businesses like mine get found, make it EASY by including "[GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]", and mention it only takes 30 seconds. Don't be begging — be confident and grateful. Under 80 words.
```

**Prompt 7 — Birthday Message:**
```
Write a birthday message for my client [NAME]. Include a birthday wish and a special offer: [OFFER DETAILS — e.g., $15 off their next service, free add-on, etc.]. Mention the offer is valid for the month of their birthday. Tone: celebratory, personal, and genuine. Under 60 words.
```

**Prompt 8 — Re-Engagement (Lapsed Client):**
```
Write a re-engagement message for a client named [NAME] who hasn't visited in [3+ months]. I don't know why they stopped coming — could be life, budget, or they found someone else. Tone: no guilt, no pressure, genuinely checking in. Mention I'd love to see them if/when they're ready. Optionally include a "welcome back" incentive of [OFFER]. Keep it under 60 words and genuinely warm.
```

### Handling Difficult Situations

**Prompt 9 — Cancellation Policy Enforcement:**
```
Write a professional but firm message to a client named [NAME] who cancelled their [SERVICE] appointment with less than 24 hours notice (this is their [1st/2nd/3rd] time). Explain the late cancellation fee of $[AMOUNT], why the policy exists (I turn away other clients to hold their spot), and offer to apply the fee toward a future appointment if they rebook within [TIMEFRAME]. Firm but not rude.
```

**Prompt 10 — Handling a Complaint:**
```
Write a response to a client who is unhappy with their [SERVICE] results. They said: "[WHAT THEY SAID]". I want to: acknowledge their frustration, take responsibility (or explain what happened), offer a solution [FREE TOUCH-UP / REFUND / REDO / etc.], and maintain the relationship. Tone: empathetic, professional, solution-oriented. Do not be defensive. Under 150 words.
```

## CATEGORY 2: SOCIAL MEDIA CAPTIONS (20 Prompts)

### Before/After Posts

**Prompt 11:**
```
Write 5 Instagram captions for a [SERVICE] before/after post. The transformation was [DESCRIBE — dramatic, subtle, corrective, etc.]. Include a mix of: one caption that's educational (explains what was done), one that's emotional (how the client felt), one that's short and punchy, one that drives bookings (with CTA), and one that asks a question for engagement. Each caption should be 2-4 sentences max. Include relevant hashtag suggestions for each.
```

**Prompt 12:**
```
Write a TikTok caption for a [SERVICE] transformation video. The hook is: "[YOUR HOOK]". Caption should be short (under 150 characters), use 3-4 relevant hashtags, and create curiosity or encourage watching until the end. Give me 5 options.
```

### Educational Content

**Prompt 13:**
```
Write an Instagram carousel post (8 slides) about [TOPIC — e.g., "5 signs you need a facial", "lash aftercare mistakes", "ingredients to avoid if you have acne"]. Slide 1 should be a hook/title that makes people stop scrolling. Slides 2-7 should each cover one point with a headline + 1-2 sentence explanation. Slide 8 should be a CTA (save, share, or book). Also write a caption (3-5 sentences) that introduces the topic and encourages saves.
```

**Prompt 14:**
```
Write 10 "myth vs. fact" statements about [YOUR SERVICE/NICHE] that I can use for social media content. Format each as:
MYTH: [Common misconception]
FACT: [The truth, explained simply in 1-2 sentences]
Make them genuinely surprising or counterintuitive — things my audience probably believes that are wrong.
```

### Engagement Posts

**Prompt 15:**
```
Write 10 "This or That" poll ideas for my Instagram Stories related to [YOUR NICHE — beauty/skincare/lashes/etc.]. Each should have two options that are both appealing and will spark debate. Examples of the format I want: "[Option A] or [Option B]?" Include a mix of product preferences, routine choices, and fun/silly ones.
```

**Prompt 16:**
```
Write 10 Instagram Story question box prompts to get my audience talking about [YOUR NICHE]. Make them feel personal and inviting — not like a survey. Mix of: asking for opinions, getting their stories, requesting advice, and fun/light questions. These should generate responses I can then reshare or create content from.
```

### Promotional Content

**Prompt 17:**
```
Write 3 versions of an Instagram caption announcing [PROMOTION DETAILS — e.g., "20% off all facials in March"]. Version 1: Urgency-focused (limited time). Version 2: Value-focused (what they get). Version 3: Fun/casual (like telling a friend about a deal). Each should include a clear CTA to book. Max 4 sentences each.
```

**Prompt 18:**
```
Write a caption announcing a new service: [SERVICE NAME + BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. Explain what it is, who it's for, what results to expect, the price ($[X]) and duration ([X] minutes), and how to book. Tone: excited but professional. End with a CTA. Give me 2 versions: one for Instagram feed (longer) and one for TikTok (short + punchy).
```

### Content Ideas

**Prompt 19:**
```
Give me 30 content ideas for my [NICHE — e.g., esthetician, lash tech, nail artist] Instagram account. Mix of: Reels ideas, carousel topics, single-image posts, and Story ideas. Organize them by content pillar: Educational (10), Transformations/Results (5), Behind-the-scenes (5), Personal/Relatable (5), Promotional/Booking (5). Keep each idea to one sentence.
```

**Prompt 20:**
```
Write 7 Instagram Reel hooks (the first sentence/3 seconds) for a [YOUR NICHE] account. These should make someone stop scrolling. Topics: [LIST 7 TOPICS]. Each hook should create curiosity, make a bold claim, or start with action. No "Hey guys" openings.
```

## CATEGORY 3: EMAIL MARKETING (15 Prompts)

### Newsletters

**Prompt 21:**
```
Write a monthly email newsletter for my beauty business clients. This month's content: [LIST WHAT TO INCLUDE — e.g., seasonal skincare tip, new service announcement, open booking dates, product recommendation]. My brand voice is [DESCRIBE — warm, professional, fun, educational]. Subject line + preview text + full email body. Keep it scannable (short paragraphs, maybe a bulleted section). One clear CTA at the end.
```

**Prompt 22:**
```
Write 10 email subject lines for a [TYPE OF EMAIL — e.g., monthly newsletter, flash sale, new service announcement, seasonal promotion] for my beauty business. Make them: intriguing (open-worthy), under 50 characters, a mix of curiosity-based, benefit-based, and urgency-based. Avoid spam trigger words.
```

### Promotional Emails

**Prompt 23:**
```
Write a promotional email announcing [SALE/OFFER DETAILS]. Target audience: my existing clients who have visited before. Include: subject line, preview text, email body with the offer clearly stated, deadline/expiration, and CTA button text. Create urgency without being pushy. Under 200 words for the body.
```

**Prompt 24:**
```
Write a 3-email sequence for my seasonal promotion: [PROMOTION DETAILS].
Email 1 (announcement): Introduce the offer, create excitement
Email 2 (reminder, 3 days later): Reinforce value, add social proof or FAQ
Email 3 (last chance, final day): Urgency, scarcity, final CTA
Each email should have: subject line, preview text, body (under 150 words each), and CTA.
```

### Automated Sequences

**Prompt 25:**
```
Write a 4-email welcome sequence for new clients who just booked their first appointment with me (a [YOUR SPECIALTY]).
Email 1 (immediately): Welcome + what to expect at their appointment
Email 2 (2 days before): Preparation tips + logistics (address, parking)
Email 3 (1 day after appointment): Thank you + aftercare + review request
Email 4 (2 weeks later): Check-in + rebooking reminder
Tone: warm, professional, reassuring. They've never been to me before so reduce anxiety.
```

**Prompt 26:**
```
Write a re-engagement email sequence (3 emails) for clients who haven't booked in 90+ days.
Email 1: "We miss you" — genuinely check in, soft offer
Email 2 (7 days later): Educational value — a seasonal tip or new service info
Email 3 (7 days later): Stronger offer — discount or incentive with deadline
Don't guilt-trip. Make them WANT to come back. Include subject lines.
```

## CATEGORY 4: BOOKING & BUSINESS OPERATIONS (15 Prompts)

### Service Descriptions

**Prompt 27:**
```
Write a service description for my booking page/website for: [SERVICE NAME]. Include: what the service involves (in client-friendly language, not technical jargon), who it's best for, what results to expect, how long it takes ([DURATION]), the price ($[PRICE]), and how often they should book (frequency). Tone: informative and enticing. Under 150 words. Also write a shorter version (2-3 sentences) for my booking software.
```

**Prompt 28:**
```
Write descriptions for my full service menu. I offer:
[LIST ALL SERVICES WITH PRICES AND DURATIONS]
For each, write: one-sentence tagline + 2-3 sentence description covering what it is and who it's for. Format them consistently. Tone: professional, clear, appealing.
```

### Policies

**Prompt 29:**
```
Write a clear, professional cancellation and no-show policy for my beauty business. Details: [CANCELLATION WINDOW — e.g., 24 hours], [LATE CANCEL FEE — e.g., 50% of service], [NO-SHOW FEE — e.g., full service price], [LATE ARRIVAL POLICY — e.g., 15 min late = shortened or rescheduled]. Tone: firm but fair. Explain WHY the policy exists (you hold that time and turn away others). This will be on my website and booking page.
```

**Prompt 30:**
```
Write an FAQ page for my beauty business website. I'm a [YOUR SPECIALTY] located in [CITY]. Cover these questions:
1. How do I book?
2. What's your cancellation policy?
3. How should I prepare for my appointment?
4. How long does [SERVICE] last?
5. How often should I come back?
6. Do you offer [packages/gift cards/memberships]?
7. What forms of payment do you accept?
8. Is parking available?
9. What if I'm not happy with my results?
10. Are you accepting new clients?
Answer each in 2-3 sentences. Tone: helpful and clear.
```

### Client Education

**Prompt 31:**
```
Write aftercare instructions for [SERVICE] that I can send to clients after their appointment. Format: clear bullet points, organized by timeframe (first 24 hours, first week, ongoing maintenance). Include: what to DO, what NOT to do, what's NORMAL to experience, and when to contact me. Tone: caring but clear. Keep it to one page max (printable).
```

**Prompt 32:**
```
Write a "what to expect at your first appointment" guide for new clients coming in for [SERVICE]. Cover: how to prepare, what happens when they arrive, what the service involves step-by-step (in simple terms), how they'll feel after, and aftercare basics. Tone: reassuring — many people are nervous about their first time. Under 200 words.
```

## CATEGORY 6: BUSINESS STRATEGY (15 Prompts)

### Pricing & Revenue

**Prompt 38:**
```
I'm a [SPECIALTY] who currently charges $[PRICE] for [SERVICE] which takes [DURATION]. My monthly overhead is $[AMOUNT]. I see approximately [NUMBER] clients per month. Help me determine if my pricing is profitable by calculating: my effective hourly rate, my profit per service after overhead allocation, and whether I should raise my prices. If I should raise them, suggest a new price point and a script for communicating the increase to existing clients.
```

**Prompt 39:**
```
Help me create 3 service packages/bundles for my [SPECIALTY] business. My current services and prices: [LIST SERVICES + PRICES]. Create packages that: increase average ticket value, encourage commitment/loyalty, and make sense together (services that complement each other). For each package, give me: a name, what's included, the individual value, the package price (10-20% savings), and who it's ideal for.
```

### Growth Planning

**Prompt 40:**
```
I'm a solo [SPECIALTY] earning $[MONTHLY REVENUE] per month working [HOURS] per week. I'm fully booked [X] weeks out. Help me create a plan to increase revenue without working more hours. Consider: raising prices, adding higher-ticket services, selling retail products, creating passive income (digital products, courses), or hiring. Give me 3 specific strategies ranked by ease of implementation and potential revenue impact.
```

**Prompt 41:**
```
Help me write a 90-day business goal plan. My beauty business current state: [DESCRIBE — revenue, client count, services offered, challenges]. My 90-day goals: [LIST 3-5 GOALS]. For each goal, break it down into: weekly actions, key metrics to track, potential obstacles, and how to measure success. Format as a clear action plan I can follow week by week.
```

### Client Retention

**Prompt 42:**
```
My rebook rate is [CURRENT %] and I want to increase it to [TARGET %]. My business: [SPECIALTY], average service price $[X], typical rebooking interval [X WEEKS]. Give me 10 specific strategies to increase client retention and rebooking, ranked from easiest to implement to most impactful. Include scripts or templates for each strategy where applicable.
```

**Prompt 43:**
```
Create a loyalty/rewards program for my beauty business. I see mostly [CLIENT TYPE] who spend an average of $[X] per visit and come every [FREQUENCY]. Design a program that: rewards consistent bookings, encourages referrals, increases average spend, and is simple to manage as a solo provider. Include: program structure, point system or tier system, specific rewards at each level, and how to announce it to existing clients.
```

### Systems & Efficiency

**Prompt 44:**
```
I spend too much time on [ADMIN TASK — e.g., answering DMs, writing follow-ups, posting content, managing bookings]. Currently it takes me [X HOURS/WEEK]. Help me create templates, systems, or workflows to cut this time in half. Give me: pre-written templates I can copy-paste with minor edits, a streamlined workflow, and any tools/automation recommendations for a solo beauty professional.
```

**Prompt 45:**
```
Help me create a weekly business routine/schedule for my [SPECIALTY] business. My service hours are [DAYS/TIMES]. I also need to fit in: social media content creation, client follow-ups, bookkeeping, supply ordering, education/training, and self-care/rest. Create a weekly schedule template that batches similar tasks and protects my energy. I have [X] hours total per week available.
```

## QUICK-START GUIDE

### How to Use These Prompts

1. **Choose the prompt** that matches what you need right now
2. **Replace all bracketed text** [LIKE THIS] with your specific details
3. **Paste into ChatGPT, Claude, or your preferred AI tool**
4. **Review the output** — edit for your voice and accuracy
5. **Save versions you love** in a "swipe file" for reuse

### Tips for Better Results

- The more specific details you provide in the brackets, the better the output
- If the first result isn't right, reply with "Make it more [casual/professional/shorter/longer]"
- Always review AI-generated content for accuracy before sending to clients
- Adjust tone/voice if the output doesn't sound like you
- Use outputs as starting points, not final drafts — add your personal touch
