# SECTION 1: HABIT SETUP GUIDE

> Before tracking anything, choose the right habits. Most people fail not because of willpower but because they picked the wrong habits.

---

## The Habit Selection Filter

For each habit you are considering, answer these five questions:

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| Is this specific enough to check off? ("Exercise" is vague. "Walk for 20 minutes" is checkable.) | Y / N |
| Can I do this in under 30 minutes? (Big commitments fail fast. Start small.) | Y / N |
| Does this align with a goal I actually care about? (Not someone else's goal for me.) | Y / N |
| Can I attach this to something I already do? (After I [existing habit], I will [new habit].) | Y / N |
| Am I willing to do this on my worst day? (If the answer is no, make it smaller.) | Y / N |

**If you answered "No" to 2+ questions, shrink the habit until all five are "Yes."**

---

## Habit Stacking Worksheet

Habit stacking means linking a new habit to an existing one. This removes the need for motivation.

| Existing Habit (I already do this) | New Habit (I want to do this) | Stacked Version |
|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| Example: Pour morning coffee | Example: Take vitamins | "After I pour my coffee, I take my vitamins." |

# SECTION 7: GOOGLE SHEETS LAYOUT

> Copy this structure into a new Google Sheets document. Set up one sheet per month.

---

## Sheet Structure

### Tab 1: Habit Tracker

**Row 1 (headers):**

| A | B | C | D | E | F | G | H |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Date** | **Day** | **Habit 1: [NAME]** | **Habit 2: [NAME]** | **Habit 3: [NAME]** | **Habit 4: [NAME]** | **Habit 5: [NAME]** | **Habit 6: [NAME]** |

**Rows 2-32:** One row per day of the month. Enter 1 for done, 0 for not done.

**Row 33 (totals):**

| A | B | C | D | E | F | G | H |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TOTAL** | | =SUM(C2:C32) | =SUM(D2:D32) | =SUM(E2:E32) | =SUM(F2:F32) | =SUM(G2:G32) | =SUM(H2:H32) |

**Row 34 (percentage):**

| A | B | C | D | E | F | G | H |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **%** | | =C33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*100 | =D33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*100 | =E33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*100 | =F33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*100 | =G33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*100 | =H33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*100 |

---

### Tab 2: Streak Calculator

| A | B | C |
|---|---|---|
| **Habit** | **Current Streak** | **Longest Streak** |
| [Habit 1] | =streak formula | =max streak formula |
| [Habit 2] | =streak formula | =max streak formula |

**Streak formula (manual method):** Count backwards from today. For each consecutive day with a "1" in the habit column, add 1. Stop counting at the first "0."

**Longest streak formula:**
```
=ARRAYFORMULA(MAX(FREQUENCY(IF(C2:C32=1, ROW(C2:C32)), IF(C2:C32=0, ROW(C2:C32)))))
```
(Enter with Ctrl+Shift+Enter)

---

### Tab 3: Monthly Summary

| A | B | C | D | E | F |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Month** | **Habit** | **Days Done** | **Days Possible** | **Consistency %** | **Streak** |
| January | [Habit 1] | | | =C2/D2*100 | |
| January | [Habit 2] | | | =C3/D3*100 | |

---

### Conditional Formatting Rules

**Green cell (habit done):**
```
=C2=1
```

**Red cell (habit missed):**
```
=AND(C2=0, A2<>"")
```

**Gold row (100% day -- all habits done):**
```
=AND(C2=1, D2=1, E2=1, F2=1, G2=1, H2=1)
```

**Progress bar (in-cell using REPT):**
```
=REPT("*", ROUND(C33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*10, 0)) & REPT("-", 10-ROUND(C33/COUNTA(A2:A32)*10, 0))
```

# SECTION 10: TROUBLESHOOTING

> When habits start to slip, use this diagnostic.

---

## The Habit Diagnostic

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| I keep forgetting | No trigger/cue | Stack it onto an existing habit |
| I do not feel motivated | Relying on motivation (motivation is unreliable) | Make it so small you cannot say no |
| I skip weekends | Weekend routine is different | Set a weekend-specific trigger |
| I missed one day and gave up | All-or-nothing thinking | Never miss twice in a row. One miss is fine. Two is a pattern. |
| It feels pointless | No visible progress | Review your tracker weekly. The data IS the progress. |
| I am tracking too many habits | Willpower is spread too thin | Drop to 3 habits for 2 weeks, then add back slowly |
| I dread the habit | It is too big or not enjoyable | Shrink it. Make the minimum version laughably easy. |
| I stopped tracking | Tracking felt like a chore | Switch formats (paper to digital, or vice versa) |

---

## The Two-Day Rule

The most important rule in this entire system:

**Never miss the same habit two days in a row.**

One missed day is a rest day. Two missed days is the start of a new (bad) habit. If you miss Monday, Tuesday is non-negotiable -- even if you do the absolute minimum version.

This single rule is more powerful than any tracker, any app, or any system.
