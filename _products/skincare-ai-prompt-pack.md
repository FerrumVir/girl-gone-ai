# Skincare & Beauty AI Prompt Pack

> 40+ AI prompts for skincare professionals, estheticians, and beauty business owners. Copy-paste into ChatGPT or Claude. Replace [BRACKETED VARIABLES] with your details.

## SECTION 1: CLIENT CONSULTATION & COMMUNICATION

### Prompt 1: Client Consultation Summary
```
Write a professional post-consultation summary for my skincare client.

Client name: [NAME]
Age: [AGE]
Primary concerns: [LIST — e.g., hyperpigmentation, dehydration, hormonal acne]
Skin type: [DRY / OILY / COMBINATION / SENSITIVE / NORMAL]
Current routine: [LIST THEIR CURRENT PRODUCTS]
Medications: [ANY RELEVANT MEDICATIONS — e.g., retinoids, birth control, antibiotics]
Lifestyle factors: [SUN EXPOSURE / DIET / STRESS / SLEEP / WATER INTAKE]
Treatment performed today: [WHAT YOU DID]
Products recommended: [WHAT YOU'RE SUGGESTING]
Next appointment: [DATE]

Write the summary in a warm, professional tone that:
- Recaps their concerns and what we discussed
- Explains the treatment performed and expected results/downtime
- Lists the recommended home care routine (AM and PM, step by step)
- Notes any ingredients or products to avoid
- Includes aftercare instructions
- Confirms next steps

Keep it clear enough for a non-expert client to understand. Under 400 words.
```

### Prompt 4: Treatment Plan Proposal
```
Write a treatment plan for a skincare client.

Client name: [NAME]
Primary goal: [WHAT THEY WANT TO ACHIEVE — e.g., clear acne, reduce fine lines, even skin tone]
Skin type: [TYPE]
Budget level: [BUDGET-CONSCIOUS / MODERATE / INVESTMENT-READY]
Timeframe: [HOW LONG THEY'RE WILLING TO COMMIT]
Frequency they can come in: [WEEKLY / BIWEEKLY / MONTHLY]
Treatments available at my practice: [LIST YOUR SERVICES]
Product lines I carry: [LIST]

Create a phased treatment plan:
- Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Foundation — what treatments and why
- Phase 2 (Weeks 5-12): Correction — intensive treatments
- Phase 3 (Ongoing): Maintenance — long-term plan

For each phase, include:
- In-office treatment recommendations with frequency
- Home care routine (AM and PM)
- Expected results at each stage
- Investment estimate

Write it as if presenting to the client — professional but not intimidating.
```

### Prompt 7: Educational Carousel Script
```
Write an Instagram carousel (8-10 slides) about [SKINCARE TOPIC — e.g., "Why Your Moisturizer Isn't Working," "The Truth About Retinol," "How to Layer Your Serums"].

Target audience: [WHO — e.g., skincare beginners, 30+ women, acne-prone teens]

- Slides 2-8: One point per slide (header + 1-2 sentence explanation)

```

### Prompt 8: Reel Script — Myth Busting
```
Write a 30-second Instagram Reel script debunking a skincare myth.

Myth: "[THE MYTH — e.g., 'You don't need sunscreen if it's cloudy']"
Truth: [THE CORRECT INFORMATION]
Why people believe the myth: [BRIEF EXPLANATION]
One actionable tip: [WHAT THEY SHOULD DO INSTEAD]
Your credential that makes this credible: [e.g., licensed esthetician for 8 years]

Structure:
- Hook (0-3 sec): "Stop believing this skincare lie..."
- Myth stated (3-8 sec): What people think
- Truth bomb (8-20 sec): The real science/fact
- Actionable tip (20-27 sec): What to do instead
- CTA (27-30 sec): Follow for more / link in bio

Write it conversational — like talking to a friend, not reading a textbook.
```

## SECTION 4: EDUCATION & EXPERTISE

### Prompt 16: Client FAQ Answer
```
Write a clear, professional answer to this frequently asked question from skincare clients:

Question: "[THE FAQ — e.g., 'How often should I get a facial?', 'Can I use retinol and vitamin C together?', 'What's the difference between a chemical peel and a facial?']"

Answer requirements:
- Start with the short, direct answer (1 sentence)
- Then explain WHY (2-3 sentences of context)
- Include any "it depends" factors
- End with a specific recommendation
- Tone: knowledgeable friend (not textbook)
- Length: 100-150 words

Write it suitable for: [FAQ PAGE / INSTAGRAM STORY / EMAIL RESPONSE]
```
