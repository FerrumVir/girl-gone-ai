## Pre-Launch Checklist

### Week 4: Validation & Foundation

**Product Validation:**
- [ ] Problem confirmed: You've talked to 5+ people who have this problem
- [ ] Solution confirmed: At least 3 people said they'd pay for this
- [ ] Market check: Similar products exist and sell (validation, not competition threat)
- [ ] Unique angle identified: What's different about YOUR version?
- [ ] Price validated: Your target audience can and will pay $[PRICE]

**Offer Architecture:**

```
CORE OFFER: [Your main product]
Price: $___________
Format: [PDF / Course / Template / Software / Community]
Delivery: [Instant download / Drip access / Platform login]

BONUS 1 (Optional): [What it is]
Value if sold separately: $___
Why it enhances the core offer: ___________

BONUS 2 (Optional): [What it is]
Value if sold separately: $___
Why it enhances the core offer: ___________

LAUNCH INCENTIVE: [Discount / Extra bonus / Limited spots / Early access]
Deadline: ___________
```

---

**Sales Page Sections Checklist:**
- [ ] Headline (benefit-driven, specific)
- [ ] Subheadline (clarifies the headline)
- [ ] Hero image/video (product mockup or demo)
- [ ] Problem section (pain points the reader recognizes)
- [ ] Solution section (your product as the answer)
- [ ] What's inside (detailed breakdown of deliverables)
- [ ] Who it's for / Who it's not for
- [ ] Social proof (testimonials, results, credentials)
- [ ] Pricing (with value comparison)
- [ ] FAQ (5-8 common objections answered)
- [ ] Guarantee / Risk reversal
- [ ] Final CTA (with urgency if applicable)
- [ ] Mobile-responsive and fast-loading

---

**Beta Tester Program:**
- [ ] Recruit 5-10 beta testers (email, DM, community)
- [ ] Give them free access in exchange for: honest feedback + testimonial if they love it
- [ ] Set feedback deadline: [DATE - at least 5 days before launch]
- [ ] Follow up for testimonial quotes you can use on launch day

**Beta Outreach Message:**

```
Hey [NAME],

I'm finishing up [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION] and I'm looking for 5-10 people
to test it before launch. You'd get free access in exchange for
honest feedback and (if you love it) a testimonial quote I can use.

You came to mind because [SPECIFIC REASON — they match your target buyer].

Interested? It'll take about [TIME] to go through, and I need feedback by [DATE].

[YOUR NAME]
```

### Week 2: Systems & Email Prep

**Technical Setup:**
- [ ] Sales page live (can be hidden/password-protected)
- [ ] Payment gateway connected and tested (Stripe/PayPal)
- [ ] Product delivery automated (Gumroad, SendOwl, Teachable, etc.)
- [ ] Purchase confirmation email configured
- [ ] UTM parameters set up for tracking (utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign)
- [ ] Analytics configured (page views, button clicks, conversion tracking)
- [ ] Abandoned cart sequence set up (if platform supports it)

**Email Infrastructure:**
- [ ] Launch email sequence written (see templates below)
- [ ] All emails loaded into email platform
- [ ] Send times optimized (Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am your audience's timezone)
- [ ] Preview text filled in for every email
- [ ] Links tested in every email
- [ ] Segmentation set: buyers vs. non-buyers tagged correctly

---

### Week 1: Final Prep

**Days 7-5:**
- [ ] Full purchase flow dry run (start to finish as a customer)
- [ ] Test on mobile (50%+ of opens happen on mobile)
- [ ] Proofread everything: sales page, emails, social posts
- [ ] Beta tester testimonials collected and formatted
- [ ] Social media posts scheduled or drafted

**Days 4-2:**
- [ ] Send "coming soon" email to list
- [ ] Post countdown content on social
- [ ] Brief any affiliates/partners on launch details
- [ ] Clear calendar for launch day and day after

**Day 1 (Eve of Launch):**
- [ ] Everything is ready — no more changes
- [ ] Double-check: first email is scheduled correctly
- [ ] Deep breath and early bedtime

## Launch Day Timeline

### Hour-by-Hour Operations

```
[6:00 AM] Wake up, review the plan, open all dashboards
[7:00 AM] Final technical check — sales page live, payment working
[8:00 AM] LAUNCH: Send Email #1, Post launch announcement

[12:00 PM] Midday metrics check + post social proof

[4:00 PM] Respond to all new messages
[6:00 PM] Evening social post (grateful/casual)
[8:00 PM] Record final day-1 metrics
[9:00 PM] Celebrate + prep for Day 2
```

## Conversion Rate Optimization Guide

### Benchmarks for Digital Products

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| Sales page conversion (cold traffic) | <1% | 1-2% | 2-4% | 4%+ |
| Sales page conversion (warm/email traffic) | <3% | 3-5% | 5-10% | 10%+ |
| Email open rate (launch emails) | <20% | 25-35% | 35-45% | 45%+ |
| Email click rate (launch emails) | <2% | 3-5% | 5-8% | 8%+ |
| Cart abandonment recovery | <5% | 5-10% | 10-20% | 20%+ |

### Quick Wins for Higher Conversions

1. **Add urgency that's real** (price increase, bonus expiration, limited quantity)
2. **Reduce friction** (fewer form fields, clearer CTA, instant access messaging)
3. **Add social proof near the CTA** (testimonial directly above the buy button)
4. **Improve your guarantee** (stronger = less risk = more purchases)
5. **Match the landing page to the traffic source** (if they came from a specific email/ad, the page should reference what they clicked on)

## Evergreen Funnel Setup

### Converting Your Launch Into an Always-On Sales Machine

**The Evergreen Funnel Structure:**

```
[Traffic Source] → [Lead Magnet] → [Welcome Sequence] → [Evergreen Pitch Sequence] → [Sales Page]
                                                              ↓ (if no purchase)
                                                    [Long-term nurture / weekly newsletter]
                                                              ↓ (periodic)
                                                    [Re-engagement offers]
```

**Key Differences from Live Launch:**
- No real-time urgency → Create deadline using countdown timers (evergreen deadlines)
- No live energy → Social proof and automation do the heavy lifting
- No "everyone's talking about it" → Testimonials and case studies substitute for buzz

**Evergreen Email Sequence (Post-Welcome):**

| Email | Day | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|
| 1 | Day 12 | Problem awareness (deepen the pain) |
| 2 | Day 14 | Solution introduction (present your product) |
| 3 | Day 16 | What's inside (detailed breakdown) |
| 4 | Day 18 | Social proof (testimonials + results) |
| 5 | Day 20 | FAQ / Objection handling |
| 6 | Day 22 | Deadline (their personal deadline based on signup date) |
| 7 | Day 23 | Final reminder (last chance at this price) |

**Evergreen Urgency Options:**
- Time-limited discount from sign-up date (e.g., "48-hour launch price" unique to each subscriber)
- Expiring bonus (available only for X days after joining your list)
- Limited spots per month (if applicable to your product type)
- "Introductory price" that you genuinely plan to increase
