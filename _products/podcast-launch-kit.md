### Recording Space Setup

**Ideal Recording Environment Checklist:**
- [ ] Quiet room (no HVAC noise, traffic, appliances running)
- [ ] Soft surfaces (carpet, curtains, bookshelves absorb echo)
- [ ] Away from windows (traffic noise, reflections)
- [ ] Door closed during recording
- [ ] Phone on airplane mode
- [ ] "Recording in Progress" sign on door
- [ ] Consistent setup (same position each time for consistent audio)
- [ ] Test recording: clap in the room — if you hear echo, add more soft surfaces

**Quick Acoustic Fixes (No Money Required):**
- Record in a closet full of clothes (the best budget booth)
- Hang blankets on walls or over mic
- Record facing a bookshelf (books absorb sound)
- Place pillows behind and around your recording area
- Avoid recording in kitchens, bathrooms, or empty rooms (hard surfaces = echo)

```
[PRE-RECORDING — Before you hit record]
- Confirm audio setup with guest (headphones, quiet room, mic check)
- Brief the guest: "This will run about [X] minutes. I'll intro you, then we'll dive in.
  Don't worry about being perfect — we can edit. Just be yourself."
- Tell them: "I'll ask [X] questions, then a lightning round at the end."

"Welcome to [SHOW NAME]. Today I'm talking with [GUEST NAME], who is [BRIEF BIO].

[WARM-UP QUESTION — Easy, gets them talking]
"For anyone who doesn't know you yet, give us the 60-second version:

[CORE QUESTIONS — 4-6 questions, deeper each time]

Q1 (Background): "How did you get into [THEIR FIELD]? What was the path?"

Q2 (Expertise): "What's the biggest misconception about [THEIR AREA]?"

Q3 (Tactical): "Walk us through [SPECIFIC PROCESS/FRAMEWORK they're known for].
How does someone actually do this?"

Q4 (Story): "Tell me about a time when [RELEVANT SITUATION].
What happened and what did you learn?"

Q5 (Contrarian): "What's something you believe about [TOPIC]
that most people in your field disagree with?"

Q6 (Actionable): "If someone listening wants to [START/IMPROVE at THING],
what's the ONE thing they should do this week?"

[LIGHTNING ROUND — Quick, fun, memorable]
- "Best book on [TOPIC]?"
- "Most overrated [THING IN THEIR INDUSTRY]?"
- "One tool you can't live without?"
- "Best piece of advice you've ever received?"
- "What's next for you?"

[WRAP-UP]
"[GUEST], this was incredible. Where can people find you?"
(Let them plug their stuff)

"I'll put all those links in the show notes. [GUEST], thank you for being here."

[OUTRO — Record separately]
"That was [GUEST NAME]. My biggest takeaway from that conversation was [YOUR INSIGHT].
Links to everything we mentioned are in the show notes.
If you enjoyed this, [CTA]. See you next [DAY]."
```

**Guest Qualification Criteria:**

- [ ] They have expertise my audience wants to hear
- [ ] They're a good conversationalist (check their other podcast appearances)
- [ ] They have an audience they might share the episode with
- [ ] They're accessible (not necessarily famous — be strategic, not starstruck)
- [ ] They align with my show's values and tone

## Recording & Editing Workflow

### Pre-Recording Checklist (Every Episode)

- [ ] Outline/questions prepared
- [ ] Recording software open and tested
- [ ] Microphone connected and levels checked (peak around -12dB to -6dB)
- [ ] Headphones on (prevents echo/feedback)
- [ ] Room quiet (AC off, phone on airplane mode, notifications disabled)
- [ ] Water nearby (throat gets dry)
- [ ] "Recording" sign on door (if applicable)
- [ ] Backup recording running (phone voice memo or second app)
- [ ] If remote guest: confirm their audio setup, share meeting link

### Recording Best Practices

- Record in WAV or FLAC (lossless) — export as MP3 later
- Leave 5 seconds of silence at the start (helps with noise floor calibration)
- If you stumble, pause, then re-say the sentence cleanly (easier to edit)
- Snap or clap at the start if recording separate tracks (sync point)
- Record 10-20% more than you need (you can always cut, hard to add)

### Editing Workflow (Step-by-Step)

**Basic Edit (30-60 minutes per episode):**

1. [ ] Import audio into editing software
2. [ ] Remove dead air at beginning/end
3. [ ] Cut obvious mistakes, long pauses, "um/uh" clusters
4. [ ] Remove background noise (noise reduction tool)
5. [ ] Normalize audio levels (target -16 LUFS for podcasts)
6. [ ] Add intro/outro music and sound effects
7. [ ] Add any ad spots or sponsor reads
8. [ ] Listen through once at 1.5x speed for quality check
9. [ ] Export as MP3 (128kbps mono or 192kbps stereo)
10. [ ] Save project file (in case you need to re-export)

**Advanced Edit (additional steps):**

- [ ] EQ adjustment (cut low rumble below 80Hz, slight presence boost at 2-5kHz)
- [ ] Compression (even out volume differences between loud and soft parts)
- [ ] De-essing (reduce harsh "S" sounds)
- [ ] Match volume levels between host and guest
- [ ] Add chapter markers (for Apple Podcasts and Spotify)

## Growth & Promotion Strategy

### Launch Strategy (First 10 Episodes)

**Before Launch:**
- [ ] Record 3-5 episodes before publishing ANY (batch content = consistency)
- [ ] Create show artwork (1400x1400 minimum, 3000x3000 recommended)
- [ ] Write a compelling show description (include keywords, who it's for, what they'll get)
- [ ] Set up a simple website or landing page for the show
- [ ] Announce the upcoming launch to your existing audience (email, social)
- [ ] Ask 10 friends/supporters to listen and review on launch day

**Launch Day (Release 3 Episodes Simultaneously):**
- [ ] Publish 3 episodes at once (gives new listeners something to binge)
- [ ] Email your list: "My podcast is live!"
- [ ] Post on all social platforms
- [ ] Ask friends and family to listen, subscribe, and leave a review
- [ ] Cross-post in relevant communities (where allowed)

**First Month:**
- [ ] Release consistently (same day, same time, every week)
- [ ] Ask for reviews at the end of every episode
- [ ] Engage with every comment, review, and DM you receive
- [ ] Guest on other podcasts (cross-promotion is the #1 growth channel)
- [ ] Clip the best 30-60 second moments and share as audiograms/video clips
