# Weekly Meal Planning & Grocery System

> **Your Complete System for Planning Meals, Organizing Groceries, Tracking Your Pantry, and Managing Your Food Budget**
>
> How to use: This system is designed to work as an interconnected set of trackers. Start with the Recipe Database (Section 4) to build your meal library, then use the Weekly Meal Planner (Section 1) to plan each week. The Grocery List (Section 2) and Pantry Tracker (Section 3) work together to ensure you only buy what you need. Use the Budget Tracker (Section 6) weekly to stay on target.

## Setup Guide: Getting Started in 15 Minutes

### Step 1: Build Your Recipe Starter Kit (5 minutes)
Enter 10-15 of your most-made recipes into the Recipe Database (Section 4). You don't need to fill in every field — start with recipe name, ingredients, servings, and dietary tags. Add nutrition and cost details over time.

### Step 2: Inventory Your Kitchen (5 minutes)
Do a quick scan of your fridge, freezer, and pantry. Enter items into the Pantry Inventory Tracker (Section 3). Focus on proteins, produce, and anything with an expiration date. Mark anything expiring soon as "Use First."

### Step 3: Plan Your First Week (5 minutes)
Open the Weekly Meal Planner (Section 1). Start with dinners — pick 5-6 from your Recipe Database. Plan to eat leftovers 1-2 nights. Fill in lunches (leftovers or simple meals) and breakfasts (keep it to 2-3 rotating options). Generate your Grocery List (Section 2) by pulling ingredients from your planned recipes and cross-referencing your pantry.

### Step 4: Establish Your Routine
- **Sunday:** Plan the week + grocery shop (15-20 minutes planning, normal shopping time)
- **Sunday or Monday:** Meal prep session if batch cooking (1-3 hours)
- **Daily:** Check Leftover Tracker for items to use, take 30 seconds to note what you used
- **End of week:** Update Budget Tracker, review waste log, note improvements for next week
