## Pre-Launch Phase

### Week 4: Foundation (Days 28-22)

**Product Readiness:**
- [ ] Product is 90%+ complete (no launching half-finished products)
- [ ] Product has been tested by you personally (use your own product first)
- [ ] Price is decided (use the pricing framework below)
- [ ] Product name and tagline are finalized
- [ ] Product mockups/screenshots created
- [ ] File format tested and working (downloads, access, etc.)

**Strategic Decisions:**

| Decision | Your Answer |
|----------|------------|
| Launch date | [Pick a Tuesday-Thursday, avoid holidays] |
| Launch duration | [3 days / 5 days / 7 days] |
| Discount strategy | [Launch price / no discount / early bird / tiered] |
| Urgency mechanism | [Price increase / bonus expires / limited quantity / cart close] |
| Primary traffic source | [Email list / social media / paid ads / affiliates] |
| Success metric | [Revenue target: $___ / Unit target: ___] |

**Pricing Quick Framework:**

```
Step 1: What's the value of the outcome? (If someone implements this, what's it worth to them?)
        $_________

Step 2: What do alternatives cost? (Courses, consultants, competing products)
        Range: $_________ to $_________

Step 3: What's your audience's budget reality? (What can they actually afford?)
        Sweet spot: $_________

Step 4: Your price: 1/10th to 1/5th of the value, competitive with alternatives,
        within your audience's budget.
        YOUR PRICE: $_________
```

### Week 3: Audience Warming (Days 21-15)

**Goal:** Create anticipation without revealing everything.

**Day 21-19: Tease**
- [ ] Post #1: Hint something is coming ("I've been working on something for [AUDIENCE]...")
- [ ] Post #2: Share behind-the-scenes (process photo, screenshot, creation story)
- [ ] Post #3: Ask a question related to the problem your product solves

**Day 18-16: Reveal**
- [ ] Post #4: Share WHY you're building this (your motivation, the problem you saw)
- [ ] Post #5: Reveal one specific feature or section (give a taste of the quality)
- [ ] Post #6: Share a beta tester quote or early feedback

**Day 15: Announce**
- [ ] Public announcement: "Launching [PRODUCT] on [DATE]. Here's what it is..."
- [ ] Email your list: "Something exciting is coming on [DATE]"
- [ ] Optional: Open a waitlist or "notify me" page

**Content Calendar: Pre-Launch Social Media**

| Day | Post Type | Content Focus |
|-----|-----------|---------------|
| Day 21 | Mystery tease | Something's coming... |
| Day 19 | Behind the scenes | Show creation process |
| Day 17 | Problem post | Address the pain point |
| Day 15 | Reveal + date | Announce what and when |
| Day 13 | Feature preview | Show a specific component |
| Day 11 | Testimonial/feedback | Early reactions |
| Day 9 | "Who it's for" | Describe ideal buyer |
| Day 7 | Countdown begins | 1 week! + full details |
| Day 5 | Objection buster | Address top concern |
| Day 3 | Value breakdown | What's inside (full list) |
| Day 1 | Final countdown | Tomorrow! Last details |

### Week 2: Systems & Content Prep (Days 14-8)

**Sales Infrastructure:**
- [ ] Landing page / sales page written and live (can be hidden/unlisted)
- [ ] Payment processing set up and tested (make a test purchase)
- [ ] Product delivery mechanism confirmed (instant download, access email, etc.)
- [ ] Purchase confirmation email drafted and loaded
- [ ] Analytics/tracking in place (UTM links, conversion tracking)

**Launch Content Prepared:**
- [ ] Launch email sequence drafted (all emails: announcement through close)
- [ ] Social media posts written for each day of launch
- [ ] FAQ document prepared (anticipate 5-8 questions)
- [ ] Testimonials collected and formatted for use
- [ ] Launch graphics created (product mockups, announcement images, story templates)

**Pre-Launch Email Content:**

| Email | Send Date | Purpose | Subject Line |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Teaser | Day -7 | Build anticipation | "I've been working on something..." |
| Reveal | Day -5 | Explain what's coming | "[PRODUCT NAME]: launching [DATE]" |
| Waitlist/Save date | Day -3 | Encourage early interest | "Save the date: [DATE] at [TIME]" |
| Eve-of-launch | Day -1 | Final reminder | "Tomorrow is the day" |

---

### Week 1: Final Prep (Days 7-1)

**Day 7: Full Dry Run**
- [ ] Go through entire purchase flow as a customer
- [ ] Test on mobile and desktop
- [ ] Verify: payment works, product delivers, confirmation sends
- [ ] Fix any issues immediately

**Day 6: Proofread Everything**
- [ ] Landing page: zero typos, all links work, images load
- [ ] Email sequence: all links correct, personalization works, preview text set
- [ ] Social posts: no errors, hashtags correct, links functional
- [ ] Product itself: final check for errors, formatting, completeness

**Day 5: Schedule & Automate**
- [ ] All launch emails scheduled in your email platform
- [ ] Social media posts scheduled (or drafted and ready to post)
- [ ] Automated sequences activated (welcome, onboarding, abandoned cart if applicable)

**Day 4: Community & Support Prep**
- [ ] Identify 10-20 people to personally notify on launch day (supporters, friends, past buyers)
- [ ] Prepare a personal message template for DMs
- [ ] Set up a system for tracking questions and feedback during launch

**Day 3: Personal Prep**
- [ ] Clear your launch day calendar (no meetings, no distractions)
- [ ] Prepare your workspace (tabs organized, files accessible, energy drinks stocked)
- [ ] Do a confidence check: re-read testimonials, remind yourself why this is valuable

**Day 2: Final Checks**
- [ ] One more test purchase (from a different device if possible)
- [ ] Confirm all scheduled emails show correct content
- [ ] Re-read your launch day operations checklist (below)
- [ ] Set your alarm for launch morning

**Day 1: Launch Eve**
- [ ] Everything is ready. No more changes.
- [ ] Get a good night's sleep.
- [ ] Tomorrow, you execute.

## Launch Day Operations

### Launch Day Hour-by-Hour

**6:00 AM — Early Prep**
- [ ] Wake up, eat breakfast, hydrate
- [ ] Quick review of today's plan (this checklist)
- [ ] Open all relevant tabs: email platform, sales platform, social media, analytics

**7:00 AM — Final Checks**
- [ ] Confirm landing page is live and accessible
- [ ] Confirm first email is scheduled or ready to send
- [ ] Confirm social posts are ready
- [ ] Take a deep breath

**8:00 AM — GO LIVE** (adjust to your timezone and audience)
- [ ] Make product publicly available (remove any "hidden" settings)
- [ ] Send launch email #1 (Announcement)
- [ ] Post launch announcement on primary social platform
- [ ] Update social media bio with product link
- [ ] Send personal DMs to your 10-20 supporters ("It's live! Would love your support")

**9:00 AM — First Hour Check**
- [ ] Monitor for technical issues (payment errors, delivery problems)
- [ ] Respond to any immediate questions or DMs
- [ ] Check: did email send properly? Open rate looking normal?
- [ ] Record first metrics: page views, sales, shares

**10:00 AM - 12:00 PM — Engage**
- [ ] Post second social media update (what's inside / breakdown)
- [ ] Respond to every comment, question, and DM personally
- [ ] Share in relevant communities (with permission/following rules)
- [ ] Engage with anyone who shares or recommends your product

**12:00 PM — Midday Check-In**
- [ ] Record metrics: total page views, sales, revenue, email stats
- [ ] Assess: Is anything not working? Any common questions coming up?
- [ ] Post a personal/story-based social update (why you built this)
- [ ] Thank early buyers publicly (if appropriate)

**3:00 PM — Afternoon Push**
- [ ] Post social proof: share any feedback, messages, or results from early buyers
- [ ] Optional: share in 1-2 additional communities or platforms
- [ ] Respond to all new messages and comments
- [ ] Consider: Is there a question multiple people are asking? Address it publicly.

**6:00 PM — Evening Wrap**
- [ ] Post final social update of the day (vulnerable/grateful/casual)
- [ ] Record end-of-day metrics
- [ ] Send personal thank-yous to anyone who shared or bought
- [ ] Confirm tomorrow's email and posts are scheduled

**9:00 PM — Debrief**
- [ ] Write quick notes: What went well? What surprised you? Issues?
- [ ] Celebrate. You launched. Regardless of numbers, you did the thing.
- [ ] Rest. Tomorrow continues the momentum.

---

### Launch Day Troubleshooting

| Problem | Quick Fix |
|---------|-----------|
| Payment not working | Check Stripe/PayPal dashboard, test from incognito browser, contact support |
| Email didn't send | Check scheduled time, spam folders, resend manually if needed |
| Low open rate | Send a follow-up with different subject line to unopeners (wait 4+ hours) |
| Product not delivering | Check automation triggers, manually send to affected buyers + apologize |
| Negative feedback | Respond graciously, fix if valid, don't engage publicly with trolls |
| No sales yet | Don't panic. Most sales come from email, not social. Give it time. |
| Site/page is down | Contact hosting provider, share alternate purchase link, stay calm |

## Post-Launch Phase

### Days 1-3: Momentum

**Day +1:**
- [ ] Send Email #2 (Story behind the product / personal angle)
- [ ] Post 2 social updates (morning: what's inside breakdown; evening: early feedback)
- [ ] Continue responding to all questions and comments
- [ ] Record daily metrics
- [ ] Collect any testimonials or feedback for use in later emails

**Day +2:**
- [ ] Send Email #3 (Social proof / testimonials)
- [ ] Post social proof content (screenshots of nice messages, results)
- [ ] Reach out to buyers for quick testimonial quotes
- [ ] Address any common questions in a public FAQ post

**Day +3:**
- [ ] Send Email #4 (FAQ / Objection handling)
- [ ] Share a free preview or sample from the product (build desire)
- [ ] Consider: Is there a specific audience segment that hasn't been addressed?

---

### Days 4-5: Urgency Phase

**Day +4:**
- [ ] Send Email #5 (Urgency — price increasing / bonus expiring / limited spots)
- [ ] Post countdown content on social media
- [ ] Share final testimonial or success story
- [ ] Personal outreach to anyone who expressed interest but didn't buy

**Day +5 (or Final Day):**
- [ ] Send Email #6 AM (Last chance morning reminder)
- [ ] Send Email #7 PM (Final hours — evening)
- [ ] Post 2-3 social updates throughout the day (countdown)
- [ ] Be available and responsive all day

**Midnight: Launch Closes**
- [ ] Remove discount / change price / close cart (FOLLOW THROUGH — if you said it closes, close it)
- [ ] Record final launch metrics

---

### Days 6-14: Post-Launch Optimization

**Day +6: Thank You & Reset**
- [ ] Send "Thank you" email to buyers (onboarding, how to get started)
- [ ] Send "The launch is over" email to non-buyers (graceful, no guilt)
- [ ] Social post: gratitude, results summary, what's next
- [ ] Begin launch debrief (template below)

**Days 7-10: Buyer Care**
- [ ] Send check-in email to buyers (Day 7): "How's it going? Need help?"
- [ ] Monitor for support requests and respond quickly
- [ ] Collect testimonials from buyers who've had time to use the product
- [ ] Fix any issues or answer common questions with a FAQ update

**Days 11-14: Optimize & Plan**
- [ ] Complete full launch debrief (below)
- [ ] Identify what to improve for next launch
- [ ] Plan evergreen strategy: Will this product sell ongoing? If so, set up evergreen funnel.
- [ ] Request testimonials from remaining buyers
- [ ] Update landing page with new testimonials and results
