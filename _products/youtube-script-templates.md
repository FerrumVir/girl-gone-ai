# YouTube Script Writing Templates — 8 Proven Formats with Hook Formulas

---

## How to Use These Templates

This kit contains 8 complete YouTube script templates, each designed for a specific video type. Every template includes: a hook formula, full script structure, transition phrases, and CTA placement guide.

**The 8 templates:**

1. Tutorial / How-To
2. Product Review
3. Listicle / Top 10
4. Story-Based / Personal
5. Vlog / Day-in-the-Life
6. Interview / Conversation
7. Comparison / Versus
8. Explainer / Educational

**Script formatting key:**

- `[HOOK]` — first 5-10 seconds, must stop the scroll
- `[B-ROLL]` — visual overlay notes
- `[CUT TO]` — transition direction
- `[GFX]` — on-screen text or graphics
- `[CTA]` — call to action moment
- `(NOTE)` — production notes, not spoken

---

## Hook Formula Bank (Use with Any Template)

### The 30 Best YouTube Hooks

**Curiosity Hooks:**
1. "This one thing changed everything about how I [ACTIVITY]..."
2. "Nobody talks about this, but [SURPRISING FACT]."
3. "I spent [TIME] testing [THING] so you don't have to."
4. "The secret to [OUTCOME] isn't what you think."
5. "After [X] years of [ACTIVITY], here's what actually works."

**Result/Proof Hooks:**
6. "I went from [BEFORE STATE] to [AFTER STATE] in [TIMEFRAME]. Here's exactly how."
7. "[NUMBER] [PEOPLE/COMPANIES] use this method to [OUTCOME]. Today I'm breaking it down."
8. "This [STRATEGY/TOOL] made me [SPECIFIC RESULT]. Let me show you."
9. "I tested [X] different [THINGS] and these [NUMBER] were the clear winners."
10. "In the next [X] minutes, you'll know exactly how to [OUTCOME]."

**Problem/Pain Hooks:**
11. "If you're struggling with [PROBLEM], stop. You're probably making this mistake."
12. "Most people get [TOPIC] completely wrong. Here's why."
13. "I wasted [TIME/MONEY] on [WRONG APPROACH] before I discovered this."
14. "[TOPIC] is broken. Here's how to fix it."
15. "Stop doing [COMMON THING]. It's costing you [WHAT THEY'RE LOSING]."

**Challenge/Controversy Hooks:**
16. "I'm going to say something controversial about [TOPIC]..."
17. "Everyone's doing [COMMON APPROACH]. Here's why I do the opposite."
18. "I challenged myself to [CHALLENGE] and the results surprised me."
19. "[POPULAR OPINION] is wrong. Let me explain."
20. "I tried the most [EXTREME/POPULAR] [THING] and here's my honest take."

**Pattern Interrupt Hooks:**
21. "[SURPRISING STATEMENT THAT CONTRADICTS EXPECTATIONS]."
22. "Okay, watch this." (immediately show the result/demo)
23. "Before you [COMMON ACTION], watch this video."
24. "This is the video I wish existed when I started [ACTIVITY]."
25. "I'm about to save you [TIME/MONEY]. Pay attention."

**Story Hooks:**
26. "Three months ago, I was [NEGATIVE SITUATION]. Today, [POSITIVE SITUATION]."
27. "The moment I realized [REALIZATION], everything changed."
28. "Let me tell you about the worst [MISTAKE/EXPERIENCE] I ever had with [TOPIC]..."
29. "Last week, something happened that completely changed my perspective on [TOPIC]."
30. "I was about to [GIVE UP / QUIT / FAIL] when [TURNING POINT]."

## Template 1: Tutorial / How-To

**Best for:** Teaching a specific skill, process, or technique
**Ideal length:** 8-15 minutes
**Retention strategy:** Promise a clear outcome, deliver steps, show results

### Script Structure

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:15]
"In the next [X] minutes, I'm going to show you exactly how to [SPECIFIC OUTCOME].
By the end of this video, you'll be able to [TANGIBLE RESULT] even if you're [STARTING POINT/LIMITATION].
Let's get into it."

[GFX: Title card with video topic]
(NOTE: Show a quick flash of the end result here — 2 seconds max — to prove it's worth watching)

---

[CONTEXT — 0:15-1:00]
"Before we dive in, let me quickly explain [WHY THIS MATTERS / WHO THIS IS FOR / WHAT YOU NEED].

You'll need:
- [TOOL/REQUIREMENT 1]
- [TOOL/REQUIREMENT 2]
- [TOOL/REQUIREMENT 3]

[GFX: List on screen]

If you've got those, you're ready. Let's start with step one."

---

[STEP 1 — 1:00-3:00]
"Step one: [ACTION VERB + WHAT TO DO]."

[B-ROLL: Screen recording or demonstration of step 1]

"Here's what this looks like in practice..."

(Walk through the step with specific details. Show, don't just tell.)

"The reason this step matters is [WHY]. A common mistake here is [MISTAKE] —
instead, make sure you [CORRECT APPROACH]."

[GFX: "Step 1: [Summary]" on screen]

---

[STEP 2 — 3:00-5:00]
"Now that you've [COMPLETED STEP 1], we can move to step two: [ACTION]."

[B-ROLL: Demonstration]

"This is where most people get tripped up. The key is [KEY INSIGHT]."

(Show the process. Pause at tricky moments to explain.)

"Pro tip: [EXPERT SHORTCUT OR INSIGHT THAT SAVES TIME]."

[GFX: "Step 2: [Summary]" on screen]

---

[STEP 3 — 5:00-7:00]
"Step three is [ACTION]. This is where it starts to come together."

[B-ROLL: Demonstration]

(Continue detailed walkthrough. Reference how this connects to previous steps.)

"If you're following along and yours looks like [DESCRIBE], you're on the right track."

[GFX: "Step 3: [Summary]" on screen]

---

[STEP 4 — 7:00-9:00]
"Almost there. Step four: [ACTION]."

[B-ROLL: Demonstration]

(Walk through final technical steps.)

"And just like that — [SHOW THE COMPLETED RESULT]."

[GFX: "Step 4: [Summary]" on screen]

---

[RESULTS + RECAP — 9:00-10:30]
"Here's the final result: [SHOW FINISHED PRODUCT/OUTCOME]."

[B-ROLL: Show result from multiple angles or in context]

"Quick recap of what we covered:
1. [Step 1 summary in one sentence]
2. [Step 2 summary]
3. [Step 3 summary]
4. [Step 4 summary]"

[GFX: All steps listed]

---

[CTA — 10:30-11:00]
"If this was helpful, drop a comment below and let me know [SPECIFIC QUESTION — e.g., 'what topic should I cover next?'].

If you want to go deeper on [RELATED TOPIC], I've got a [PRODUCT/VIDEO/RESOURCE] linked in the description.

Subscribe if you want more [CONTENT TYPE] like this — I post every [SCHEDULE]. See you in the next one."

[END SCREEN: Subscribe button + recommended video]
```

## Template 2: Product Review

**Best for:** Reviewing tools, software, products, courses, or services
**Ideal length:** 8-12 minutes
**Retention strategy:** Give the verdict tease early, then back it up with details

### Script Structure

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:20]
"I've been using [PRODUCT] for [TIME PERIOD] and I have thoughts.
Is it worth [PRICE]? Short answer: [YES/NO/IT DEPENDS].
But stick around because there are [NUMBER] things you need to know before you buy.
Let me break it down."

(NOTE: Giving a quick verdict early keeps people watching to understand WHY)

[GFX: Product name + your rating teaser: "X/10?"]

---

[OVERVIEW — 0:20-1:30]
"For anyone who doesn't know, [PRODUCT] is [BRIEF DESCRIPTION: what it is, who makes it, what it does].
It costs [PRICE] and it's designed for [TARGET USER]."

[B-ROLL: Product footage, website, or box/unboxing]

"I bought this with my own money [OR: this was sent to me — full disclosure]
and I've been using it [DAILY/WEEKLY] for [TIME] to [SPECIFIC USE CASE]."

"Here's how I'm going to structure this review:
- First, what I love
- Then, what I don't love
- And finally, who should buy this and who should skip it."

---

[WHAT I LOVE — 1:30-5:00]
"Let's start with the good stuff. [NUMBER] things I genuinely love about [PRODUCT]."

"Number one: [FEATURE/BENEFIT]."
[B-ROLL: Show this feature in action]
"What this means in practice is [HOW IT HELPS IN REAL LIFE].
For example, [SPECIFIC EXAMPLE FROM YOUR EXPERIENCE]."

"Number two: [FEATURE/BENEFIT]."
[B-ROLL: Demonstration]
"This one surprised me. I didn't expect [UNEXPECTED POSITIVE].
Compared to [ALTERNATIVE], this is noticeably better because [REASON]."

"Number three: [FEATURE/BENEFIT]."
[B-ROLL: Demonstration]
"[PERSONAL ANECDOTE OR SPECIFIC RESULT showing this feature in action]."

---

[MID-ROLL CTA — 5:00-5:15]
"Quick note — if you do end up buying [PRODUCT],
I've got a link in the description [with a discount / that supports the channel].
No pressure, but it's there if you want it."

---

[WHAT I DON'T LOVE — 5:15-8:00]
"Now let's talk about the downsides. Because nothing's perfect."

"Issue one: [PROBLEM/LIMITATION]."
[B-ROLL: Show the issue]
"In practice, this means [HOW IT AFFECTS THE USER].
For some people this is a dealbreaker, for others it won't matter."

"Issue two: [PROBLEM/LIMITATION]."
[B-ROLL: Show the issue]
"I reached out to [COMPANY] about this and [THEIR RESPONSE / NO RESPONSE / IT'S BEING FIXED]."

"Issue three: [PROBLEM/LIMITATION]."
"This is minor, but worth mentioning: [EXPLAIN]."

---

[COMPARISON — 8:00-9:30]
"Now, how does this compare to the alternatives?"

[GFX: Comparison table on screen]

"Versus [COMPETITOR A]: [PRODUCT] wins on [X] but loses on [Y].
Versus [COMPETITOR B]: [PRODUCT] is better if you need [SPECIFIC THING],
but if you need [OTHER THING], go with [COMPETITOR B].
Versus doing nothing/the free option: [HONEST ASSESSMENT]."

---

[WHO SHOULD BUY — 9:30-10:30]
"So who is this actually for?"

"Buy [PRODUCT] if:
- You're [SITUATION 1]
- You need [SPECIFIC CAPABILITY]
- You value [PRIORITY] over [OTHER PRIORITY]"

"Skip [PRODUCT] if:
- You're [SITUATION WHERE IT DOESN'T FIT]
- You need [SOMETHING IT DOESN'T DO]
- Your budget is under [THRESHOLD]"

---

[FINAL VERDICT — 10:30-11:30]
"Final verdict: I'm giving [PRODUCT] a [X] out of 10."

[GFX: Rating on screen]

"It's [SUMMARY SENTENCE: excellent/good/decent/not worth it] for [SPECIFIC USE CASE].
After [TIME PERIOD] of daily use, I [AM/AM NOT] keeping it in my workflow."

"Would I recommend it? [HONEST ANSWER WITH CAVEAT]."

---

[CTA — 11:30-12:00]
"If you found this helpful, hit subscribe — I review [CATEGORY] tools every [FREQUENCY].
Link to [PRODUCT] is in the description.
Drop a comment if you have questions — I'll answer as many as I can.
See you in the next one."
```

## Template 3: Listicle / Top 10

**Best for:** Rankings, collections, recommendations, tips compilations
**Ideal length:** 10-15 minutes
**Retention strategy:** Tease #1, build anticipation, reveal progressively better items

### Script Structure

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:15]
"[NUMBER] [THINGS] that [PROMISE/OUTCOME].
Number [LAST] literally [DRAMATIC CLAIM].
Stick around to the end — these get progressively better."

(NOTE: Teasing the best item at the end keeps watch time high)

---

[INTRO — 0:15-0:45]
"I spent [TIME/EFFORT] [TESTING/RESEARCHING/COMPILING] these
so you don't have to. Quick ground rules:"

[GFX: Criteria on screen]

"I ranked these based on [CRITERIA 1], [CRITERIA 2], and [CRITERIA 3].
No sponsorships influenced this list — these are my genuine picks. Let's go."

---

[ITEM #10 — 0:45-2:00]
"Number 10: [ITEM NAME]."

[GFX: "#10 - [NAME]" on screen]
[B-ROLL: Show the item]

"[WHAT IT IS in one sentence].
[WHY IT'S ON THE LIST in 2-3 sentences].
[ONE SPECIFIC EXAMPLE OR USE CASE].
[ONE CAVEAT OR LIMITATION]."

"Good, but not the best. Let's keep going."

---

[ITEM #9 — 2:00-3:00]
"Number 9: [ITEM NAME]."

[GFX: "#9 - [NAME]"]
[B-ROLL: Show it]

"[Same structure: What, Why, Example, Caveat]"

(TRANSITION: "Next up is one that surprised me...")

---

[ITEMS #8-6 — 3:00-7:00]
(Continue same structure for each. Vary your transitions:)
- "This next one is underrated..."
- "Number [X] might be controversial..."
- "Now we're getting into the really good ones..."
- "I almost didn't include this one, but..."

(NOTE: Pacing tip — spend 45-90 seconds per item in the lower half, 90-120 in the upper half)

---

[MID-ROLL CTA — 7:00-7:15]
"Before we get to the top 5 — if you're enjoying this,
hit that like button. It genuinely helps the channel.
Okay, top 5. These are the elite."

---

[ITEMS #5-2 — 7:15-12:00]
(Spend more time on these. Add more detail, personal stories, demonstrations.)

"Number 5: [ITEM]."
"Now we're in serious territory. [EXPANDED BREAKDOWN — why this is significantly better than items 6-10]."

"Number 4: [ITEM]."
"This is where things get personal. [SHARE YOUR SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE]."

"Number 3: [ITEM]."
"If you take one thing from this video... actually wait, we're not at number 1 yet. But seriously, [STRONG ENDORSEMENT]."

"Number 2: [ITEM]."
"So close to the top. And honestly, on a different day this could be number 1. [EXPLAIN WHY IT'S EXCEPTIONAL]."

---

[ITEM #1 — 12:00-14:00]
"And number 1... the best [CATEGORY] is..."

(PAUSE — 2 seconds of silence for dramatic effect)

[GFX: "#1 - [NAME]" with special animation/emphasis]

"[ITEM NAME]. And it's not even close. Here's why:"

"[REASON 1 — specific and detailed]"
"[REASON 2 — with personal evidence]"
"[REASON 3 — what separates it from everything else]"

"If you only get one [THING] from this list, make it this one."

---

[RECAP + CTA — 14:00-15:00]
"Quick recap — full list on screen:"

[GFX: All 10 items listed]

"Disagree with my ranking? Tell me in the comments — I want to hear your top pick.

If you want my [RELATED RESOURCE/GUIDE/PRODUCT], it's linked in the description.

Subscribe for more [CONTENT TYPE]. I'll see you in the next one."
```

## Template 4: Story-Based / Personal

**Best for:** Sharing experiences, lessons learned, transformations, vulnerable moments
**Ideal length:** 8-12 minutes
**Retention strategy:** Open with the emotional peak, then rewind to tell the full story

### Script Structure

```
[HOOK — THE PEAK MOMENT — 0:00-0:30]
"[Describe the climactic moment of your story in vivid present tense.
Put the viewer INTO the scene.]"

"I'm standing in [LOCATION]. My [EMOTION] is [PHYSICAL SENSATION].
And I realize: [THE REALIZATION/TURNING POINT]."

"But to understand how I got here, I need to take you back to [TIME PERIOD]."

(NOTE: Start at the emotional peak, then rewind. This is the In Medias Res technique.)

---

[THE BEFORE — 0:30-2:30]
"[TIME PERIOD AGO], I was [DESCRIBE YOUR SITUATION]."

[B-ROLL: Photos, footage, or visuals of this period]

"I was doing [WHAT YOU WERE DOING WRONG/BEFORE].
And I genuinely believed [FALSE BELIEF YOU HELD]."

"The thing nobody tells you about [SITUATION] is [HONEST INSIGHT]."

(Paint the "before" picture vividly. Make it relatable. Be specific — specific is universal.)

"And then [INCITING INCIDENT] happened."

---

[THE TRIGGER — 2:30-4:00]
"It was [DATE/TIME/CONTEXT]. I was [DOING WHAT].
And [THE THING THAT CHANGED EVERYTHING] happened."

[B-ROLL: Relevant visuals]

"In that moment, I felt [EMOTION]. And I knew [WHAT YOU KNEW]."

"Most people, when [THIS HAPPENS], they [COMMON RESPONSE].
I almost did that too. But instead, I decided to [YOUR CHOICE]."

---

[THE JOURNEY — 4:00-7:00]
"What followed was [TIME PERIOD] of [DESCRIBE THE PROCESS]."

"The first thing I did was [ACTION 1]. And it [WORKED/DIDN'T WORK] because [REASON]."

"Then I tried [ACTION 2]. This is where I learned [LESSON]."

[B-ROLL: Show the process, the work, the struggle]

"The hardest part was [HONEST DIFFICULTY].
There were days when I wanted to [QUIT/GIVE UP/GO BACK].
But I kept going because [YOUR WHY]."

"The breakthrough came when [SPECIFIC MOMENT OF PROGRESS]."

---

[THE RESULT — 7:00-9:00]
"Fast forward to today."

[CUT TO: Present day / the result]

"I [DESCRIBE YOUR CURRENT SITUATION — the transformation complete].
I went from [BEFORE] to [AFTER]. And it took [TIMELINE]."

"But here's what matters more than the result: [THE DEEPER LESSON]."

"Because the truth is, [UNIVERSAL TRUTH that connects your story to the viewer's life]."

---

[THE LESSON — 9:00-10:30]
"If you're watching this and you're in the [BEFORE] stage right now, here's what I want you to know:"

"[LESSON 1 — practical]. Do this."
"[LESSON 2 — mindset]. Believe this."
"[LESSON 3 — encouragement]. You're closer than you think."

"The version of me who was [BEFORE STATE] could never have imagined
being where I am now. And the version of you watching this?
You're capable of [THEIR POTENTIAL]."

---

[CTA — 10:30-11:00]
"If this story resonated with you, share yours in the comments.
I read every single one."

"If you want to [RELATED ACTION], I have a [RESOURCE] in the description
that will help you [SPECIFIC OUTCOME]."

"Subscribe if you want more [HONEST/REAL/PERSONAL] content like this.
I appreciate you watching until the end. See you next time."
```

## Template 5: Vlog / Day-in-the-Life

**Best for:** Behind-the-scenes, daily routine, lifestyle content, building personal brand
**Ideal length:** 10-15 minutes
**Retention strategy:** Promise a narrative arc within the day (challenge, project, or event)

### Script Structure

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:20]
"Today I'm [DOING THING / ATTEMPTING CHALLENGE / SHOWING YOU SOMETHING].
This is going to be [INTERESTING BECAUSE...]. Let's see how it goes."

(NOTE: Every good vlog has a PREMISE — what makes today different from every other day?
Even a "day in my life" needs a hook: a deadline, a challenge, a first-time experience.)

---

[MORNING — 0:20-3:00]
[B-ROLL: Morning routine footage — alarm, coffee, workspace setup]

"[TIME] AM. [Describe the morning routine briefly. Keep it moving.]"

(VOICEOVER or TO-CAMERA:)
"Today's plan is [OUTLINE THE DAY]. The big thing is [MAIN EVENT/TASK].
But first, [MORNING ROUTINE ELEMENT that's relevant or interesting]."

[SHOW: 2-3 morning moments. Don't show everything — just the interesting parts.]

"Alright, [TRANSITION TO MAIN ACTIVITY]. Let's go."

---

[MID-MORNING — 3:00-5:30]
[B-ROLL: Working, creating, traveling to location]

"So right now I'm [ACTIVITY]. [Quick context for viewers who need it.]"

(TO-CAMERA:)
"The thing about [WHAT YOU'RE DOING] that people don't realize is [INSIGHT].
It looks [EASY/GLAMOROUS/SIMPLE] but really it's [HONEST TRUTH]."

[SHOW: The work. The process. The behind-the-scenes reality.]

(If something goes wrong — include it. Real moments > polished moments in vlogs.)

"Okay, [SOMETHING UNEXPECTED HAPPENED]. [React naturally.]"

---

[MIDDAY — 5:30-8:00]
[B-ROLL: Transition, food, change of scenery]

"It's [TIME] now. [Update on progress/status of the day's main event.]"

(TO-CAMERA — this is a good spot for a reflective moment:)
"Something I've been thinking about lately is [THOUGHT/OBSERVATION].
[Share 30-60 seconds of genuine reflection. This is where connection happens.]"

[SHOW: Continue documenting the day. Mix of B-roll and to-camera.]

---

[THE MAIN EVENT — 8:00-11:00]
"Okay, this is the moment. [THE MAIN THING THAT MAKES TODAY'S VLOG WORTH WATCHING]."

[DOCUMENT: The experience, event, challenge, or accomplishment in detail.]

(Show reactions. Show process. Show results. Let the viewer experience it with you.)

"[REACTION TO HOW IT WENT]. I honestly [DIDN'T EXPECT / WAS HOPING FOR / WAS NERVOUS ABOUT] [ASPECT]."

---

[WIND-DOWN — 11:00-13:00]
[B-ROLL: Evening, heading home, cooking, relaxing]

"It's [TIME] now. [How the day is wrapping up.]"

(TO-CAMERA — evening reflection:)
"Today was [ADJECTIVE]. The highlight was definitely [MOMENT].
If I'm honest, [SOMETHING VULNERABLE OR REAL ABOUT THE DAY]."

[SHOW: 1-2 evening moments that feel complete/satisfying.]

---

[CLOSING — 13:00-14:00]
(TO-CAMERA — final thought:)
"I think the thing I want you to take away from today is [ONE TAKEAWAY].
Whether that's [PRACTICAL LESSON] or just [MOTIVATIONAL/LIFESTYLE INSIGHT]."

"Tomorrow I'm [TEASE NEXT VIDEO/NEXT DAY].
If you want to come along, subscribe and turn on notifications."

"Thanks for hanging out today. [SIGN-OFF CATCHPHRASE]. See you tomorrow."

[END SCREEN]
```

## Template 6: Interview / Conversation

**Best for:** Expert interviews, podcast-style content, guest features
**Ideal length:** 15-30 minutes (or clipped into shorter segments)
**Retention strategy:** Promise specific insights, use chapter markers, front-load the best moments

### Script Structure

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:30]
(Option A — Result Tease:)
"Today I'm sitting down with [GUEST NAME], who [IMPRESSIVE CREDENTIAL/RESULT].
I asked them the one question everyone wants to know: [COMPELLING QUESTION].
Their answer changed how I think about [TOPIC]."

(Option B — Clip Tease:)
[PLAY: 10-15 second clip of the most compelling/surprising moment from the interview]
"That's [GUEST NAME]. Full conversation coming up."

---

[INTRODUCTION — 0:30-2:00]
"[GUEST NAME] is [TITLE/ROLE]. They're known for [ACHIEVEMENT 1] and [ACHIEVEMENT 2].
I invited them on because [SPECIFIC REASON THIS CONVERSATION MATTERS TO YOUR AUDIENCE]."

(TO GUEST:)
"[GUEST], welcome. For anyone who doesn't know you yet — give us the 60-second version.
Who are you and what do you do?"

[GUEST INTRODUCTION — let them speak. Don't interrupt. React genuinely.]

---

[SECTION 1: ORIGIN/BACKSTORY — 2:00-7:00]
"I want to start at the beginning. [SPECIFIC ORIGIN QUESTION]."

Suggested questions:
- "What were you doing before [CURRENT THING]? How did you get here?"
- "Was there a specific moment when you knew [THIS PATH] was it?"
- "What's something most people don't know about your early days?"
- "What's the biggest mistake you made getting started?"

(NOTE: Let answers breathe. Pause after they finish — they often add the best part after a silence.)

---

[SECTION 2: THE MEAT — 7:00-18:00]
"Let's get tactical. [AUDIENCE] watching this wants to know: [SPECIFIC PRACTICAL QUESTION]."

Suggested questions (choose 4-6):
- "What's your [DAILY ROUTINE/PROCESS/FRAMEWORK] for [THING THEY'RE KNOWN FOR]?"
- "If you were starting from zero today, what would you do differently?"
- "What's the most common mistake you see [AUDIENCE] making?"
- "What's one thing you believe about [TOPIC] that most people disagree with?"
- "Can you walk us through a specific example of [THEIR METHOD] in action?"
- "What tools/resources do you use every day?"
- "What's the 80/20 of [THEIR EXPERTISE]? What gives the biggest results with least effort?"

(Follow-up after each answer:)
- "Can you give a specific example?"
- "What does that look like in practice?"
- "How would someone watching this start doing that tomorrow?"

---

[SECTION 3: RAPID FIRE / LIGHTNING ROUND — 18:00-22:00]
"Alright, let's do a lightning round. Short answers."

- "Best book on [TOPIC]?"
- "Most overrated [THING IN YOUR INDUSTRY]?"
- "Most underrated?"
- "One tool you can't live without?"
- "Best piece of advice you've ever received?"
- "What would you tell your [AGE]-year-old self?"
- "What's next for you?"

---

[CLOSING — 22:00-24:00]
"Last question: if someone watching this takes ONE action after this conversation, what should it be?"

[LET THEM ANSWER — this is usually gold]

"[GUEST], this was incredible. Where can people find you?"

[GUEST shares their links]

"I'll put all those links in the description. [GUEST], thank you."

---

[OUTRO — TO CAMERA — 24:00-25:00]
"My biggest takeaway from that conversation was [YOUR KEY INSIGHT].
I hope you got as much from that as I did."

"If you want more conversations like this, subscribe.
Drop a comment — who should I interview next?"

"See you in the next one."
```

## Template 7: Comparison / Versus

**Best for:** Comparing products, methods, strategies, tools, approaches
**Ideal length:** 10-15 minutes
**Retention strategy:** Promise a clear winner, build tension, deliver a verdict

### Script Structure

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:20]
"[OPTION A] vs. [OPTION B]. Which one is actually better?"

"I've [TESTED/USED/RESEARCHED] both extensively,
and by the end of this video, you'll know exactly which one to choose.
Spoiler: the answer might surprise you."

[GFX: "[A] vs [B]" title card]

---

[OVERVIEW — 0:20-2:00]
"Quick context. [OPTION A] is [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. [OPTION B] is [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]."

[B-ROLL: Show both options]

"I'm going to compare them on [NUMBER] criteria:
1. [CRITERION 1]
2. [CRITERION 2]
3. [CRITERION 3]
4. [CRITERION 4]
5. [CRITERION 5]

At the end, I'll give each a score and declare an overall winner. Let's go."

[GFX: Criteria list + scoring system]

---

[CRITERION 1 — 2:00-4:00]
"First up: [CRITERION 1]."

[GFX: "[CRITERION 1]" header]

"[OPTION A]: [detailed assessment with specific examples, numbers, or demonstrations]."
[B-ROLL: Show Option A performing on this criterion]

"[OPTION B]: [detailed assessment]."
[B-ROLL: Show Option B]

"Winner for [CRITERION 1]: [WINNER]."
```
