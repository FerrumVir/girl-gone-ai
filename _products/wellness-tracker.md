# SECTION 6: SELF-CARE MENU

> When you need to fill up your cup, pick from the menu based on how much time you have. No overthinking.

---

## 5-Minute Self-Care
- Drink a full glass of water slowly
- Step outside and breathe fresh air
- Do 5 deep breaths (4-count in, hold 4, out 4)
- Stretch your neck and shoulders
- Splash cold water on your face
- Put on a song that lifts your mood
- Write 3 things you're grateful for
- Look out a window at something natural
- Text someone you care about
- Close your eyes and do nothing for 5 minutes

## 15-Minute Self-Care
- Go for a short walk
- Do a guided meditation (app or YouTube)
- Journal — just write whatever's on your mind
- Light a candle, make tea, sit quietly
- Do gentle stretching or yoga
- Watch something that makes you laugh
- Take a hot shower
- Call a friend
- Read a few pages of something you enjoy
- Tidy one small area that's been bothering you

## 30-Minute Self-Care
- Exercise — any kind
- Cook a nourishing meal from scratch
- Take a bath
- Watch an episode of a comfort show
- Spend time in nature
- Creative activity — draw, paint, write, play music
- Read
- Nap (20–30 min, set an alarm)
- Have a real conversation with someone you love
- Do a hobby you've been neglecting

## 1-Hour Self-Care
- Full workout + shower
- Extended nature walk or hike
- Complete a creative project
- Cook and eat a slow, intentional meal
- Long bath + book
- Social time with people who energize you
- Massage or other bodywork
- Full day planning and reflection session
- Spend time doing something you loved as a kid
- Total screen-free hour

# APPENDIX: USING YOUR DATA

**The goal of tracking isn't the tracking — it's the patterns.**

After 30 days of consistent logging, look for:
- Your best sleep window (what time you fall asleep vs. how you feel)
- Foods that improve or tank your energy
- Exercise types that boost mood vs. those that feel like a chore
- Stress patterns by day of week or life context
- The relationship between your hydration and your focus

Use the Monthly Wellness Review to surface these patterns. The data means nothing if you don't act on what it shows you.

**Start small.** Log what you can. Even partial data is useful data. One perfect entry per week beats seven abandoned ones.
